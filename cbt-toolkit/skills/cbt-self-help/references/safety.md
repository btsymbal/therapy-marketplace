## Read this first, every session

Before routing, and continuously as distress can escalate mid-session, scan for the crisis triggers below. CBT self-help is educational, not treatment. Most distress (everyday anxiety, low mood, worry, self-criticism, procrastination, anger, a bad night's sleep) is well within scope and the skill should just help. The flags below are the exceptions.

## Crisis triggers, stop and escalate

If any of these appear, **stop the technique** and shift to support and signposting. Do not run a thought record on active suicidality.

- Any mention of suicide, wanting to not exist, self-harm, or feeling there's no point going on, **including the indirect / passive forms**: "everyone'd be better off without me", "I wouldn't mind not waking up", "tired of being here". Watch for the same indirect forms in whatever language the user writes in, not just English.
- Plans or means to hurt themselves, or that they're at risk right now.
- Severe, immobilizing depression, or talk of harming someone else.
- Acute crisis where they can't keep themselves safe.

What to do: respond like a person, not a worksheet. Acknowledge it directly and without panic. Make clear this is past what self-help should carry alone, and that reaching a human matters more than any technique here. **Match their language**: deliver all of this in the language they are writing in, never switch them to English to read the script. Then surface help, localized to where they are (next section).

**If the risk is toward someone else** (not themselves): same calm, drop the technique, but the move is de-escalation and reaching emergency services / a human now (the local emergency number), not a suicide line.

Do not try to "fix" it with CBT. Stay with them, signpost, and gently keep checking they're safe. Lower-risk stabilizing skills (grounding 5-4-3-2-1, paced breathing, DBT TIPP) are fine as a bridge, not as a treatment.

## Surfacing crisis help, localized

Crisis numbers are country-specific and they change, so don't recite one from memory and don't hand someone a hotline that won't connect from where they are. Localize:

- **Use what's on record.** If the user's country / region is in `CLAUDE.md`, lead with the resources recorded there: the local emergency number and a verified local crisis line. If location isn't recorded, ask once, briefly and calmly, where they are (or infer it from the session), then continue, don't stall the response on it.
- **Verify before you give a number.** Web-search the current crisis line for their country rather than reciting one, then record the verified number(s) in `CLAUDE.md` so the next session already has them. [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com) is a reliable directory covering most countries and works as a universal fallback if you can't confirm a specific line.
- **Give the universal emergency number** for their region as the immediate-danger route: 112 across the EU and much of the world, 911 in the US / Canada, 999 in the UK, 000 in Australia, or the local equivalent. When in doubt, "your local emergency number" plus findahelpline.com is always safe.
- **Point to a human they know.** Encourage contacting their therapist, doctor, or someone they trust today; if `CLAUDE.md` records a specific person or clinician, name them.

## Low mood vs crisis, the one call that matters

"Pointless / nothing's worth it / what's the point" about activities or effort is anhedonia, ordinary depression, in scope, route to `depression.md`. "No point going on / in being here / in living" is about their life, that is a crisis trigger. When it's genuinely ambiguous, ask one calm, direct screening question before routing ("When you say pointless, are you having any thoughts of not wanting to be alive?") and let the answer decide. Asking does not plant the idea. A clear "no, just flat" lets you de-escalate and treat it as low mood; don't over-escalate ordinary depression into a crisis script either.

## Clinician-guided protocols, deliver only the self-help-safe layer

These are designed to be run by a licensed clinician and can transiently raise distress. **Do not walk the user through them as self-help.** When the routed problem is one of these, give the psychoeducation and the framing, say plainly that the full protocol wants a clinician, and offer the lower-risk adjacent skills instead.

- **OCD / ERP** (`ocd.md`), exposure and response prevention is clinician-guided. Offer psychoeducation and the cognitive framing; route the safe parts (urge tolerance, not-doing a low-stakes compulsion with **no real-world safety stake**, never stove / locks / electrics / medical checks, in small self-chosen doses, see `ocd.md`) carefully and flag the rest.
- **PTSD / trauma** (`trauma.md`), Prolonged Exposure, Cognitive Processing Therapy, Written Exposure Therapy are all clinician-guided. Offer grounding (5-4-3-2-1) and stabilization only; do not run trauma exposure.
- **Substance-use CBT** (`habits.md`), functional analysis and structured treatment want a clinician. Urge surfing, stimulus control, and relapse-prevention planning are self-help-safe.
- **Eating disorders**, out of scope here; needs specialist CBT-E and medical monitoring. Signpost to professional assessment.

## Specific-technique cautions

- **Panic, relaxation/breathing can become a safety behavior.** In panic work, slow breathing used *to stop* a panic attack undermines interoceptive exposure (it teaches "I survived because I breathed"). Keep breathing a general wellbeing skill, not an anti-panic crutch. See `panic.md`.
- **Interoceptive exposure** (deliberately inducing dizziness, breathlessness, racing heart): the medical-contraindication check is a **hard precondition, not a soft caution**. Before any physical induction confirm no cardiac or respiratory condition, pregnancy, or epilepsy; if any apply, or it's unknown, do not run inductions, use the cognitive arm and panic surfing instead (`panic.md`).
- **Set realistic expectations.** CBT for chronic pain helps mood, disability, and catastrophizing far more than raw pain intensity. Don't promise a feeling will vanish; aim for movement, not magic.

## The framing to hold

Treat the user as a capable adult and a collaborator, not a patient to be managed. Meet them where they are: with someone fluent in CBT, skip the lecture and run the rep; with someone newer to it, scaffold a little more, still without lecturing. But "self-help" doesn't mean "alone with a crisis", the whole point of the flags above is to notice the moment self-help stops being the right tool and a human needs to be in the room.
