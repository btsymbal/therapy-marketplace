## How to use this file

The specialized layer for **health/illness anxiety**. It assumes the transdiagnostic core in `core-toolkit.md` (restructuring, behavioral experiments) and adds the two health-specific moves: response prevention for checking and reassurance-seeking, and attention training away from the body. Deliver every step per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one step per turn, end on a question, wait.**

**Medical-reality gate, first.** Response prevention targets the *anxiety and the repeated checking*, not the legitimacy of one proper assessment. Before cutting checking, confirm the symptom has had (or has a booked plan for) one appropriate medical evaluation. A genuinely unevaluated, persistent symptom may warrant real assessment; "stop googling, tolerate uncertainty" applied to that is the somatic version of restructuring a true thought (`walkthrough-protocol.md`). Once it has been checked once, the *repeated re-checking* is the target.

## 1 · Reducing checking & reassurance-seeking (response prevention)

**Reach for it when:** the anxiety is maintained by checking and reassurance, symptom googling, body checks, asking family or doctors, repeated tests. These give short-term relief but reinforce the anxiety, so cutting them is the core treatment.

**Walk it through:**
1. Identify and monitor all the checking/reassurance behaviors. Ask: "What do you actually do when the health worry spikes, googling, body-checking, asking someone, booking a test? List them all."
2. Make the cycle explicit: each one gives only short-term relief, then feeds the anxiety back stronger. Ask: "After you check, how long does the relief last before the worry returns?"
3. Response prevention, in graded steps, not all at once, cut, postpone, or resist a chosen behavior. Ask: "Which one is the easiest to cut or delay first?"
4. When the urge hits, tolerate the uncertainty and the anxiety spike without checking, and let it subside on its own. Ask: "Can you sit with the not-knowing this time and watch what the anxiety does if you don't check?"
5. Optionally corral the worrying into a brief scheduled "worry time" for health concerns. Ask: "Would a fixed 15-minute slot for health worries help contain it?"
6. Track that anxiety falls over repetitions without the checking. Ask: "Over the last few urges you resisted, what happened to the anxiety each time?"

**If it stalls:** body-focus keeps pulling them in → **attention training** (2). Catastrophic illness interpretations drive it → **cognitive restructuring** (`core-toolkit.md` technique 2). Belief "this symptom means something is wrong" feels true → **behavioral experiment** + **interoceptive exposure** (`core-toolkit.md` technique 5, `panic.md`). Can't tolerate uncertainty → intolerance-of-uncertainty work (`anxiety-worry.md`). Reassurance comes from others → cut it at the source, agree limits with family/GP.

## 2 · Attention training

**Reach for it when:** chronic inward body-scanning is amplifying and "finding" symptoms. The more they monitor the body, the more sensations it surfaces; training attention outward reduces the preoccupation. Pairs with response prevention (1).

**Walk it through:**
1. Catch the body-scanning. Ask: "When do you notice yourself scanning your body for symptoms, and how often across a day?"
2. Redirect attention to external activities, the task in front of them, surroundings, other people. Ask: "Right now, what outside yourself can you put your attention on instead?"
3. Practice it daily, not only when anxious. Ask: "When each day could you run a short outward-attention rep as routine?"
4. Combine it with response prevention so the urge to check and the inward focus are both starved. Ask: "Next time a sensation grabs you, can you redirect outward *and* not check?"

**If it stalls:** attention snaps back to the body → shorter reps, lower-stakes settings first. Preoccupation persists despite training → return to **response prevention** (1) and restructure the catastrophic interpretation (`core-toolkit.md` technique 2).
