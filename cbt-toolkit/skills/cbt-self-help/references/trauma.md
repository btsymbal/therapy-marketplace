## How to use this file

Self-help-safe to deliver and fully operationalize: grounding and stabilization (1). That is the runnable skill in this file, and the down-regulation tool other files cross-reference. Everything else (PE, CPT, WET) is **clinician-delivered, not self-run**: present it as psychoeducation, what a clinician would do and why, never as a protocol to walk the user through. Do not run trauma exposure as self-help. The core nine live in `core-toolkit.md`; for crisis-level distress where no cognitive skill will land, DBT TIPP is in `third-wave.md`.

For adult PTSD, trauma-focused CBT and EMDR are first-line; among CBT variants CPT, CT, and PE have the strongest evidence (NNT <4 to lose the diagnosis; PTSD shows the largest CBT effect overall, g 1.27). All trauma-focused protocols are clinician-guided.

## 1 · Grounding (5-4-3-2-1) and stabilization

**Reach for it when:** flashbacks, dissociation, feeling unreal or pulled out of the present, or acute anxiety / flooding too high for any cognitive work. This is the lower-risk skill that re-anchors attention in the present through the senses. Other files point here as the down-regulation step before thinking is possible.

**Walk it through (guide it live, one sense at a time, don't list them all at once):**
1. Settle them into the room. "Feet on the floor, what's one thing you can see right now?" Then name, in turn:
2. **5 things you can see.** Slow, one at a time, describe each briefly.
3. **4 things you can hear or feel.**
4. **3 things you can touch.** Have them actually touch them.
5. **2 things you can smell.**
6. **1 thing you can taste.**
7. Check in: "Where's the body now, more here than a minute ago?" (Per-sense ordering varies by source; the point is the slow sensory sweep, not the exact list.)

**Other forms if 5-4-3-2-1 doesn't fit:** Categories (name items in a category, e.g. cities, animals); Body Awareness (simple physical actions, press palms together, stretch); Mental Exercises (count backwards from 100 by 7s, spell a word backwards).

**If it stalls:** switch to **diaphragmatic breathing** (`core-toolkit.md`); if distress is too high for any skill, **DBT TIPP** (`third-wave.md`); for distress about something genuinely unchangeable, **radical acceptance** (`third-wave.md`). If grounding consistently fails and trauma symptoms are driving this, that is the signal the work has outgrown self-help, route to the clinician.

## 2 · Prolonged Exposure (PE)

> [!warning] Clinician-delivered, not self-run. Educational framing only. Do not walk the user through trauma exposure.

**Reach for it when:** they ask what trauma treatment actually involves, or why avoidance keeps the memory raw. PE gradually approaches avoided trauma memories, feelings, and situations. Clinician-guided.

**What a clinician does (educational, do not self-run):**
1. Early sessions teach a breathing technique and the rationale.
2. **In-vivo:** build a list of avoided people / places / activities and confront them step by step.
3. **Imaginal:** from about session 3, revisit and recount the trauma memory with the provider, listening to recordings between sessions. (8-15 weekly 60-90 min sessions, ~3 months.)

**If it stalls:** the clinician may switch to **CPT** (3) as an interchangeable choice, **WET** (4) as a lower-burden option, or CT / EMDR. The self-help layer the user can use alongside any of this is **grounding** (1).

## 3 · Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)

> [!warning] Clinician-delivered, not self-run. Educational framing only.

**Reach for it when:** explaining how trauma changes core beliefs and how those are treated. CPT evaluates and changes trauma-related "stuck points" across safety, trust, control, self-esteem, and intimacy. Clinician-guided.

**What a clinician does (educational, do not self-run):**
1. Identify negative trauma beliefs ("stuck points").
2. Examine whether the facts actually support them.
3. Consider alternative ways of thinking.
4. (Optional) Written trauma account around session 3: write a detailed account, read it privately, then aloud next session.
5. Worksheets between sessions. (~12 weekly sessions.)

**If it stalls:** the clinician may switch to **PE** (2), **WET** (4), or EMDR. Note the cognitive moves resemble the restructuring in `core-toolkit.md`, but applied to trauma beliefs they belong with the clinician, not in self-help. The user's safe layer stays **grounding** (1).

## 4 · Written Exposure Therapy (WET)

> [!warning] Clinician-delivered, not self-run. Educational framing only.

**Reach for it when:** they ask about a briefer, lower-burden trauma protocol. WET is brief, manualized, and used when full PE / CPT isn't feasible. Clinician-guided.

**What a clinician does (educational, do not self-run):** Session 1, psychoeducation plus a first 30-min narrative. Sessions 2-5, feedback, instructions, a 30-min write, check-in. They write about the traumatic experience in detail, paying particular attention to the thoughts and emotions felt *at the time*. No homework, no required in-vivo. (5 sessions, ~50 min each.)

**If it stalls:** the clinician may move to **PE** (2) or **CPT** (3), or CT / EMDR. For the user, the runnable skill remains **grounding** (1), the only piece of this file to deliver as self-help.
