## How to route

The user won't name a diagnosis, they'll name a feeling or a situation ("I'm spiraling about tomorrow", "I feel like a fraud", "can't make myself start"). Your job: from what they say plus the concrete moment, identify the **problem area**, then reach for that area's **first-line technique** and open its file. Pick one technique to start, not a menu. Re-route freely if their answers point elsewhere.

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) and get a feeling + 0-100 intensity + a concrete moment first (`SKILL.md` flow steps 1-2).

| Trauma memories, flashbacks, dissociation, hypervigilance | PTSD / trauma | `trauma.md` (clinician-guided, read `safety.md`) |
| "Logic isn't changing the feeling", sticky/fused thoughts, distress too high to think | Acceptance / distress tolerance | `third-wave.md` |
| Resistance to change itself, mixed feelings about getting better, can't get going despite knowing how | Motivation / resistance | `burns-team.md` |
| Distress driven by a real, solvable problem (the thought is **true**: a genuine deadline, a real loss, an actual task undone) | Problem to solve, not a distortion | problem-solving (`core-toolkit.md` 8), do **not** restructure a true thought |

| Generically "stuck" / blocked / can't move forward, no clear flavour yet | Needs triage | ask one locating question, then route (usually procrastination, low mood, or worry) |

If two rows fit, ask one clarifying question to pick, or start with the one tied to the most acute feeling.

## Multi-problem and re-routing

- Most sessions are one problem; some are layered (worry *and* self-criticism; panic *and* avoidance). Work one at a time, the most acute first. Name the others and offer to come back.
- If a walk-through reveals the real problem is different from where you started, say so plainly and switch files. Don't finish the wrong technique for tidiness.
- If a Burns method (double-standard, cost-benefit, externalization of voices) fits better than the Beckian default, reach for it, `burns-team.md` and `self-esteem-motivation.md` carry them.
- When distress is too high to think, route to down-regulation first (`third-wave.md` TIPP, `trauma.md` grounding, `core-toolkit.md` breathing), then back.
- **"Overwhelmed" alone** splits two ways: a concrete task they can't start → procrastination (`self-esteem-motivation.md`); diffuse emotional flooding / can't-think → down-regulate first (`third-wave.md`). Ask which it is before routing.
- **Post-event rumination** (replaying an event already over, "everyone thought I sounded stupid") is not a forward-exposure case: open with restructuring the mind-read (`core-toolkit.md` 2) and treat the post-mortem itself as a safety behavior to drop, see `social-anxiety.md`.
- **Acute night vs chronic insomnia:** at 2am don't open the multi-week CBT-I package; restructure the "wrecked tomorrow" catastrophizing + the get-out-of-bed rule + paradoxical intention in the moment, reserve the diary / sleep-restriction for a planning session. See `sleep.md`.
