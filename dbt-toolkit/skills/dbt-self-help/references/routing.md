## How to route

The user won't name a module — they'll name a feeling or a situation ("I'm about to blow up at
him", "I can't stop crying and I need to get through tonight", "she keeps walking over me and I
can't say no", "I can't accept that it's over"). Your job: from what they say plus the concrete
moment, identify the **problem area** and the **level of arousal**, then reach for that area's
**first-line skill** and open its file. Pick one skill to start, not a menu. Re-route freely if
their answers point elsewhere.

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) and get an emotion + 0-100 / SUDS intensity + a concrete
moment first (`SKILL.md` flow steps 1-2). The single most important early read in DBT: **are
they flooded right now?** If arousal is so high they can't think (mid-crisis, panicking, an urge
they can barely hold), route to crisis survival first (`distress-tolerance.md`, TIPP) regardless
of the underlying problem — you cannot do change-oriented work through a flooded nervous system.

## Front door: what the user says → problem area

| What they bring | Problem area | File |
|---|---|---|
| Flooded right now, can't think, panicking, "I can't calm down", an urge they can barely hold (lash out, quit, drink, binge, self-harm) | Acute crisis → crisis survival | `distress-tolerance.md` (TIPP first); self-harm urge also `safety.md` |
| An intense emotion they want to understand or shift — too angry, too anxious, crushed, ashamed — attached to a situation | Emotion dysregulation | `emotion-regulation.md` |
| About to act on an emotion in a way that'll make it worse (send it, say it, throw it) | Impulse about to fire | STOP then the emotion (`emotion-regulation.md`); if too high, TIPP first (`distress-tolerance.md`) |
| A painful reality that can't be changed — a loss, an ending, an illness, the past, someone else's behavior — and they're suffering from fighting it | Reality acceptance | `distress-tolerance.md` (Radical Acceptance) |
| An interpersonal situation: needs to ask for something, say no, set a boundary, handle a conflict, or repair one | Interpersonal | `interpersonal-effectiveness.md` |
| "Logic isn't moving this feeling", sticky / fused, can't get distance, distress too high to think | Acceptance / distress tolerance | `distress-tolerance.md` + `mindfulness.md` (Wise Mind) |
| Numb, dissociating, "I can't feel anything", floaty / unreal | Down-regulate the other direction → grounding / crisis survival | `distress-tolerance.md` (TIPP, Self-Soothe, sensation); `safety.md` if can't come out of it |
| Scattered, can't focus, can't access any skill, "my head's everywhere" | Needs the foundational module | `mindfulness.md` (Wise Mind, One-Mindfully) |
| Building long-term resilience, fewer crises, "I keep ending up here" | Prevention / vulnerability reduction | `emotion-regulation.md` (ABC PLEASE) |
| Recurring nightmares disrupting sleep | Nightmare Protocol (self-help-safe only for non-trauma) | `emotion-regulation.md` (see `safety.md`) |
| Family / parent–teen conflict, all-or-nothing splitting, escalating "you always / you never" | Walking the Middle Path (DBT-A) | `interpersonal-effectiveness.md` |
| Suicidal thoughts, self-harm plan or injury, harm to others | OUT OF SCOPE for self-help | `safety.md` — stop and signpost |
| Generically "overwhelmed", no clear flavour yet | Needs triage — split two ways (below) | ask one locating question, then route |

**"Overwhelmed" alone splits two ways:** diffuse emotional flooding / can't-think → down-regulate
first (`distress-tolerance.md`, TIPP); a specific situation or emotion they *can* name and think
about → route to that (`emotion-regulation.md` or `interpersonal-effectiveness.md`). Ask which it
is before routing.

If two rows fit, ask one clarifying question to pick, or start with the one tied to the most
acute arousal.

The subset to reach for **first** in each area. Each skill lives in the listed file; the
foundational module is `mindfulness.md`.

| Problem area | First-line skill(s) | File |
|---|---|---|
| Acute crisis / flooded | TIPP (temperature first if spiking), then ACCEPTS / Self-Soothe / IMPROVE | `distress-tolerance.md` |
| Urge to act on a self-destructive impulse | Pros and Cons (acting vs not) + TIPP to lower the urge | `distress-tolerance.md` |
| Reality acceptance (unchangeable pain) | Radical Acceptance → Turning the Mind → Willingness → Half-Smile & Willing Hands | `distress-tolerance.md` |

| Emotion that doesn't fit the facts | Opposite Action (all the way) | `emotion-regulation.md` |
| Extreme emotion, skills breakdown point | Managing Extreme Emotions (crisis-survival front end; see `safety.md`) | `emotion-regulation.md` |
| Long-term vulnerability reduction | ABC PLEASE (Accumulate positives, Build mastery, Cope ahead + PLEASE self-care) | `emotion-regulation.md` |
| Get what you want / say no | DEAR MAN | `interpersonal-effectiveness.md` |

## The acceptance/change decision tree

This is the spine of DBT and resolves most "which skill?" questions. When an emotion is driving
the distress, run it (it lives in full in `emotion-regulation.md`):

1. **Check the Facts** — does the emotion, and its intensity, fit the actual facts of the
   situation?
2. If the emotion **does not** fit the facts → **Opposite Action** (act opposite to the urge,
   all the way). `emotion-regulation.md`.
3. If the emotion **fits the facts** and the situation **can be changed** → **Problem Solving**
   (or **DEAR MAN** if the change is an interpersonal ask). `emotion-regulation.md` /
   `interpersonal-effectiveness.md`.
4. If the emotion **fits the facts** but the situation **cannot be changed** → **Radical
   Acceptance** and **ride the wave** (Mindfulness of Current Emotion). `distress-tolerance.md` /
   `mindfulness.md`.

Acceptance and change are two poles of one dialectic, not opposites to choose between
ideologically — the *facts* decide which the moment calls for. The modules sit on the poles:
Mindfulness and Distress Tolerance are the **acceptance** side; Emotion Regulation and
Interpersonal Effectiveness are the **change** side (`foundations.md`).

## Ordering rules and re-routing

- **Crisis survival before reality acceptance.** If they're at risk of acting in a way that makes
  things worse, get through the moment first (TIPP, ACCEPTS) before any acceptance work. You
  can't radically accept anything mid-flood.
- **Down-regulate before change or interpersonal work.** Too activated to think → a
  crisis-survival skill first, then the cognitive or interpersonal skill once they're back in the
  window.
- **Most sessions are one skill; some are layered** (an intense emotion *and* an interpersonal
  ask; a crisis *and* an unchangeable loss). Work one at a time, the most acute arousal first.
  Name the others and offer to come back.
- **Re-route plainly** if a walk-through reveals the real problem is elsewhere (you started on the
  emotion, it's actually a boundary they need to set). Say so and switch files; don't finish the
  wrong skill for tidiness.
- **Mindfulness underlies everything.** When no skill is landing because attention is too
  scattered or emotion and logic are at war, drop to Wise Mind / One-Mindfully (`mindfulness.md`)
  and build back up.
