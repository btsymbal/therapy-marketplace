## When SFBT fits trauma (and when it doesn't)

A hard past event the person is carrying, where they want a **strengths-based approach that does not require revisiting the memory**. The evidence here is early and based mostly on uncontrolled designs — treat it as emerging, not established. SFBT can support trauma survivors who don't want to (or aren't ready to) process the memory directly, by building hope, safety, and day-to-day functioning. It is **not** a trauma-processing therapy.

**Read `safety.md` before this file.** Complex PTSD requiring memory processing — especially with co-occurring substance use — is out of scope; the evidence-based routes there are trauma-focused CBT (Prolonged Exposure, CPT), EMDR, or stabilization models. SFBT is for *selected* presentations where the client chooses a non-revisiting, forward-focused approach.

Assumes the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`; this adds the trauma-specific care.

## Technique emphasis

- **Coping questions** (`core-techniques.md` §7) — the lead and safest move: "How have you managed since this happened? What's kept you going?" Surfaces resilience without going into the event.
- **Scaling** (§8) — safety, hope, day-to-day functioning. "How safe do you feel right now, 0 to 10?" Safety-scaling can also flag when to slow down or signpost.
- **Exception questions** (§6), **client-led** — "When do you feel a bit safer, or a bit more like yourself?" Let the client decide what to look at; never push toward the memory.
- **Miracle question** (§5) — **only if the client chooses it and is not recently triggered.** With care, and never as a way to surface the trauma.
- **Relationship questions** (§9) — to identify support systems.

## The clinical position

SFBT practitioners aren't "problem-phobic" — the client is allowed to raise the trauma whenever *they* want; you simply don't steer them into it or require it. The stance is: follow the client, stay strengths- and safety-focused, build hope. If the client wants or needs to process the memory, that's a signal to signpost trauma-focused care, not to attempt exposure here.

## Clear contraindication

Complex PTSD with co-occurring substance use disorder — SFBT is absent from the evidence base for this, and the indicated approaches are structured (Seeking Safety, DBT-based stabilization, trauma-processing protocols). Recent acute traumatization, dissociation, or anything where the client can't stay grounded → stabilization and a clinician, per `safety.md`. Do not run the miracle question on someone acutely triggered.

## If it stalls

The session pulls toward the memory and the client gets distressed → slow down, ground, return to coping and safety-scaling; if they need to process it, signpost (`safety.md`). Can't find any safer moment → coping (§7) only, and an observation task framed gently. Dissociation, fresh trauma, or complex PTSD → stop the technique, `safety.md`, signpost trauma-focused care.
