## When SFBT fits substance use

Wanting to cut down or stay off a substance, and keeping recovery momentum going. SFBT is best positioned as an **adjunct within broader addiction treatment, not a standalone intervention** for severe substance use disorder. It's listed among evidence-based options and has been used with substance-using parents in child-welfare contexts. The work builds on the times the client didn't use, their reasons for change, and their readiness — rather than confronting denial.

Assumes the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`; this adds the substance-use emphasis.

## Technique emphasis

- **Pre-session change** (`core-techniques.md` §3) — "Has anything been different since you decided to do something about this?" Catches the momentum that often starts before the first session.
- **Scaling** (§8) — readiness to change, motivation, confidence, days sober. "Where are you on wanting this, 0 to 10? What makes it that and not lower?"
- **Exception questions** (§6) — "Tell me about days, or even hours, when you didn't use — what was different? What were you doing? Who was around?" Build on deliberate exceptions.
- **Coping questions** (§7) — for early recovery or crisis: "How have you got through the cravings you've already resisted?"
- **EARS** (§12) in follow-up — "What's been better?" including any reduction or sober time.

## SFBT or MI first?

SFBT and Motivational Interviewing share a non-confrontational base but differ in mechanism: **MI resolves ambivalence; SFBT assumes change is already underway.** If the client is significantly ambivalent about *whether* to change, MI usually needs to come first — SFBT's future-focused questions can feel premature and dismissive while they're still weighing it. Once they've decided to change, SFBT helps construct what change looks like. See `integration.md`.

## Scope and safety

Severe substance use disorder, withdrawal risk, or co-occurring complex PTSD needs structured treatment and medical oversight — SFBT is adjunct, not primary, and `safety.md` (complex PTSD + SUD is explicitly out of scope) governs. Functional analysis and structured relapse treatment want a clinician; signpost. Self-help-safe here means motivation, exception-building, coping, and readiness work — not managing detox or severe dependence alone.

## If it stalls

Ambivalent about quitting at all → MI first (`integration.md`); don't push the goal. No sober exceptions findable → coping (§7) and an observe-for-exceptions task. Talk turns to severe dependence / withdrawal / a dual diagnosis → `safety.md`, signpost specialist care.
