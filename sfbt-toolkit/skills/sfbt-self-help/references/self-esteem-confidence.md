## When SFBT fits self-esteem & confidence

"I'm a fraud / not good enough", low confidence, a harsh self-view. There are no targeted trials of SFBT as a primary self-esteem treatment; the support is subsumed under its general mental-health evidence. Treat it as practice-based but well-aligned: SFBT builds self-esteem by surfacing the times the person already coped, succeeded, or liked themselves, and getting *them* to articulate the competence — which lands harder than being told they're fine.

Assumes the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`; this adds the confidence-specific emphasis.

## Technique emphasis

- **Scaling self-liking / confidence** (`core-techniques.md` §8) — "On 0 to 10, where 10 is feeling completely comfortable and confident in yourself, where are you? How come that and not lower?" The upward-from-0 question forces them to name evidence of their own worth.
- **Coping questions** (§7) — "Given everything you've been through, how have you kept going?" Reframes survival as strength.
- **Compliments, especially indirect** (§10) — "How did you manage that?" makes them find and state their own competence; far stronger here than direct praise, which a self-critical person will deflect.
- **Exception questions** (§6) — "Tell me about times you felt good about yourself, or handled something well — what was different then?"
- **Miracle question** (§5) — build a concrete picture of a confident self: "When you wake up feeling at ease with yourself, what's the first thing you'd notice you were doing differently?"

## Adaptations

- **Expect deflection; route it back.** Self-critical clients discount the positive. When they wave off an exception ("that doesn't count"), don't argue — ask "how did you do it anyway?" and let them supply the answer.
- **Indirect over direct.** Praise bounces off; questions that make them name their own competence stick. Lean on §10's indirect compliments.

## If it stalls

Every exception gets discounted → switch to indirect competence questions; scale self-liking and work the "why not lower?" relentlessly but gently. The self-attack is entrenched and pervasive → SFBT may not be enough on its own; a core-belief / self-compassion approach (CBT, compassion-focused) can fit alongside — see `integration.md`. Any hopelessness shading into "no point in me being here" → `safety.md`.
