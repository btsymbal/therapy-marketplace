## When SFBT fits grief (honest status)

Bereavement — missing someone, "how do I carry on after this loss". Be honest about the evidence: SFBT for grief is **practice-based, not evidence-based**. The only peer-reviewed study is decades old with no controlled follow-up; the rest is practitioner writing with no outcome data. For clinical-level or prolonged grief, an evidence-based grief-specific approach is the better referral. SFBT can offer gentle, strengths-based support for everyday bereavement and for rebuilding functioning — not a treatment for complicated grief.

Assumes the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`; this adds the grief-specific care. Hold this lightly — grief is not a "problem to solve", and the future-focus must never imply the person should be "over it".

## Technique emphasis

- **Problem-free talk** (`core-techniques.md` §2) — here, talk about the person who died and their life, not only the loss; it honours the relationship and surfaces what the bond gave them.
- **Coping questions** (§7) — the lead move: "How have you managed since the loss? What's helped you through the hardest days?"
- **Scaling** (§8) — functioning, connection to others, hope for the future. Not "how sad are you" — scale getting-through-the-day, gently.
- **Exception questions** (§6) — "When have you felt even a little more like yourself recently? What was happening?"
- **Miracle question** (§5) — used **carefully and only if it fits**: framed not as the grief vanishing but as "what would life look like when the grief has made a bit of space for other things too?"

## Adaptations

- **Don't future-rush.** The miracle question can land as "hurry up and move on" if mistimed. Lead with coping and honouring the person; reach for the preferred future only when the client is oriented toward rebuilding, and frame it as *alongside* the grief, not instead of it.
- **Name the limits.** If this is prolonged or complicated grief, say plainly that grief-specific therapy exists and may fit better.

## If it stalls

The client isn't ready to look forward at all → stay entirely in coping and honouring the relationship; that's a complete session. Grief looks prolonged/complicated or there's risk → signpost grief-specific care; `safety.md` if any crisis trigger appears. The miracle question feels offensive in the moment → drop it, apologise lightly, return to coping.
