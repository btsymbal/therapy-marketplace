## When SFBT fits depression

Mild-to-moderate adult depression — flat, heavy, withdrawn, "what's the point", doing less. This is one of SFBT's better-supported areas: the most conservative meta-analysis (RCT-only) puts the effect in the moderate range, and a primary-care RCT delivered meaningful symptom reduction in just 3 short (20–30 min) sessions. SFBT scales down well and works in brief contacts. For severe, chronic, or complex depression, expect it may help but longer-term work is often needed — and any suicidal thought means `safety.md` first, not this file.

Assumes the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`; this adds the depression-specific emphasis.

## Technique emphasis

- **Miracle question** (`core-techniques.md` §5) — project into a depression-free preferred future; the imagery bypasses hopelessness when goal-setting alone can't get traction.
- **Exception questions** (§6) — "When was the last time your mood lifted even a little? What was different then?" Sort deliberate from random; build on the deliberate.
- **Scaling** (§8) — mood, motivation, hope. Lead with upward-from-0 ("how come you're at a 3 and not at 0?") to surface the competence that's keeping them off the floor.
- **Coping questions** (§7) — "How have you kept going through this?" The re-entry move when they're at or near 0 and can't find an exception.
- **Best-hopes goal** (§4) — translate "stop feeling depressed" into what they'd be *doing* instead.
- **Between-session tasks** (§11) — observational first ("notice what you'd like to keep happening"), then behavioral ("do more of what works") as exceptions appear.
- **EARS** (§12) in every follow-up.

## Adaptations

- **Short sessions are fine** — 3–6 sessions, even 20–30 minutes each, is an effective dose in primary-care-style contexts. Don't pad.
- **Start small with tasks.** Depressed clients underestimate how much a small action will lift mood; a behavioral task that "does more of an exception" is, in effect, a gentle experiment that disproves "nothing helps."
- **Lead with exceptions and coping over the miracle** if hopelessness makes the miracle question feel flippant — come back to it once there's a foothold.

## Honest caveats

The overall SFBT evidence base has real methodological weaknesses and much of it comes from authors who advocate the approach. The honest claim is that SFBT is *at least as* effective as established short-term alternatives, with the advantage of brevity — not that it's superior or a fast cure. Say so if the client asks.

## If it stalls

Can't picture any better future → good-day framing of the miracle (§5), or drop to exceptions (§6). At 0 and no exceptions → coping (§7), and don't force next-step planning. Heaviness blocks everything → stay with coping and an observational task; the next session's EARS may surface movement the client couldn't see in the moment. Any suicidal ideation → `safety.md`.
