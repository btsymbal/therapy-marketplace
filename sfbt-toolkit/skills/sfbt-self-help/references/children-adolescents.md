## When SFBT fits children & adolescents

Young people's low mood, anxiety, school-related problems, motivation, and behavior. Reasonable support across controlled studies; promising (if small-base) effects for school-related problems, and reductions in adolescent depression/anxiety. SFBT engages young people well because it's strengths-based and doesn't require a deficit-focused conversation they often resist.

Assumes the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`; this adds the developmental adaptation. Note: most self-help use will be a parent or the young person themselves; for younger children, work largely runs through the parent (`family-parenting.md`).

## Technique emphasis

- **Problem-free talk** (`core-techniques.md` §2) — essential for engaging adolescents; talk about what they're into before anything hard.
- **Good-day framing of the miracle question** (§5) — replace "miracle" with "a great day" or "things just a bit better" for younger children: "Imagine you wake up tomorrow and the day goes really well — what's the first thing that's different?"
- **Scaling adapted to 1–5 with visuals** (§8) — faces, a ladder, a thermometer; concrete anchors.
- **Relationship questions** (§9) — "What would your teacher / coach / friend notice when things are going well?"
- **EARS** (§12) in follow-up — "What's been better?"

## Adaptations

- **Flexible, short, accessible.** Session length should match engagement (as little as 15 minutes); use plain, non-clinical language.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Younger children do better with "a great day" and a 1–5 face-scale than with an open miracle image or a 0–10 number.
- **Route through the parent for younger children** — the parent becomes the agent of change; see `family-parenting.md`.

## Scope and safety

A young person in acute psychiatric crisis or at risk needs immediate intervention, not a solution-building session — `safety.md` overrides. For a child or adolescent, also weigh whether a parent/carer and a professional (school counsellor, GP) should be involved; self-help with a minor has real limits. Signpost rather than carry anything beyond mild, everyday difficulty alone.

## If it stalls

Adolescent disengaged / monosyllabic → more problem-free talk, drop the clinical register, scale something they care about. "Miracle" feels childish or silly to a teen → near-future framing ("what would need to happen this week for things to head the right way?"). Younger child can't do 0–10 → 1–5 with faces. Anything acute → `safety.md`.
