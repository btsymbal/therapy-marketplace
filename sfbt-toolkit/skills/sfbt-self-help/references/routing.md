## How to route

The user won't name a diagnosis or a technique — they'll name a situation or a wish ("I feel stuck at work", "things are flat and I want them better", "we keep having the same fight", "I can't seem to get going"). Your job: from what they say plus a little problem-free talk, identify the **area**, set a **goal** (best hopes, positive and concrete — `core-techniques.md` §4), then reach for that area's **first-line technique subset** and open its application file. Pick one move to start, not a menu. Re-route freely if their answers point elsewhere.

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) and set a direction first: problem-free talk + pre-session change + best-hopes goal + a scale reading (`SKILL.md` flow steps 1–2; `session-structure.md`).

Unlike a problem-routed model, SFBT applies **the same core sequence** to almost everything — the routing mostly decides *which subset to emphasize first* and *which population adaptation* to load, not a different therapy per problem.

| What they bring | Area | File |
|---|---|---|
| Flat, heavy, low, withdrawn, "what's the point", doing less, mild-to-moderate depression | Depression (mild–moderate) | `depression.md` |
| Worried, on edge, anxious, can't settle, general anxiety | Anxiety (general) | `anxiety.md` |
| Same fights, distance, "we keep going in circles", a relationship they want better | Couples / relationship distress | `couples-relationships.md` |
| Parenting strain, a child's behavior, family tension, "the house is at war" | Family / parenting | `family-parenting.md` |
| A child or teenager's low mood, anxiety, school refusal, motivation, behavior | Children & adolescents | `children-adolescents.md` |
| Wanting to cut down / stay off a substance, motivation to keep recovery going | Addiction / substance use (adjunct) | `addiction.md` |
| A hard past event they're carrying, but they want a strengths-based, non-revisiting approach | Trauma (selected, **not** complex PTSD) | `trauma.md` (read `safety.md` first) |
| Bereavement, missing someone, "how do I carry on after this loss" | Grief & bereavement | `grief.md` |
| "I'm a fraud / not good enough", low confidence, harsh self-view | Self-esteem / confidence | `self-esteem-confidence.md` |
| A work goal, a stuck career, performance, leadership, "coach me on this" | Workplace / coaching (SFBC) | `workplace-coaching.md` |
| Generically "stuck" / blocked / "nothing's working", wants to move forward, no clear flavour yet | Needs a goal first | set best hopes (`core-techniques.md` §4), then route by what they want |
| Active suicidal thoughts, self-harm, abuse/violence risk, acute psychosis, a presentation SFBT shouldn't carry alone | **Stop — safety / out of scope** | `safety.md` (overrides this table) |

If two rows fit, set the goal first — the goal usually tells you which area is live — or start with the one tied to the most acute feeling.

## The technique sequence is the same everywhere

Whatever the area, the engine is the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`: set a positive, concrete **goal** → build the **preferred future** (miracle question) → find **exceptions** where it's already happening → **scale** to locate position and the next small step → use **coping** questions if they're at or near 0 → widen with **relationship** questions → close with a **compliment + bridge + task**. The application file tells you which moves to lead with and how to adapt the wording for the population; it does not replace the sequence.

## Multi-goal, re-routing, and "follow the client"

- Most sessions have one goal; some are layered (low mood *and* a relationship strain). Work one at a time — the one tied to the most acute feeling, or the one they most want different first. Name the others and offer to come back.
- **Follow the client.** If a walk-through reveals the real goal is different from where you started, say so plainly and switch files. Don't finish the wrong technique for tidiness.
- **When they're overwhelmed or at 0 on the scale,** route to **coping questions** first (`core-techniques.md` §7), regardless of area — it's the universal re-entry move. Return to goal/exception/next-step work once a shred of competence has surfaced.
- **"Stuck" with no flavour yet** resolves by setting the goal: ask best hopes ("What would you like to be different?"), and the answer routes you. If they can't name any hope at all, the miracle question (§5) is the bypass — it uses imagery to get around goal-blocking hopelessness.
- **Ambivalent about changing at all** (not "how" but "whether")? Standard SFBT assumes the client wants change. If they genuinely don't yet, Motivational Interviewing may need to come first — see `integration.md`.
