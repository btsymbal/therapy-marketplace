## How to use this file

SFBT is not the right tool for everything, and sometimes another approach should lead or run alongside. This file says **when to blend with, defer to, or hand off** to a neighbouring approach. Reach for it when a session reveals that the client needs something SFBT doesn't provide — most often when they're ambivalent about change (MI), need cognitive restructuring (CBT), or are stuck inside a problem-identity narrative (narrative therapy). For anything that's a safety or scope issue rather than a fit issue, `safety.md` governs, not this file.

## SFBT and Motivational Interviewing (MI)

Shared non-confrontational, humanistic base. **Key distinction: MI resolves ambivalence and builds motivation to change; SFBT presupposes change is already occurring and builds on the momentum.**

- **SFBT leads** when the client has already decided to change and needs help constructing what change looks like.
- **MI leads first** when the client is ambivalent about *whether* to change — using SFBT's future-focused questions prematurely can feel dismissive. Once ambivalence resolves, hand to SFBT.
- Best blended in school settings with young people, and in addiction with clients in early contemplation (`addiction.md`).

In practice here: if best-hopes goal-setting keeps failing because the client isn't sure they *want* the change, stop pushing the goal and do MI-style work first — explore the ambivalence, the costs and benefits, their own reasons — then return to SFBT.

## SFBT and CBT

Complementary in integrated settings. **Key distinction: CBT identifies and restructures cognitive distortions and needs a theory of how the problem is maintained; SFBT requires no theory of maintenance and does no explicit cognitive restructuring.**

- **SFBT leads** when the client is future-focused, motivated, has identifiable exceptions, and the window is brief (primary care, early intervention).
- **Blend** when the client benefits from SFBT's goal-setting and exception-finding but also has entrenched cognitive distortions or behavioral avoidance that need structured work. (There's no single validated hybrid protocol — blend pragmatically.)
- **CBT leads** when the difficulty is driven by specific distortions or maladaptive avoidance needing systematic restructuring or exposure — specific phobia, panic with avoidance, OCD. For these, SFBT alone is insufficient (`safety.md`, `anxiety.md`).

If you have access to a CBT self-help skill in the same workspace, that's the natural place to hand off the restructuring/exposure work while SFBT keeps the goal-framing.

## SFBT and positive psychology

Strong conceptual overlap — the miracle question, scaling, and coping questions directly cultivate hope, agency, and pathway-thinking, aligning with well-being models. SFBT predates formal positive psychology but anticipates much of it.

- **Blend** in wellness coaching, post-acute support, resilience-building in medical populations, and non-clinical settings already using a positive-psychology frame. The two reinforce each other without tension.

## SFBT and narrative therapy

Shared social-constructionist base; both use exception/unique-outcome talk. **Key distinction: narrative therapy uses exceptions to deconstruct a dominant problem-narrative and re-author identity — which is why it's typically *not* brief; SFBT uses exceptions to build toward a preferred future and skips the narrative excavation, which is why it stays brief.**

- **Blend / hand off** when the problem is deeply embedded in the client's identity story ("I've always been the anxious one", "I'm just a failure") — re-authoring work may be needed alongside SFBT's preferred-future construction. If the self-narrative is the real block, SFBT's exception-building may not shift it alone; see also `self-esteem-confidence.md`.

## The honest bottom line

SFBT's most defensible claim is that it's *at least as* effective as established alternatives for mild-to-moderate presentations, with the advantage of brevity — not that it's superior or universal. Reaching for another approach when the fit is wrong isn't a failure of SFBT; it's the responsible move. When you hand off, do it cleanly: name what SFBT isn't providing, name what fits better, and (within this skill) switch to the relevant application file or to `safety.md`.
