## When SFBT fits workplace & coaching

A work goal, a stuck career, performance, leadership, "coach me on this" — and the everyday "I want to make progress / get unstuck" that isn't clinical at all. This is **Solution-Focused Brief Coaching (SFBC)**, the non-clinical adaptation of SFBT for goals and performance. The support here is practice-based. SFBC is a natural fit for the broadened, forward-looking triggers this skill responds to: it's future-focused, goal-driven, and assumes the person has the resources.

Assumes the core sequence in `core-techniques.md`; this adds the coaching emphasis.

## Technique emphasis

- **Best-hopes goal** (`core-techniques.md` §4), adapted for professional goals — "What are your best hopes from working on this?" → positive, concrete, within their influence.
- **Scaling** (§8) — performance, motivation, confidence, progress toward a target. "Where are you on getting this done, 0 to 10? What's a 5 look like?"
- **Exception questions** (§6) — "When in your work have you been at your best? What was different? What were you doing?" Build on deliberate exceptions.
- **EARS** (§12) in follow-up — "What's moved forward since last time?"
- **Between-session tasks** (§11) — "do more of what's working", or experiment with one new thing.

## The referral boundary (important)

SFBC is **coaching, not therapy**. When a clinical presentation surfaces in what started as a goal/coaching session — depression, anxiety, trauma, risk — recognize the boundary and apply **acknowledge → stabilize → refer**:
1. **Acknowledge** the issue empathically.
2. **Stabilize** with simple coping/exception questions if appropriate.
3. **Refer** to therapy / a clinician, and within this skill switch out of the coaching frame to the relevant application file (e.g. `depression.md`) or, if a flag fires, to `safety.md`.

The forward-looking triggers ("get unstuck", "make progress") are the front door to coaching — but stay alert for the moment a goal conversation is really a clinical one in disguise, and route accordingly.

## If it stalls

Goal stays vague ("just want things better at work") → press for concrete and observable: "What would you be *doing* differently? What would a colleague notice?" Stuck at the same scale point session after session → re-examine whether the goal is theirs and within their power (§4), or whether ambivalence means MI fits first (`integration.md`). A clinical issue surfaces → acknowledge, stabilize, refer; switch files or go to `safety.md`.
