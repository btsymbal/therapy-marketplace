## How to use this file

This file gives the **shape of a session** — the order the core techniques usually fall in. `core-techniques.md` gives each move; this gives the arc they form. Deliver it all per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: one question per turn, follow the client. The arc is documented in the SFBT literature, but it is a *typical* sequence, not a fixed agenda — "follow the client" always wins over completing the arc.

The whole skill maps onto these two arcs: a **first session** sets a goal and builds a preferred future; **every later session** opens with EARS ("What's better?"). Which one you're in is decided at the start by `CLAUDE.md` / the `sfbt-log/` (a returning user → EARS).

---

## First-session arc

1. **Problem-free talk** (`core-techniques.md` §2) — rapport and a quick surface-map of strengths before the problem is introduced.
2. **Pre-session change** (§3) — "Has anything already been a bit better since you decided to do something about this?"
3. **Brief problem acknowledgment** — acknowledge what they're bringing, honestly and in one line. *Do not* open an extended exploration of causes or history; SFBT acknowledges the problem and turns toward the goal.
4. **Best-hopes goal-setting** (§4) — co-construct the goal in positive, concrete terms. This is the north star for everything after.
5. **Miracle question** (§5) — generate a rich preferred-future picture; slow down and amplify.
6. **Exception-finding** (§6) — "When is even a little of that miracle already happening?"
7. **Scaling** (§8) — "On 0 to 10, where are you now?" Then upward-from-0 ("why not lower?") and the next small step.
8. **Relationship questions** (§9) — "What would [significant other] notice is different?"
9. **Consulting break** — a brief pause to formulate the closing message.
10. **End-of-session message** (§10–11) — compliment + bridge + one task matched to readiness.

Not every element appears in every first session. Follow the client: if the miracle question opens into a rich exception conversation, stay there. If they arrive in crisis, **coping questions** (§7) may replace most of this arc — and the safety scan (`safety.md`) comes before all of it.

## Subsequent-session arc (EARS)

1. **EARS opening** (§12) — "What's better since last time?" → Amplify → Reinforce → "What else is better?" Loop until the improvements are out.
2. **Amplify and reinforce** every reported improvement; help them attribute it to their own agency.
3. **Scaling for progress** (§8) — "Last time you were at a [4]. Where are you now? What moved it?"
4. **Exception / coping work as needed** (§6, §7) — if they're stuck or have slipped back, return to exceptions, or to coping if they're overwhelmed.
5. **Consulting break.**
6. **End-of-session message** (§10–11) — compliment + bridge + an updated task.
7. **Consolidation** (§13) — when they're at or near the goal (scaling 8–10), shift to maintenance and a planned, door-open ending.

This arc assumes you read `CLAUDE.md` at the start (`logging.md`) so you know the goal and the last scale position — that's what makes "What's better?" land rather than starting cold.

## Reading it as a guide, not a script

- **The goal sets the direction; the scale tracks it; exceptions and the miracle supply the content.** If you only had three moves, those are the three.
- **Crisis or overwhelm overrides the arc** — drop to coping (§7) and, if a `safety.md` flag fires, drop the arc entirely.
- **Brevity is client-determined.** SFBT runs typically 3–8 sessions, but "brief" means *they* decide when it's enough, not a fixed ceiling. Consolidation (§13) can happen in session three or session eight.
- **One arc per session in the log:** record `session: first` or `session: follow-up` in the entry (`logging.md`) so the progression is legible across the folder.
