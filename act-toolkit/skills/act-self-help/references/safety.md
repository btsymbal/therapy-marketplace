## Read this first, every session

Before routing, and continuously as distress can escalate mid-session, scan for the crisis triggers below. ACT self-help is educational, not treatment. Most distress (everyday anxiety, low mood, worry, self-criticism, being stuck, a craving, ordinary grief) is well within scope and the skill should just help. The flags below are the exceptions.

## Crisis triggers, stop and escalate

If any of these appear, **stop the technique** and shift to support and signposting. Do not run a defusion or acceptance exercise on active suicidality.

- Any mention of suicide, wanting to not exist, self-harm, or feeling there's no point going on, **including the indirect / passive forms**: "everyone'd be better off without me", "I wouldn't mind not waking up", "tired of being here". Watch for the same indirect forms in whatever language the user writes in, not just English.
- Plans or means to hurt themselves, or that they're at risk right now.
- Severe, immobilizing depression, or talk of harming someone else.
- Acute crisis where they can't keep themselves safe.

What to do: respond like a person, not a worksheet. Acknowledge it directly and without panic. Make clear this is past what self-help should carry alone, and that reaching a human matters more than any technique here. **Match their language**: deliver all of this in the language they are writing in, never switch them to English to read the script. Then surface help, localized to where they are (next section).

**If the risk is toward someone else** (not themselves): same calm, drop the technique, but the move is de-escalation and reaching emergency services / a human now (the local emergency number), not a suicide line.

Do not try to "fix" it with ACT. Stay with them, signpost, and gently keep checking they're safe. Lower-risk stabilizing skills (dropping anchor, grounding 5-4-3-2-1, paced breathing — `present-moment.md`) are fine as a bridge, not as a treatment.

## Surfacing crisis help, localized

Crisis numbers are country-specific and they change, so don't recite one from memory and don't hand someone a hotline that won't connect from where they are. Localize:

- **Use what's on record.** If the user's country / region is in `CLAUDE.md`, lead with the resources recorded there: the local emergency number and a verified local crisis line. If location isn't recorded, ask once, briefly and calmly, where they are (or infer it from the session), then continue, don't stall the response on it.
- **Verify before you give a number.** Web-search the current crisis line for their country rather than reciting one, then record the verified number(s) in `CLAUDE.md` so the next session already has them. [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com) is a reliable directory covering most countries and works as a universal fallback if you can't confirm a specific line.
- **Give the universal emergency number** for their region as the immediate-danger route: 112 across the EU and much of the world, 911 in the US / Canada, 999 in the UK, 000 in Australia, or the local equivalent. In the US the **988** Suicide and Crisis Lifeline is call-or-text. When in doubt, "your local emergency number" plus findahelpline.com is always safe.
- **Point to a human they know.** Encourage contacting their therapist, doctor, or someone they trust today; if `CLAUDE.md` records a specific person or clinician, name them.

## Low mood vs crisis, the one call that matters

"Pointless / nothing's worth it / what's the point" about activities or effort is ordinary low mood and values-drift, in scope, route to **values** + **committed action** (`values.md`, `committed-action.md`). "No point going on / in being here / in living" is about their life, that is a crisis trigger. When it's genuinely ambiguous, ask one calm, direct screening question before routing ("When you say pointless, are you having any thoughts of not wanting to be alive?") and let the answer decide. Asking does not plant the idea. A clear "no, just flat" lets you de-escalate and treat it as low mood; don't over-escalate ordinary depression into a crisis script either.

## Clinician-guided presentations, deliver only the self-help-safe layer

These are designed to be run by a licensed clinician and can transiently raise distress. **Do not walk the user through the full protocol as self-help.** When the presentation is one of these, give the psychoeducation and the framing, say plainly that the full protocol wants a clinician, and offer the lower-risk adjacent skills instead.

- **OCD** (`ocd.md`), ERP remains the guideline first-line treatment and ACT-for-OCD wants a clinician trained in both. Offer defusion psychoeducation and labelling applied to obsessional thoughts as a relationship-shift; do **not** design exposures or response-prevention, and **never** the playful-voice techniques (see below). Flag the rest.
- **PTSD / trauma** (`trauma.md`), trauma-focused work is clinician-guided. Offer **dropping anchor** (`present-moment.md`) and grounding for stabilization only; do **not** run long mindfulness sits, body scans, continuity-of-self exercises, or any trauma exposure / narrative.
- **Psychosis**, ACT here is strictly **adjunctive to antipsychotic medication** and coordinated with a prescriber. Not a self-help target; signpost to their clinical team.
- **Eating disorders**, out of scope here; ACT alone is contraindicated for anorexia and not an empirically supported standalone for any eating disorder. Signpost to specialist assessment with medical / nutritional monitoring; do **not** run the urge / willingness frame on restriction or purging.
- **Substance use** (`substance-use.md`), functional analysis and structured treatment want a clinician. Urge surfing, defusion from craving-thoughts, values work, and Choice Point / BOLD are self-help-safe.

## Specific-technique cautions

- **Playful voices (singing the thought, silly / cartoon voice) are contraindicated** for trauma-related intrusions, OCD obsessions, and shame-rooted thoughts. They need a strong sense of safety and can read as mocking the user's pain. Use a gentler defusion move (labelling, leaves on a stream) instead. See `defusion.md`.
- **Long mindfulness sits, body scans, and continuity-of-self exercises can destabilize** dissociative or severely traumatized users (flashbacks, dissociation). Prefer **dropping anchor** (`present-moment.md`), keep eyes open, check in frequently, and stop if distress climbs. See `trauma.md`, `self-as-context.md`.
- **"Letting go" framing can become suppression.** In leaves-on-a-stream and similar, the instruction is to *watch* thoughts pass, not to *make* them leave. For anxious users with sticky threat-thoughts, watch for it turning into "get rid of it" and re-anchor on observing. See `defusion.md`.
- **Acceptance is not resignation.** Making room for a feeling always serves a value; without that rationale it can collapse into giving up. Never run acceptance work without connecting it to what the user is willing to have the feeling *in service of*. See `acceptance.md`.
- **Set realistic expectations.** ACT changes the *relationship* to pain and distress, not necessarily their intensity. For chronic pain especially, don't promise the pain will shrink; aim for a fuller life carried alongside it. See `pain.md`.

## The framing to hold

Treat the user as a capable adult and a collaborator, not a patient to be managed. Meet them where they are: with someone fluent in ACT, skip the lecture and run the rep; with someone newer to it, scaffold a little more, still without lecturing. But "self-help" doesn't mean "alone with a crisis", the whole point of the flags above is to notice the moment self-help stops being the right tool and a human needs to be in the room.
