## How to use this file

This is the PTSD / trauma layer, and it is **gated**. ACT is not a first-line PTSD treatment, and trauma-focused work is clinician-guided. As self-help, deliver **only stabilization**: Dropping anchor (`present-moment.md` 4) and five-senses grounding (`present-moment.md` 3). Do **not** run long mindfulness sits or body scans (dissociation and flashback risk), continuity-of-self or other self-as-context exercises (`self-as-context.md` carries this caution), or any trauma exposure, narrative, or retelling. The safe framing to hold: **"acceptance does not mean condoning the trauma."** Keep eyes open, keep it short, pace it, and **stop if distress climbs** rather than pushing through. Anything beyond grounding → signpost a trauma-trained clinician. Assumes the model in `hexaflex.md`; deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one step per turn, end on a question, wait.**

## Off-limits as self-help (and why)

These are clinician-guided in trauma and are **not** to be run from this skill — name the line, don't cross it:
- **Long mindfulness sits and body scans** — sustained inward attention raises dissociation and flashback risk. Keep present-moment work brief and eyes-open only.
- **Continuity-of-self and other self-as-context exercises** — `self-as-context.md` carries this caution; the "observer" stance can destabilise around trauma.
- **Any trauma exposure, narrative, or retelling** — in trauma-focused ACT, exposure happens as a by-product of values pursuit inside a clinician's care, never as a solo self-help exercise.

The safe framing to keep close: **"acceptance does not mean condoning the trauma."** Acceptance is about making room for after-effects, not endorsing what happened. Anything past grounding → a trauma-trained clinician.

## 1 · Stabilization & grounding (the only self-help layer)

**Reach for it when:** the user is activated, flooded, dissociating, or pulled into a flashback, and needs to come back into the present and the body — safely, in small doses. This is steadying, not processing.

**Walk it through:** run **Dropping anchor / ACE** (`present-moment.md` 4) as the primary move — acknowledge what's showing up, come back into the body, engage with the world — keeping it short and **eyes open**. Use **five senses** grounding (`present-moment.md` 3) to re-anchor in the room. Take a 0–100 distress reading before and after. If the reading is *climbing* rather than settling, stop the exercise, slow right down, and re-orient to the room and to safety (`safety.md`). Ask: "Looking around the room right now — what are five things you can see, and where's the distress on 0–100?"

**If it stalls:** if grounding isn't settling the distress, or flashbacks/dissociation keep recurring → this is past the self-help line; signpost a trauma-trained clinician and stay with brief grounding only. Do **not** move on to acceptance of the trauma affect, defusion from trauma beliefs, self-as-context, or any values-based exposure — those are the clinician's work, not this file's.
