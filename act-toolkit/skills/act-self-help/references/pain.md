## How to use this file

This is the chronic-pain layer. It is the strongest evidence base ACT has, but that does **not** mean the pain shrinks — be explicit about this up front and don't let hope quietly attach to pain reduction (`safety.md`). The central reframe is the whole layer: **pain itself isn't the problem — the struggle with pain is.** The goal is not a smaller pain signal but a fuller life carried alongside it. These are condition adaptations, not new techniques: they foreground acceptance/willingness, values, and committed action, and point back to the process files for the actual walk-throughs. Assumes the model in `hexaflex.md`; deliver every guided piece per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one step per turn, end on a question, wait.**

## 1 · Willingness for pain

**Reach for it when:** the day has narrowed to bracing against the sensation, and the bracing is its own exhausting second layer. The aim is to let the pain be its actual size while life keeps moving — not to talk it down.

**Walk it through:**
1. Set the expectation cleanly, once. Say it plainly: "We're not trying to lower the pain. We're trying to lower the *fight* with it, so it governs less." Ask: "Are you willing to aim at a fuller life rather than a smaller pain?"
2. Make room for the sensation. Run Expansion (`acceptance.md` 1) or Physicalising emotions (`acceptance.md` 5) pointed at the pain itself — its shape, edges, temperature, movement — rather than at the story about it.
3. Name the two halves of willingness in plain terms: **activity engagement** (doing what matters even with pain present) and **pain willingness** (stopping the project of avoiding situations *because* pain might show up). Ask: "Which of those two is the bigger sticking point for you right now?"
4. Take it for a small test. Ask: "What's one thing you'd do in the next day if you were willing to bring the pain along rather than wait for it to leave?"

**If it stalls:** if willingness slides into grim endurance with no point to it → name the value first (3). If a sticky **thought** ("this means something's badly wrong"), not the sensation, is the hook → `defusion.md` 1. If the fight is exhausting and palpable → Dropping the rope (`acceptance.md` 2).

## 2 · Quota-contingent (values-based) pacing

**Reach for it when:** activity is governed by the pain dial — push on good days, collapse and rest on bad ones — and the boom-and-bust keeps the world shrinking. Resting *whenever pain spikes* is hidden avoidance; this replaces it with a planned, values-linked amount done regardless of the day's pain level.

**Walk it through:**
1. Find a values-linked activity and a baseline quota that's doable on a **bad** day without a flare. Ask: "For [the activity], how much could you do on a rough day and still be okay — not your best day, your worst one?"
2. Commit to the quota by plan, not by pain. Stop *at* the quota even when the day feels good; do the quota even when it doesn't. Ask: "What's the amount you'll hold whether the pain is loud or quiet today?"
3. Raise it in small, scheduled steps — not by how you feel. Ask: "What's the next small increase, and what date does it start?"

**If it stalls:** quota keeps getting broken on good days → shrink it; the discipline is holding it when you *could* do more. Pain-contingent resting keeps creeping back → name it as the avoidance move it is, and return to willingness (1). Fear of movement blocks the plan → `defusion.md` 11 (what's the hook) on the catastrophic prediction.

## 3 · Values-based action despite pain

**Reach for it when:** life has contracted around managing pain, and what matters has dropped off the calendar. This rebuilds direction with the pain still in the room.

**Walk it through:** map the live domains with the bull's-eye (`values.md` 2) to see how far pain has pushed life off-course, then build one concrete step with SMART-ACT (`committed-action.md` 1). Attach a **willingness-to-act rating** (`committed-action.md` 5) to each step — the question is never "will it hurt?" but "am I willing to do it with the pain present?" Keep steps small enough that willingness, not pain level, is the deciding factor.

**If it stalls:** steps keep getting postponed to a pain-free day that never comes → drop the precondition; willingness doesn't wait for low pain. Barriers are fused thoughts and feelings → `committed-action.md` 3 (FEAR/DARE). Beyond self-help scope → signpost an interdisciplinary pain-management program, which outperforms going it alone.
