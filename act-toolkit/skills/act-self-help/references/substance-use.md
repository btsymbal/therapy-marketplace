## How to use this file

This is the substance-use / craving layer. The working model: substance use as **experiential avoidance** — using to escape an aversive inner state — and ACT's alternative is willingness and acceptance of that state instead of escape. **Note up front:** functional analysis of use and any structured addiction treatment want a clinician (`safety.md`); what's self-help-safe here is urge surfing, defusion from craving-thoughts, values, and the Choice Point / BOLD drill. These are condition adaptations pointing back to the process files. Assumes the model in `hexaflex.md`; deliver every guided piece per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one step per turn, end on a question, wait.**

---

## 1 · Urge surfing

**Reach for it when:** a craving is spiking and the urge feels like a command that will only grow until it's obeyed. The move is to ride it, not fight it or feed it.

**Walk it through:** run Urge surfing (`acceptance.md` 4). The core teaching points to land live: a craving is a **wave** — it rises, peaks (usually within about 20–30 minutes), and subsides on its own if not acted on; you are the **surfer, not the wave**. Watch its shape and intensity 0–100 rather than trying to flatten it. Ask: "Can you stay on the board and watch it crest, without paddling for it or away from it?"

**If it stalls:** if the urge is overwhelming and there's no surface to surf from → ground first with Dropping anchor (`present-moment.md` 4), then return. If a craving-**thought** is the hook rather than the body sensation → defusion (2).

## 2 · Defusion from craving-thoughts

**Reach for it when:** the pull is carried by a sentence the mind keeps handing over — "I need it," "I can't stand this," "just this once." The aim is distance from the thought, not winning an argument with it.

**Walk it through:** run Labelling (`defusion.md` 1) on the exact craving-thought — "I'm having the thought that *I need it*" — so it's held as a mental event the mind produces, not a fact or an instruction to follow. Ask: "When you put 'I'm having the thought that…' in front of it, does it sit at any more distance — and does it still have to be obeyed?"

**If it stalls:** if the body sensation is the real driver and the thought is secondary → urge surfing (1). If the thought is true-ish and the discomfort underneath needs room → Expansion (`acceptance.md` 1).

## 3 · Choice Point / BOLD

**Reach for it when:** a decision is live right now and they want a fast in-the-moment drill rather than a sit-down exercise. Sorts the next action into a **toward-move** (in the direction of values) or an **away-move** (escaping the inner state).

**Walk it through:**
1. Name the hook and the fork. Ask: "What's pulling at you, and what are the two moves — the one toward what matters and the one away from it?"
2. Run **BOLD**: **B**reathe (one slow breath), **O**bserve (the urge, the thought, the feeling, without acting), **L**isten to values (what matters here), **D**ecide (pick the toward-move). Ask: "Run those four — what does the toward-move look like in the next five minutes?"

**If it stalls:** can't find the toward-move because everything feels like escape → values (4). Too activated to run BOLD at all → Dropping anchor (`present-moment.md` 4) first.

---

## 4 · Values as the why

**Reach for it when:** willingness keeps collapsing because the cost of riding out cravings has no clear point behind it. The toward-moves need a destination.

**Walk it through:** clarify the life being moved toward with `values.md` (bull's-eye or values card sort), then build concrete toward-moves with `committed-action.md` (SMART-ACT, willingness-to-act ratings). The recovery isn't *from* something; it's *toward* a life worth the discomfort. Ask: "What does the craving keep stealing from — and what would moving toward it look like this week?"

**If it stalls:** values feel hollow or unreachable → keep the step tiny and concrete; vitality usually shows up in the doing, not before it. Barriers are fused fears → `committed-action.md` 3 (FEAR/DARE).
