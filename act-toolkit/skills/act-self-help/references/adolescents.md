## How to use this file

This is the adolescent layer, framed as **DNA-V** — the developmentally adapted, younger-accessible version of the hexaflex for roughly ages 12–24. It is a **reframing, not new processes**: the four DNA-V elements map onto the same six processes the rest of the skill uses, just in concrete, non-clinical language. Keep the words plain with younger users — skill names like "self-as-context" stay behind the scenes; the moves underneath are identical. Use this file to translate, then point to the process files for the actual walk-throughs. Assumes the model in `hexaflex.md`; deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one step per turn, end on a question, wait.**

---

## 1 · D — Discoverer

**Reach for it when:** they're stuck, avoiding, or only ever doing the safe known thing. The Discoverer is the part that **tries stuff out** — trial-and-error, willing to be uncomfortable to find out what works. Maps to committed action + acceptance.

**Walk it through:** build a small experiment with `committed-action.md` (SMART-ACT, willingness-to-act rating) and make room for the discomfort that comes with trying via `acceptance.md` (urge surfing, expansion). Frame it as *experimenting*, not *succeeding*. Ask: "What's one new thing you could try out and just see what happens — even if it feels a bit awkward?"

**If it stalls:** if the worry-voice keeps blocking the experiment → Advisor (3). If there's no reason to try → Values & Vitality (4).

---

## 2 · N — Noticer

**Reach for it when:** they're swept up — in a feeling, a craving, a spinning thought — and not aware it's happening. The Noticer is the part that **pays attention on purpose** to body, feelings, and the world right now. Maps to present-moment awareness.

**Walk it through:** use `present-moment.md` (five senses, dropping anchor, mindful breathing) in short, concrete doses. Keep it grounded and brief — noticing what's actually here, not a long meditation. Ask: "Right now, what can you notice in your body and around you that you weren't paying attention to a second ago?"

**If it stalls:** if a thought keeps grabbing them back → Advisor (3). If a feeling is too big to just notice → make room for it with `acceptance.md`.

---

## 3 · A — Advisor

**Reach for it when:** the **rule-making, judging, worrying voice in the head** is running the show — "you'll mess it up," "everyone's looking," "don't even try." The Advisor is useful but bossy; it's the same target adult ACT calls fusion. Maps to defusion.

**Walk it through:** use `defusion.md` (labelling, leaves on a stream, naming the story) to put distance between the teen and the Advisor's lines — the Advisor *gives advice*, it doesn't *give orders*, and you get to decide whether to take it. Ask: "If that's just your Advisor talking — useful sometimes, wrong sometimes — do you have to do what it says?"

**If it stalls:** if the worry is about something real and solvable → it might be good advice; check it with Values & Vitality (4). Never use the silly-voice move (`defusion.md` 4) where the content is trauma or shame.

---

## 4 · V — Values & Vitality

**Reach for it when:** there's no direction, or everything feels pointless — the overarching *why* under all the rest. Values & Vitality is what matters and the aliveness that comes from moving toward it.

**Walk it through:** clarify direction with `values.md` (values card sort, bull's-eye) in concrete, everyday terms — the kind of friend, teammate, or person they want to be — then loop back to the Discoverer (1) to take a step. Ask: "What kind of person do you actually want to be — and what's one small move in that direction?"

**If it stalls:** if values feel fake or imposed → make them specific and theirs, not what adults want; vitality shows up in the doing. If they can't act on them → Discoverer (1) with a smaller step.
