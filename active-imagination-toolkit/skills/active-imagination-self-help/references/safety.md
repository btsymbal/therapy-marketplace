## Read this first, every session

Before routing, and continuously as material can escalate mid-session, scan for the
contraindications and crisis triggers below. Most of the time, for a psychologically stable
person bringing a dream image, an inner figure, or a charged mood, this practice is within
scope and the skill should help. The flags below are the exceptions — but they matter more here
than in ordinary cognitive self-help, because active imagination deliberately lowers the ego's
guard and engages autonomous material that can flood an unprepared psyche.

## This is not primarily a self-help technique

Be honest about the frame. Active imagination is a later-stage **analytic** intervention,
appropriate after groundwork has been laid that provides context for the material, assesses
readiness, and contains what exceeds the user's integration capacity. The IAAP's own writers
state it "cannot be read from a book" — it is an attitude transmitted in the analytic
relationship, not an instrumental technique. Self-directed practice is feasible **only** for
psychologically stable individuals with a stable life structure (see
`clinical-vs-self-guided.md`). When the prerequisites aren't there, the right move is to say so
and route to the safer alternative, not to run the practice anyway.

## The four absolute contraindications, do not proceed

If any of these is present, **do not run active imagination.** Name it plainly, explain why
briefly and without alarm, and offer the safer alternative.

1. **Active or latent psychosis.** Ego-lowering in a psychotic or latent-psychotic state removes
   the dual awareness that makes the practice therapeutic and produces unmediated identification
   with unconscious content — accelerating symptoms rather than helping. Even latent psychosis
   is a contraindication. → No active imagination; stabilization and professional care.

2. **Borderline ego-structure without stabilization.** Where object relations are unstable, the
   self/other boundary is permeable, and intense affect overwhelms the reflective function, the
   practice activates exactly what can't yet be contained. The prerequisite is being "well-rooted
   in reality." → Safer: therapist-held image-making (the therapist holds the image rather than
   the user holding it alone), not solo practice.

3. **Active infantile-affect states (unprocessed early-developmental trauma).** When raw
   preverbal material is live, the practice produces not symbolizable imagery but raw affect
   flooding the ego cannot contain. Jung's own caveat: suitable "only if infantile affects were
   not active." → Safer: sandplay with a trained therapist present, or structured art therapy;
   build ego-structure first.

4. **Disrupted symbolic capacity / concrete thinking.** The practice requires holding an image
   "as if" it were real-but-not-real. Where trauma or developmental disruption has impaired
   symbolic capacity, that "as if" can't be met. → Build symbolic capacity first; sandplay is the
   documented bridge modality.

A note on DID: it is **not** an absolute contraindication in all circumstances, but it requires
extensive scaffolding, therapist presence, and sustained clinical support. Solo active
imagination with DID is contraindicated.

## Situational contraindications

Even without one of the four, do not open the unconscious when:

- **Acute emotional crisis** of any kind — stabilization precedes exploration.
- **Severe dissociation / permeable ego boundaries** right now — at most movement with
  relational scaffolding, or sandplay; not solo visual or verbal practice.
- **Substance intoxication** — the ego-complex isn't engaged; abstain, resume only when sober.
- **Early-stage / no analytic groundwork** and the material is heavy (deep shadow, anima/animus,
  numinous) — the images can't yet be contextualized; keep it light or defer.
- **Grief with the dead as the figure** done solo — carries a documented dissociation risk; the
  contained form is shared/relational, with another person present (`clinical-presentations.md`).

## Crisis triggers, stop and escalate

If any of these appear, **stop the practice** and shift to support and signposting. Do not run
imagination on active suicidality.

- Any mention of suicide, wanting to not exist, self-harm, or feeling there's no point going on,
  **including indirect / passive forms** ("everyone'd be better off without me", "I wouldn't mind
  not waking up", "tired of being here"). Watch for these in whatever language the user writes in.
- Plans or means to hurt themselves, or being at risk right now.
- Severe, immobilizing depression, or talk of harming someone else.
- Acute crisis where they can't keep themselves safe.

What to do: respond like a person, not a protocol. Acknowledge it directly and without panic.
Make clear this is past what self-help should carry alone and that reaching a human matters more
than any inner-work technique. **Match their language** — never switch them to English to read
the script. Then surface help, localized (next section). If the risk is toward someone else,
same calm, but the move is de-escalation and emergency services now, not a crisis line.

## Surfacing crisis help, localized

Crisis numbers are country-specific and change, so don't recite one from memory.

- **Use what's on record.** If the user's country / region is in `CLAUDE.md`, lead with the
  resources recorded there (local emergency number + a verified local crisis line). If location
  isn't recorded, ask once, briefly, where they are (or infer it), then continue.
- **Verify before you give a number.** Web-search the current crisis line for their country rather
  than reciting one, then record the verified number(s) in `CLAUDE.md` for next time.
  [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com) covers most countries and is a safe universal
  fallback.
- **Give the universal emergency number** for their region as the immediate-danger route: 112
  across the EU and much of the world, 911 in the US / Canada, 999 in the UK, 000 in Australia, or
  the local equivalent.
- **Point to a human they know** — therapist, doctor, someone they trust today; if `CLAUDE.md`
  names a specific clinician, name them.

## The three in-session failure modes

All three share one cause: **loss of the "as if" stance and its dual awareness.** Keep them live
the whole session.

**Dissolution (overwhelm by the unconscious).** Recognition: a shift from dual-awareness
participation to feeling swallowed, flooded, unable to hold any observing position; imagery
becomes compulsive rather than symbolic; they can't exit voluntarily. **Corrective: exit
immediately.** Do not "work through" it. Run the grounding protocol below.

**Domination (scripting the imagery).** Recognition: the figures say exactly what the ego
expects; no surprise; it reads like a script already composed; the material flatters or merely
confirms. This isn't active imagination — it's fantasy. **Corrective:** stop; ask the most
genuinely open question possible; fall completely silent and wait. If nothing autonomous comes,
end and suggest discussing with an analyst.

**Inflation (identifying with an archetypal figure).** Recognition: they begin to feel they
*are* a powerful / wise / divine figure, or that it specially chose them, or that what it said
carries universal authority; post-session behavior turns grandiose, messianic, or withdrawn.
**Corrective:** the ethical-confrontation step (Phase 4) and the concrete act return them from
archetypal identification to specific personal obligation. If inflation persists across sessions,
that's a signal for analytic consultation, not for more practice.

## The exit and grounding protocol

Use this whenever any failure mode appears or the material becomes overwhelming. (Note: no Tier-1
clinical source for this exact protocol exists; it rests on a single Tier-3 source. Use it as
sound first aid, not a validated clinical procedure.)

1. Open the eyes immediately.
2. Feel the weight of the body in the chair or on the floor.
3. Name five objects in the room, aloud.
4. Stand, if possible, and walk several steps.
5. Breathe deliberately for ~30 seconds.
6. Do a brief ordinary task: make tea, wash hands, straighten the room.
7. Write only the minimum note: "Session ended early — [brief descriptor]." **Do not** try to
   process or analyze the overwhelming material alone.

## When a session goes persistently wrong

Persistent stalling, persistent flooding, or persistent inflation **across** sessions are signals
that the prerequisites aren't yet in place — **not** signals to intensify the practice. Stop
practicing and consult a Jungian analyst or depth-oriented therapist. For a self-practitioner,
transitioning to working with an analyst is the correct next step, not a failure.

## Safer fallback options by situation

| Situation | Safer alternative |
|---|---|
| Active imagination contraindicated (any cause) | Sandplay with a trained therapist |
| Borderline ego-structure | Therapist-held image-making (therapist holds the image) |
| Preverbal / somatic trauma | Embodied AI / Authentic Movement with an analyst or trained witness present |
| Persistent ego dissolution | Grounding, somatic therapy, or structured emotion-regulation first |
| Persistent inflation | Immediate analytic consultation |
| Self-practice stalling repeatedly | Transition to working with a Jungian analyst |

## Evidence limits, stated plainly

Don't overclaim. Jungian psychotherapy as a *whole* has naturalistic/retrospective support with
moderate-to-large effect sizes, but **no RCT of active imagination as a standalone intervention
exists**, and EMDR / CBT / certain exposure therapies have a substantially stronger trial base.
Within this catalog, the **music** modality and the **exit/grounding protocol** rest on the
thinnest sources (Tier 2-3); flag that when relevant rather than presenting them as settled. The
Jungian literature itself acknowledges this gap.
