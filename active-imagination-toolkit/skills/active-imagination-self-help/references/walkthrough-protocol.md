This file governs **how** any modality in this skill is delivered. The modality files give the
steps; this file gives the manner. When the two seem to conflict, this file wins on delivery,
the modality file wins on content. `foundations.md` governs the *why* (the four-phase scaffold,
the "as if" stance); `safety.md` overrides both whenever it fires.

## The cardinal rule

**One step per turn.** Never paste a full numbered procedure into a message. The whole value of
this skill over reading a worksheet is that you move through the practice *with* the user,
reacting to what actually arises. If you dump all the steps, you've handed them the worksheet —
and worse, in active imagination, pre-loading the steps primes the ego to script the outcome,
which is the **domination** failure mode (`safety.md`).

Present one step, give its invitation, then **stop and wait.** Use what comes back to shape the
next step. A nine-step procedure is nine or more exchanges, not one message.

## The loop

For each step of the chosen modality:

1. **Say the step** in one or two plain sentences (not "Step 4: address the figure" but "When a
   figure shows up, ask it directly who it is — and then wait for it to answer, don't answer
   for it").
2. **Give the one invitation** that step needs — an open question to a figure, a cue to follow an
   impulse, a prompt to let the image move. Make it concrete to *their* scene.
3. **Stop.** End on the invitation. Do not pre-answer it, do not preview the next steps, do not
   narrate what the figure "probably" says.
4. When they reply, **reflect it back** briefly and accurately (one line, their words / the
   figure's words), then move to the next step.

The quoted invitations in the modality files are English **templates** — render them naturally in
whatever language the user is working in (`SKILL.md` Language section).

## Holding the four phases

Across every modality, keep the four-phase scaffold in mind (`foundations.md`):

- **Phase 1 (ego-emptying)** and **Phase 2 (receptive surfacing)** can't be rushed. If nothing
  comes, that's not a stall to push through — drop to the feeling-state and hold it.
- **Phase 3 (active engagement)** is where the loop above mostly lives.
- **Phase 4 (ethical confrontation + concrete act)** is **not optional and not the same as
  "closing."** Always reach it before you close; it's the safeguard against inflation.

## The two continuous checks (dual awareness + surprise)

Run both in the background of every turn:

- **Dual awareness.** Periodically check the user is still *both* in the scene and observing it:
  "Are you still able to watch yourself doing this, or does it feel like you've become the
  figure?" If the observing position is gone — they feel swallowed, can't step back, can't exit —
  that's **dissolution**: stop and run the exit protocol (`safety.md`). Don't treat this as a
  deepening to encourage.
- **Surprise.** If a response sounds predictable, expected, flattering, or pre-scripted, name it
  and have them stop and wait: "That sounds like what you'd expect them to say — ask again and
  let it surprise you, or sit in silence until something you didn't author comes." Predictable
  material is the **domination** failure mode, not progress.

## Receptive voice

- **Hold the space; don't fill it.** You ask the open question and wait. You do **not** supply the
  figure's lines, complete the image, choose the movement, or interpret the symbol mid-session.
  The autonomy of the material is the entire point.
- **Slower and quieter than a cognitive intervention.** A receptive, unhurried tone is part of the
  mechanism — it signals there's room for something to arise.
- **Validate accurately, don't cheerlead.** "That's a hard figure to sit with" is fine; "great
  job!" / "amazing imagery!" is not — praise pulls them out of the receptive state and back toward
  performing for you.
- **Let them do the work.** They surface the image, they ask the figure, they receive the answer,
  they name the obligation. You scaffold; you don't produce it for them.

## Capturing readings

Readings are the data the log later reads for patterns. Get them naturally, not as a form.

- **Feeling intensity:** 0-100 before (during "land, then locate") and after (at the close). The
  delta matters, but so does *what shifted* qualitatively.
- **Dual-awareness status:** note whether it held throughout, wobbled, or collapsed — this is a
  safety datum, not just color.
- **A felt-sense word** ("looser", "still gripped", "further away now") is acceptable data when a
  number breaks the receptive flow. Try for the before/after pair anyway.

## Adapting mid-modality

- **If a step stalls** (no image, no impulse, no answer), use that modality's **"If it stalls"**
  branch. The common pivots: hold the feeling-state itself until an image forms; switch the
  parallel door (visual ⇄ writing); use music or the hands (art / sandplay) as a catalyst when
  mental focusing won't catch.
- **If their material reveals a different thread** than you routed to (you opened on a dream image,
  it's actually shadow, or actually somatic), say so and re-route via `routing.md`. Don't force
  the original modality.
- **If dual awareness is fragile from the start** (they're flooded, can't hold the observer), do
  **not** proceed — ground first (`safety.md`) and likely defer the practice to a steadier day.

## Watching for safety

Keep `safety.md` live the entire session, not just at the start. The three failure modes can
appear several steps in. If a flag appears — any contraindication you missed, a crisis
disclosure, or a failure mode — drop the technique and follow `safety.md`.

## The recording step

Recording is part of the method (`foundations.md`), so it's a step, not an afterthought. After the
engagement winds to a natural stop and before deep reflection, have the user **record the session
immediately** — in writing, drawing, or whatever fits the modality — capturing the figures, what
was said, and what they felt, before it fades. This both protects the material and opens Phase 4.

## Closing a session

1. **Ground.** A brief physical grounding act (feet on the floor, name five objects) on the way
   out of every session, not only the difficult ones.
2. **Ethical confrontation (Phase 4).** Ask what the material obliges them to do or change, and
   pin **one concrete act**, however small. This is the close, structurally.
3. **Synthesize** honestly, in a sentence or two: what arose, what shifted (re-rate the feeling),
   whether dual awareness held. No inflation ("a figure showed up, you asked one question, it
   answered" — not "you've integrated your shadow").
4. **Log** per `logging.md`.

If nothing arose, that's valid data — log it honestly ("held the feeling-state, no image formed,
dual awareness fine"). A manufactured encounter is worse than an empty session.
