## How to use this file

These are **DSM-adjacent presentations** where active imagination has documented clinical use — but
each carries gating that the classic indications (`indications.md`) don't. This file tells you **what
is self-help-safe and what wants a clinician**; the procedures live in the modality files, and
`safety.md` overrides everything here. Active imagination is not a substitute for evidence-based
treatment of any of these conditions, and several have a far stronger trial base in other therapies
(EMDR, CBT, exposure) — say so honestly (`safety.md`, evidence limits).

## The gating principle

The deeper and earlier the wound, and the more the ego-structure is compromised, the more the work
needs a clinician's relational presence — up to and including the four absolute contraindications,
where you don't run it at all (`safety.md`). For a clinical presentation, the safe self-help posture
is: deliver psychoeducation and the gentlest externalizing layer, watch dual awareness closely, and
route the heavier work to a professional rather than running it solo.

## Depression (non-acute)

The Jungian frame treats a depressed mood as **prima materia** — raw material from the unconscious,
not pure pathology — and works by externalizing the mood state and entering dialogue with "the figure
of the depression itself." **Modality:** writing (`writing.md`) or visual art (`art.md`).
**Gating:** ego must be functional; **not** during acute suicidal crisis (→ `safety.md`, crisis
triggers). Set expectations honestly: Jungian therapy as a whole shows real effect sizes for
depression, but that's the whole treatment, not active imagination alone, and this is not a
replacement for evidence-based depression care.

## Anxiety with intrusive thoughts

For **ego-intact** users, the active-imagination move is to **enter the anxiety's imagery** — going
*into* the disturbance rather than away from it — and personify and dialogue with its figures.
**Modality:** visual (`visual.md`) or writing (`writing.md`). **Gating:** readiness assessment
matters; this is **contraindicated in acute panic states or where ego boundaries are permeable** (→
`safety.md`). If they're flooded, ground first and don't open the unconscious.

## Grief and bereavement

**Important risk:** solo active imagination with **figures of the dead** carries a specifically
documented dissociation risk (from Jung's own accounts). The documented clinical approach is the
**shared imaginal reverie** — analyst and patient entering an imaginal space **together**, co-present,
rather than the patient engaging the deceased figure alone. **Self-help posture:** do **not** run
solo dialogue with the dead. If grief is the material, keep to externalizing the *feeling* (art,
writing about the loss) rather than summoning the figure, and route the relational imaginal work to a
therapist or a trusted person present (`safety.md`, situational contraindications).

## Trauma — somatic and image-based intrusions

**Clinician-gated.** Two documented forms, both wanting professional presence:
- **Trauma with preserved symbolic capacity:** image-making (`art.md`) **then** verbal dialogue
  (`writing.md`). The **therapist-held image-making** variant — therapist holds the image for a
  patient with early trauma and insufficient ego-structure — is a clinician modality, not self-help.
- **Complex / preverbal / collective trauma** encoded in implicit somatic memory: **embodied active
  imagination** / authentic movement (`movement.md`), because gesture bridges implicit memory and
  emergent imagery — but with a **trained witness or analyst present**.

For **both**, the analyst's relational presence is essential and **solo active imagination is
contraindicated in active trauma states.** No active dissociation; symbolic capacity must be intact.
The self-help layer is **stabilization and grounding only** (`safety.md`); the processing work is a
clinician's.

## What stays out of scope

Conditions with no condition-specific active-imagination evidence and clear treatment elsewhere —
OCD, bipolar, eating disorders, substance-use disorders, psychosis (an absolute contraindication).
Don't improvise an active-imagination protocol for these. Give honest framing, hold the safety line,
and signpost specialist assessment (`safety.md`).
