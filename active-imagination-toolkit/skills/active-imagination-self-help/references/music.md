## How to use this file

**Music** is the first-line modality for auditory types — those who, in Jung's description, hear
"words or fragments of apparently meaningless sentences" rather than seeing images — and a useful
**catalyst** when strong affect won't produce visual imagery or other modalities have stalled. Two
forms: **expressive** (the user sounds) and **receptive** (the user listens). Rests on
`foundations.md`; delivered per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: one step per turn, wait. Keep `safety.md`
live.

## Evidence caveat, state it

Be honest here: **music is the least procedurally documented modality** in the Jungian-specific
literature. No Tier-1 source specifying a Jungian music active-imagination protocol exists; the
steps below rest on a single analyst's account (Tier 2) and general practitioner sources. Note: the
**Bonny Method of Guided Imagery and Music (GIM)** has affinities with active imagination but is a
distinct certified modality with a directive guide structure that active imagination does *not* use —
it is not a substitute here. Run the procedures below as a reasonable practice, not a settled one,
and weight that accordingly (`safety.md`, evidence limits).

## 1 · Expressive / improvisational (sounding)

**When & where it works best:** Auditory types; strong affect that won't make an image; as an entry
catalyst when other modalities stall. The point is to follow emotion into sound, not to make
"music."

**Walk it through (one step per turn):**
1. **Choose a low-pressure instrument.** A drum, cymbal, tambourine, shaker, xylophone — any simple
   percussion; voice or breath works if there's no instrument. Ask: "What can you make sound with,
   with no worry about playing it 'well'?"
2. **Enter the inward state and anchor.** Dual-awareness anchor (`foundations.md`). Ask: "Settle,
   and bring a feeling-state or theme to mind — not to depict it, just to hold it. Ready?"
3. **Begin sounding without a plan (Phase 2→3).** Ask: "Start making sound, following the impulse
   moment to moment. There's no 'right' sound. Follow the emotion into it — what comes?"
4. **Continue without interruption.** Ask: "Let it go where it goes; don't shape it into a tune.
   Where does the sound take the feeling?"
5. **Check dual awareness**, as in any modality. If it floods, stop and ground (`safety.md`).
6. **Let it end naturally**, then sit in silence for several minutes. Ask: "Let it come to its own
   end, then just sit. What's present now?"
7. **Move to words.** Ask: "Now that the emotion's been in sound, see if words come more easily —
   what do you want to say about what happened?" (Discharging affect in sound often frees words.)
8. **Record (writing or drawing) and apply Phase 4 (`logging.md`).** Ask: "What does this ask of you?
   One concrete thing."

## 2 · Receptive (listening)

**When & where it works best:** When the user would rather receive than produce; using personally
resonant music as a field for images, feelings, and memories to arise against.

**Walk it through (one step per turn):**
1. **Choose resonant music** and settle. Ask: "Pick something personally significant or emotionally
   charged, lie or sit comfortably, quiet space. Ready?"
2. **Anchor and listen fully.** Dual-awareness anchor; eyes closed. Ask: "Listen with full attention
   — not as background, as a field you're entering. Begin."
3. **Attend to what arises (Phase 2→3).** Ask: "What images, feelings, memories, or body sensations
   come up in response to the music? Let them come — what's there?"
4. **At a natural end, sit in silence** before moving. Ask: "Let it finish, then stay still a moment.
   What's left?"
5. **Process and record.** Verbally or in writing; capture what arose; apply Phase 4 (`logging.md`).

## If it doesn't work, try

- **No images arise during receptive listening:** switch to the **expressive** form — active sounding
  often bypasses a blocked visual imagination.
- **Expressive sounding also stalls:** return to the underlying feeling-state and sustain it while
  sounding without making structured music — the emotion itself is the raw material.
- **Music consistently fails to generate symbolic material:** switch to `art.md` — color-feeling as
  the entry, structurally similar to sound-feeling.
- **It floods or destabilizes dual awareness:** stop and ground (`safety.md`).
