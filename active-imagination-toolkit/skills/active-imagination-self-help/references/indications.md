## How to use this file

These are the **classic Jungian indications** — the situations active imagination was developed to
meet. This file is the *why and the readiness*; the actual procedures live in the modality files
(`visual.md`, `writing.md`, `movement.md`, `art.md`, `music.md`, `sandplay.md`), and `routing.md`
maps each indication to its first-line modality. Everything here assumes the safety gate
(`safety.md`) has been passed and the "as if" stance is anchored (`foundations.md`). Several of these
carry an elevated **identification / inflation risk**; where noted, analyst guidance is strongly
advised and the self-help layer should stay correspondingly humble.

## Individuation impasse

When the conscious/unconscious conflict becomes open and critical and "can no longer be concealed
behind a mask" — the persona's adaptive strategies have run out. At this stage the symbols arriving
carry a **teleological charge**: they point forward, toward what the person needs to become, not
backward toward what needs explaining. **Modality:** any matched to psychological type
(`routing.md`). **Readiness:** a well-structured ego in mid-to-late work; without that groundwork the
images can't be contextualized — keep it light or defer (`clinical-vs-self-guided.md`).

## Shadow work

The shadow — refused, rejected, or undeveloped parts of the personality — typically appears as a
**same-sex figure with unwanted qualities**. The ego must be able to acknowledge the shadow's reality
**without fusing with it** (acting it out) or **annihilating it** (refusing engagement). **Modality:**
written dialogue (`writing.md`) or visual confrontation (`visual.md`) are the most documented. Hold
the surprise test closely here — the shadow's value is precisely that it says what the ego wouldn't.
Phase 4 matters acutely: the obligation is usually to own something, not to enact it.

## Anima / animus work

The anima (in men) and animus (in women) appear as **opposite-sex figures**, typically tied to mood
disturbance, projections onto a partner, and relational distortion. The work is to give the figure a
face and enter dialogue (`writing.md` / `visual.md`). **This carries a specifically elevated
identification risk** — these figures can be numinous and seductive, and dual awareness is harder to
hold toward them. Analyst guidance is strongly recommended; in self-practice, keep the dual-awareness
checks frequent and stop early if the figure starts to "take over" (`safety.md`, inflation /
dissolution).

## Dream drought

When dreams have **stopped** — which may signal the unconscious compensating for an over-defended
conscious attitude — active imagination provides a way to reach symbolic material without dream
content. **Modality:** the inner-cinema mode (`visual.md`) or music as a catalyst (`music.md`). This
is more appropriate than forcing analysis onto sparse dream material.

## Recurring dreams and nightmares

Entering a recurring dream **from its ending point** and continuing it consciously — meeting the
pursuer, opening the locked door, facing the catastrophe with the ego present. **Modality:** visual
(`visual.md`, procedure 2) or writing (`writing.md`). **Readiness:** psychologically stable
individuals only; **not** during acute nightmare disorder, and if it's trauma-linked, this is
clinician-gated (`clinical-presentations.md`, `safety.md`).

## Spiritual emergency and numinous encounter

When a numinous experience — a vivid dream, an overwhelming synchronicity, a felt transpersonal
presence — threatens to overwhelm the ego, active imagination offers a structured container for
**engaging rather than being flooded by** it. **Modality:** any, to activate the transcendent
function (`foundations.md`). **The inflation risk here is specifically high** — numinous figures carry
archetypal power. An analyst as container is strongly recommended; in self-practice, the
ethical-confrontation step and a concrete, ordinary act are the essential counterweight, and
persistent inflation is a stop-and-consult signal (`safety.md`).

## Creative block

A block in creative work, met by engaging the unconscious through the user's own expressive domain.
**Modality:** match it to how they create — movement for a dancer/somatic worker (`movement.md`),
writing for a writer (`writing.md`), painting for a visual artist (`art.md`). The aim isn't to
"produce" but to let autonomous material move again; the product, if any, is a byproduct of the
encounter, not its goal.
