## How to use this file

This is the conceptual core. Every modality (`visual.md`, `writing.md`, `movement.md`,
`art.md`, `music.md`, `sandplay.md`) is an expression of the **four-phase scaffold** below,
held inside the **"as if" stance**. Read this at the start of a session; it precedes and
enables every technique. The four-phase scaffold and the "as if" stance are not optional
refinements — they are what makes active imagination distinct from visualization, meditation,
journaling, or daydreaming. A technique attempted without them is either ineffective
(ego-dominated scripting) or unsafe (loss of dual awareness). Deliver everything per
`walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one step per turn, end on an invitation, wait.**

## The "as if" stance and dual awareness

The single most important technical requirement. The practitioner holds **simultaneous
immersion in the inner scene and observation of it from a stable ego position** — fully in the
drama *and* watching it as if from a slight distance. Jung framed the practice as suitable only
when "infantile affects were not active," i.e. only when the ego can stay present and observing
rather than being submerged.

Loss of the "as if" stance — sliding from "I am speaking *to* this figure" into "I *am* this
figure" — is not a setback or a side effect. It is the operational definition of the technique
having gone wrong, and it is the common root of all three failure modes (`safety.md`). The
moment it collapses, you stop and ground; you do not push on.

## The dual-awareness anchor (do this before entering)

Before any session, have the user state, explicitly, in their own language:

> "I am [name]. I am in [location]. I am about to enter a space of imagination. I will return
> from this space, and I can exit at any moment."

This anchors the observing ego and is the operational safeguard for the double-awareness
requirement. Agree the **exit signal** up front (open the eyes, name five objects in the room —
the full protocol is in `safety.md`) so leaving is always available and never a defeat.

## The four-phase scaffold

Every modality follows this common architecture, whether it uses imagery, words, movement,
paint, sound, or sand:

**Phase 1 — Ego-emptying and invitation.** The user consciously lays aside the agenda of the
day and reaches **relaxed alertness** — neither drowsy nor analytically busy — and opens a space
for autonomous material to present itself. This is *not* meditation: the ego does not dissolve,
it pauses its directing activity and waits with intention.

**Phase 2 — Receptive image-surfacing.** Content emerges spontaneously: an image, a figure, a
feeling-state, a movement impulse, a fragment of sound. **The user does not manufacture it.** If
nothing comes, they hold the underlying feeling-state itself without forcing imagery — sustained
attention on the affect eventually produces symbolic material.

**Phase 3 — Active engagement.** The user enters genuine exchange with what appeared. In visual
and verbal modalities: question the figures and **wait for their answers** ("Who are you? What
do you want? Why are you here?"). In movement: follow the impulse wherever it leads. In painting:
continue the image where it wants to go. In music: sound without criteria. The test of genuine
engagement versus fantasy here is **surprise** (below).

**Phase 4 — Ethical confrontation and concrete integration.** This is what separates active
imagination from aesthetic fantasy or entertainment. The user asks: *what does this oblige me to
do or change in my waking life?* The integration step requires a **concrete act, however small** —
a decision made, a relationship addressed, a habitual stance revised. Without this phase, the
practice produces inflation rather than transformation. **Never skip it.**

## The surprise test

The hallmark of genuine unconscious material is that it **surprises the ego**. Anything
predictable, anything that merely confirms what the user already believes or flatters them, should
be treated as ego-manufactured (the **domination** failure mode, `safety.md`). When you notice it,
have them stop, ask the most genuinely open question they can, and fall silent and wait. If nothing
autonomous comes after sustained waiting, end the session rather than scripting a satisfying answer.

## The transcendent function

Active imagination works by activating what Jung called the transcendent function — the psyche's
capacity to generate a synthesis between conscious and unconscious positions that neither could
reach alone. It is not mystical; it is the self-regulating movement of the psyche when the tension
between opposites is **held without premature resolution**. Active imagination is the main
voluntary method for engaging it — but only when the ego is strong enough to bear the tension. If
the user can't tolerate the tension (they collapse it into a tidy answer, or get flooded), that's a
signal to step back, not to push.

## The role of recording

Recording the session — in writing, drawing, or painting — is part of the method, not an
afterthought. It protects the material from fading back into the unconscious, makes it available for
later reflection, and **initiates the ethical confrontation of Phase 4 by objectifying what was
experienced.** This is why `logging.md` is load-bearing here rather than optional bookkeeping: a
session that isn't recorded is a session whose material is largely lost.

## Why the ethical-confrontation step matters

The central risk of sustained practice **without** Phase 4 is **psychic inflation**: the user
identifies with archetypal figures or their elevated qualities (wisdom, power, divine sanction) and
begins acting from that identification in ordinary life. The ethical-confrontation step and the
concrete integration act are the structural safeguards — they return the user from archetypal
identification to a specific, ordinary, personal obligation. Insight that doesn't land as a concrete
change is exactly the condition where inflation grows. See `safety.md` for inflation as a failure
mode and what to do if it persists.
