## How to use this file

**Writing / inner dialogue** is the first-line modality for auditory-verbal types and for people
whose unconscious shows up as inner voices, verbal fragments, or complex-driven rumination. For
some, writing is a gentler entry than the visual form. It rests on `foundations.md` and is delivered
per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one step per turn, end on an invitation, wait.** Keep `safety.md`
live throughout.

**The specific risk of this modality:** the **fruitless soliloquy.** Complex-driven repetitive
writing can mimic genuine dialogue — it goes in circles, produces no surprise, and *feels* like
work without being it. The surprise test (`foundations.md`) is the guard. This is the modality where
ego-domination is easiest to miss, so watch it closely.

## 1 · Inner dialogue (the core writing procedure)

**When & where it works best:** A strong emotion with no clear object, an inner-voice fragment, a
dream figure that spoke memorable words, or a complex-episode generating repetitive thoughts.

**Walk it through (one step per turn):**
1. **Set up the space.** A dedicated journal or a private document. Ask: "Open a page you'll keep
   just for this — not personal journaling, a space for the figure to speak. Ready?"
2. **Enter the invitation state and anchor.** Do the dual-awareness anchor (`foundations.md`);
   relaxed alertness; eyes may stay open for writing. Ask: "Settled, and can you say the anchor?"
3. **Personify the content (Phase 2).** Ask: "Put a question to it — 'Who is the one inside me who
   feels this way?' or 'Who's speaking when I hear this voice?' Write the question down, then stop
   and wait."
4. **Begin the dialogue (Phase 3).** Ask them to write in **script format**, labeling the two voices
   distinctly (e.g. ME / FIGURE, or different cases). Ask: "Write your opening — 'Who are you? What
   do you want?' — then write only what spontaneously comes back. Don't compose or polish it. What
   does it say?"
5. **Run the surprise test.** If the reply is predictable or circular, name it: "That reads like the
   answer you'd expect — that's the complex talking, not the figure. Stop, sit in silence, and write
   only when something you didn't author offers itself."
6. **Check dual awareness.** Ask: "Are you still writing *to* it, or has it taken you over?" If the
   observer is gone → exit protocol (`safety.md`).
7. **Continue without censoring.** Ask: "Let it say what it came to say — don't redirect or tidy it.
   Where does it go?" Don't conclude prematurely.
8. **Natural stop; close the page; ground.** Brief physical grounding; note the date.
9. **Ethical confrontation (Phase 4).** In a separate section, ask: "What does this dialogue oblige
   you to do or change? Name one concrete act." Then record/log (`logging.md`).

## 2 · Sub-form — automatic writing

The hand moves without conscious direction, producing text that surprises the writer — the
verbal counterpart to spontaneous visual emergence. **Higher dissolution risk; not a first
approach.** Only offer it to a steady, experienced user, keep the dual-awareness anchor explicit,
and stop at the first sign the observer is slipping (`safety.md`).

## 3 · Indirect approach — fiction writing

**When & where it works best:** For people who can't enter the unconscious directly — writing a
story apparently about *other* characters. Unconscious material projects into the fictional figures
and can be worked with obliquely. A gentler door when direct dialogue feels impossible.

**Walk it through (one step per turn):**
1. Ask: "Start a short scene with a character who isn't 'you'. Who are they, where are they?"
2. Ask: "Let the scene unfold on its own — don't plan it. What does the character do or say that you
   didn't decide in advance?" (Watch for the same surprise marker.)
3. When a figure in the fiction comes alive, gently bridge: "Does anyone in this scene feel familiar
   — like they know something about you? You can speak to them, still inside the story."
4. Ground, record, and apply Phase 4 as above — what did the story show, and what does it ask of
   them?

## If it doesn't work, try

- **The figure says nothing, or the dialogue feels scripted (no surprise):** stop writing entirely.
  Return to the underlying feeling-state and sit in silence for several minutes. Resume only when
  something autonomous offers itself. Predictable repetition = **fruitless soliloquy / domination**
  (`safety.md`); set it aside, don't push.
- **Consistent repetition over a session:** switch to `visual.md`, or to `art.md`, where image-making
  bypasses verbal defenses.
- **Overwhelm / dual awareness slips:** exit immediately (`safety.md`).
- **It keeps stalling across sessions:** a readiness signal — see `clinical-vs-self-guided.md`.
