## How to route

The user won't name a modality — they'll bring a dream image, an inner figure, a recurring
nightmare, a mood with no object, a sense of being stuck. Your job: from what they bring, plus
how they're naturally wired (do they think in pictures, words, movement, sound?), pick **one**
modality to start and open its file. Re-route freely if their material points elsewhere.

## First: safety and stance

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) first — the four absolute contraindications and crisis
flags gate everything here. Then get a feeling + 0-100 intensity + a concrete entry point, and
anchor the "as if" stance (`foundations.md`, `SKILL.md` flow steps 1-3) before entering any
modality.

## Match the modality to the person (psychological type)

A real lever in Jungian practice: route to the channel the user's unconscious actually uses.

- **Visual / sees images** → `visual.md` (inner theater) or `art.md` (painting / mandala).
- **Auditory-verbal / hears voices, words, inner sentences** → `writing.md` (inner dialogue) or
  `music.md`.
- **Kinesthetic / feels it in the body, somatic symptoms, movement impulses** → `movement.md`.
- **Words fail / pre-symbolic / needs the hands** → `sandplay.md` or `art.md`.

When unsure, the **writing** and **visual** modalities are the most accessible for self-practice;
**movement** benefits from relational holding, **music** needs more specialized setup, and
**sandplay** classically assumes a trained therapist present (run the self-directed adaptation in
`sandplay.md` with that caveat named).

## Front door: what the user brings → modality + file

| What they bring | Start with | File |
|---|---|---|
| A vivid dream image or fantasy figure they want to meet | Visual / inner theater | `visual.md` |
| An inner voice / a figure that spoke / verbal rumination they want to dialogue with | Writing / inner dialogue | `writing.md` |
| Body sensations, movement impulses, psychosomatic pull, "it's in my body" | Movement / authentic movement | `movement.md` |
| Imagery present but dialogue feels premature; strong verbal defenses | Visual art / painting / mandala | `art.md` |
| "How am I right now?", wanting a check on the state of the self, a session closer | Mandala | `art.md` |
| Hears music / fragments rather than seeing; affect that won't make an image | Music | `music.md` |
| Words fail to reach it; needs the tactile; very early-stage | Sandplay (self-directed adaptation) | `sandplay.md` |
| A recurring dream or nightmare to continue from its ending | Visual or writing | `visual.md` / `writing.md` |
| Dreams have stopped (dream drought) | Visual (inner cinema) or music | `visual.md` / `music.md` |

Ported from the catalog's condition map. **Match to psychological type** where it says "any."

| Situation | First-line modality | Notes / file |
|---|---|---|
| Individuation impasse (conscious/unconscious conflict can no longer be concealed) | Any, matched to type | Late-stage; well-structured ego required. `indications.md` |
| Shadow integration (disowned aspects surfacing) | Writing or visual | Ego must tolerate the shadow's reality without acting it out. `indications.md` |
| Anima/animus activation (mood swings, projections, relational distortion) | Writing or visual (give the figure a face) | Identification risk; analyst guidance strongly advised. `indications.md` |
| Depression, non-acute, ego functional | Writing or art (externalize the mood) | Not in acute suicidal crisis. `clinical-presentations.md` |
| Anxiety with intrusive thoughts, ego-intact | Visual or writing (enter the disturbance) | Readiness assessment matters. `clinical-presentations.md` |
| Trauma with preserved symbolic capacity | Art (image-making) then writing | No active dissociation. Clinician-gated. `clinical-presentations.md` |
| Complex / preverbal / collective trauma | Movement (embodied) | Trained witness present; relational holding. `clinical-presentations.md` |

| Existential crisis / meaninglessness | Any, matched to type | Needs stable identity and psychic resources. `indications.md` |
| Creative block | Movement, writing, or art | Match to the person's expressive domain. `indications.md` |

## Contraindicated conditions, do not route into a technique

If any of these is live, do **not** route to a modality — go to `safety.md` and the safer
alternative.

| Contraindicated | Safer alternative |
|---|---|
| Active or latent psychosis | Stabilization only; no AI; no sandplay without specialist oversight |
| Unstabilized borderline ego-structure | Therapist-held image-making |
| Active infantile-affect states | Sandplay (therapist present) or structured art therapy |
| Disrupted symbolic capacity / concrete thinking | Build symbolic capacity first; sandplay as entry |
| Acute emotional crisis | Supportive grounding; not active imagination |
| Severe dissociation / permeable boundaries | Movement with relational scaffolding only, or sandplay |
| Substance intoxication | Abstain; resume only when sober |
| Early-stage / no analytic groundwork (heavy material) | Sandplay is available from session one; or keep it light / defer |

## Multi-layer and re-routing

- Most sessions are one entry point; some are layered (a dream image that's clearly shadow; a
  mood that turns out to be anima/animus). Work one thread at a time, the most charged first.
  Name the others and offer to return.
- If a walkthrough reveals the real material is elsewhere (you started visual, but it's somatic
  and wants the body), say so plainly and switch files. Don't finish the wrong modality for
  tidiness.
- If a modality persistently stalls across attempts, the standard fallback chain is: visual ⇄
  writing as parallel doors; music as a catalyst for stalled imagery; art / sandplay when mental
  focusing won't catch and the hands need to lead. Each modality file carries its own "If it
  stalls" branch.
- If distress is too high to hold dual awareness, **don't** open the unconscious — ground first
  (`safety.md`) and likely defer.
