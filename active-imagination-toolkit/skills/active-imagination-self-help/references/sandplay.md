## How to use this file

**Sandplay** is structurally different from every other modality in two ways: classically the
**analyst is present throughout the creation**, and **no ego-lowering is required** — the user
doesn't need to reach a special state, only to touch the sand and begin. That makes it the
documented **first fallback when classical active imagination is contraindicated**, and a primary
modality for early-stage work, children, preverbal-trauma survivors, and anyone "for whom words fail
to get to the heart of the true experience." It's appropriate when the ego isn't strong enough to
face the unconscious directly, and it's available from the very first session. Rests on
`foundations.md`; delivered per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: one step per turn, wait.

## The honest caveat, read it first

In its proper form, sandplay assumes a **trained sandplay therapist** (ISST or equivalent) present
as container — that presence *is* much of the method, and it's why the modality can hold fragile
presentations safely. What follows is a **self-directed adaptation** the user can do alone with a
tray and figures; run it as that, and **name the caveat** to the user: the containing presence of a
trained therapist is missing, so keep it gentle and stop if heavy material surfaces. If an absolute
contraindication is present (psychosis, unstabilized borderline structure, active infantile affect),
even sandplay needs specialist oversight — go to `safety.md`. Because no ego-lowering is required,
the dual-awareness failure modes are less acute here, but `safety.md` still governs.

## 1 · The self-directed sand-tray procedure

**When & where it works best:** Words fail to reach the material; very early-stage work; the user
needs the hands and the tactile rather than mental focusing; stalled in other modalities. A real
sand tray is ideal; a shallow box of sand and a collection of small objects/figures works for
self-practice.

**Walk it through (one step per turn):**
1. **Set the invitation (non-directive).** Frame it loosely: "Make a world in the tray that feels
   right to you today — there's no correct way." Ask: "Ready to begin, with your tray and figures
   to hand?"
2. **Ground in the sand first.** Ask: "Run your hands through the sand for a moment — just feel it.
   What's it like?" (Two trays, one dry and one damp, are traditional if available.)
3. **Select figures intuitively (Phase 2).** From whatever collection they have — people, animals,
   buildings, natural objects, vehicles, mythic figures. Ask: "Pick the figures that draw you, without
   deciding why. Which ones are asking to be picked up?"
4. **Place and arrange without a plan (Phase 3).** Ask: "Put them in the tray however feels right —
   don't design a scene, let it assemble. What's taking shape?" Stay silent and non-interpretive while
   they build; don't analyze the symbols as they go.
5. **Signal completion.** Ask: "Does the world feel complete? Tell me when it's done." Then have them
   photograph the finished tray.
6. **Describe, don't interpret (open questions only).** Ask: "Tell me about this world you've made."
   Receive the description; resist interpreting. Interpretation is **never** given during construction
   — "the process reveals itself in its own time."
7. **Defer analysis; apply Phase 4 lightly.** Symbolic understanding comes later or across a series,
   not now. Still, ask gently: "Is there anything this world seems to ask of you?" Record/log
   (`logging.md`), keeping the photo as part of the record.

## 2 · Series review

Sandplay is meant to be tracked **across time** — the sandplay equivalent of the mandala series.
Periodically, review photographs of past trays together to follow the symbolic evolution. Note the
series in `CLAUDE.md` "Open threads" and link the entries so the progression can be followed
(`logging.md`).

## If it doesn't work, try

- **There's no real "stall"** in the usual sense — touching the sand and arranging the figures *is*
  the process; an empty or sparse tray is still a tray. If the user genuinely can't begin (anxiety,
  fragility), the right response is **patient, non-anxious waiting** and calm presence — not prompting
  or structuring. Silence is itself the intervention.
- **Heavy material surfaces and there's no therapist present:** stop, ground (`safety.md`), and treat
  this as the signal that the containing presence of a trained therapist is what's needed next
  (`clinical-vs-self-guided.md`).
- **The user wants to go deeper than the tray allows:** if they're stable and ready, a dialogue-based
  modality (`writing.md`, `visual.md`) can take a tray figure further — give it a voice and ask it a
  question.
