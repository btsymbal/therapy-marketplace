## How to use this file

**Movement / Authentic Movement** is the first-line modality when the material is encoded
**somatically** — bodily sensations, movement impulses, psychosomatic symptoms, dream images with a
kinesthetic quality — and when verbal and visual approaches have stalled. It's specifically
indicated for preverbal and complex trauma, where implicit memory means words and pictures can't
reach the material, because gesture bridges implicit memory and emergent imagery.

**Read this caution first.** The formal modality uses a **Mover-Witness** structure: a trained
witness holds the space and provides relational containment. That relational holding is part of what
makes it safe for fragile presentations — and trauma-linked or dissociative material wants a trained
witness or analyst present, **not** solo practice (`clinical-presentations.md`, `safety.md`). The
self-practice form below is for **stable** users working with ordinary somatic material, who develop
an **internal witness** as their version of the "as if" stance. Delivered per
`walkthrough-protocol.md`: one step per turn, wait. Keep `safety.md` live; if dual awareness
collapses or movement turns compulsive, stop and ground.

## 1 · Authentic / embodied active imagination

**When & where it works best:** Material that lives in the body; stalled verbal/visual work;
prominent psychosomatic symptoms; a stable user with enough physical space to move freely.

**Walk it through (one step per turn):**
1. **Warm up and clear space.** Light stretching, attention to breath, enough room to move safely.
   Ask: "Do you have space to move freely, and are you warmed up enough to begin?"
2. **Anchor the internal witness.** Do the dual-awareness anchor (`foundations.md`), framed for the
   body: "As you move, keep a part of you watching — the internal witness. You're both moving and
   observing yourself moving. Can you hold that?"
3. **Drop into sensation (Phase 2).** Eyes closed, standing or sitting. Ask: "Bring attention inside
   — weight, pressure, temperature, any impulse. What's the body actually doing right now?"
4. **Wait for an impulse — don't choreograph.** Ask: "Don't plan a movement or pick a direction.
   Wait until an impulse arises on its own. When one comes, tell me — then follow it."
5. **Follow the impulse wherever it leads (Phase 3).** Into stillness, repetitive gesture, floor
   contact, sound, large movement — follow, don't direct. Ask: "Where does it want to go? Follow it
   and tell me what happens."
6. **Oscillate body ↔ image.** Ask: "Does any image, figure, or feeling come up as you move? Let the
   movement and the image inform each other — what's the link?"
7. **Check the internal witness.** Ask: "Are you still watching yourself, or has the movement taken
   you over?" If the observer is gone, or movement becomes compulsive → stop and ground (`safety.md`).
8. **Continue to a natural stop** (often ~20-30 minutes for an experienced mover; less to start). Open
   eyes gradually; ground through breath and stillness.
9. **Convert to a symbolic record immediately.** Ask: "Before it fades — draw or paint the experience,
   or sketch the movement. Capture it on paper." (Drawing the experience is part of the method here —
   it preserves the somatic event as a symbol; `art.md`, `logging.md`.)
10. **Ethical confrontation (Phase 4).** Ask: "What does this ask of you in waking life? One concrete
    thing." Then log.

## The internal-witness adaptation for self-practice

In the formal modality the **Witness** sits at the edge of the space, never interprets, never
directs, keeps the mover physically safe, attends to their own inner response, and offers
observations only afterward — as personal response, not interpretation. Self-practice has no outside
witness, so the user cultivates the **internal witness**: the capacity to observe oneself while
moving. That internal witness *is* the "as if" stance for this modality — if it can't be sustained,
the modality isn't safe to run solo today; drop to a calmer practice or defer (`safety.md`). For a
fragile presentation, the honest guidance is to seek a trained witness or analyst rather than
practicing alone.

## If it doesn't work, try

- **No impulse arises after sustained waiting:** offer gentle catalysts — stillness itself,
  attention to the breath, a small sounding or humming, a single repeated gesture — and keep
  waiting. Don't manufacture a movement.
- **Movement stays blocked:** redirect to `art.md` (drawing) or `sandplay.md` — the tactile quality
  of the sand often catalyzes somatic engagement that mental focusing won't.
- **It floods, dissociates, or turns compulsive:** stop immediately and ground (`safety.md`). For
  trauma-linked somatic material, this wants a trained witness/analyst, not solo continuation
  (`clinical-presentations.md`).
