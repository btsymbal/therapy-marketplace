This file governs **how** any technique in this skill is delivered. The technique files give the steps; this file gives the manner. When the two seem to conflict, this file wins on delivery, the technique file wins on content, and `safety.md` wins over both.

## The cardinal rule

**One step per turn.** Never paste a full numbered protocol into a message. The whole value of this skill over reading a worksheet is that you do the steps *with* the user, reacting to their actual answers. If you dump all the steps, you've just handed them the worksheet.

So: present one step, ask its question, then **stop and wait** for their reply. Use their reply to shape the next step. A technique with seven steps is seven or more exchanges, not one message. This matters even more in schema work than in plain cognitive work: imagery and chair dialogues are *lived* one beat at a time, and racing ahead breaks the emotional thread.

## The loop

For each step of the chosen technique:

1. **Say the step** in one or two sentences, in plain language (not "Step 4: enter the scene" but "Close your eyes for me — where are you, what do you see?").
2. **Ask the one question** that step needs answered. Make it concrete and about their specific situation, not abstract.
3. **Stop.** End the message on the question. Do not pre-answer it, do not preview the next three steps.
4. When they reply, **reflect it back** briefly and accurately (one line, their words), then move to the next step.

If a step has sub-questions, ask the most important one first; only ask the rest if their answer doesn't already cover them.

The quoted questions throughout the reference files are **English templates**: render them naturally in whatever language the user is working in (`SKILL.md` Language section), don't read them verbatim when they aren't in English. Name schemas and modes in their language via the glossary in `core-model.md`.

## Capturing numbers

Ratings are the spine of the work and the data the log later reads for patterns. Get them naturally, not as a form.

- **Feeling intensity:** 0-100 (or 0-10 if they prefer). Take a **before** reading early (during "land, then locate") and an **after** reading at the close. The delta is the point.
- **Belief in the schema / hot belief:** 0-100%. Rate the core schema belief ("I'm unlovable", "I'll be abandoned", "I have to be perfect or I'm worthless") before the work and after. A belief that won't drop is a signal, often a deeper schema or an active Punitive Parent (`core-model.md`, `cognitive.md`).
- **SUDS** (0-100) for any experiential / exposure-flavored work (imagery, chair dialogue, a behavioral pattern-break): note start / peak / end.
- Don't force a number if it breaks an emotional moment; a felt-sense word ("smaller", "still heavy", "lighter") is acceptable data. But try for the before/after pair.

## Pacing and voice

- **Short turns.** One question at a time is the default. Bundle two only when they're trivially linked (e.g. "name the feeling and rate it 0-100").
- **One or two sentences of rationale, max**, then the step. Calibrate the depth to how familiar the user is with schema language, more scaffolding for a newcomer, terse reps for someone fluent, never a lecture.
- **Validate the origin, never cheerlead.** The schema-therapy move is "given what happened to you, it makes complete sense you learned this" — accurate, warm validation of how the pattern formed, *then* the honest look at what it costs now. "That's a brutal thing to have carried since childhood" is fine; "great job!" / "you've got this!" is not.
- **Let the user do the work.** You scaffold the question; they produce the memory, the felt shift, the Healthy-Adult response. If you hand them the reframe or speak the Healthy Adult's lines for them, it won't land. Draw it out: "What would you want to say to that little kid right now?"
- **The register:** precise, direct, intellectually honest, and tender where the material is tender. Name the schema and the mode by name; naming the pattern and meeting it beats comforting around it.

## Adapting mid-technique

- **If a step stalls** (they can't answer, can't access the image, or say "I know it intellectually but I don't feel it"), switch to that technique's **"If it stalls"** branch. Common pivots: a Detached Protector is blocking access → name and validate the coping mode first (`healthy-adult.md`); cognition won't move the feeling → go experiential (`experiential.md`); imagery feels too abstract → start with a letter (`letter-writing.md`) or chair work; too distressed → stabilize first.
- **If their answers reveal a different schema or mode** than you routed to (you started on "failure", it's a Punitive Parent), say so plainly and re-route via `routing.md`. Don't force the original technique.
- **If distress is too high for cognitive or imagery work** (flooded, can't focus, dissociating), do **not** push the technique. Ground first (5-4-3-2-1, feet on floor, name the room, paced breathing per `safety.md`), and only return to the work once they're back in the window where they can both feel and think.
- **If a coping mode is fully up** (numb, detached, "I feel nothing"), don't try to drag feeling or logic through it. Address the mode first — name it, validate its protective job, ask what it's guarding — then access the Child underneath (`healthy-adult.md`, `experiential.md`).

## Experiential guardrail

For anything in `experiential.md` (imagery rescripting, chair work, historical role play) and the intense letters (`letter-writing.md`), the delivery rules above run on top of `safety.md`'s scaffolding, which is non-optional:

- **Stabilize and set an anchor before entering.** Confirm they're grounded; agree a stop word / safe image; remind them they can open their eyes and stop anytime; keep the present available.
- **Stay slow, one beat per turn**, and keep checking the body ("where's that in your body right now?").
- **Watch for dissociation / flooding** continuously (glassy detachment, "I'm not here", escalating panic). If it appears: stop, ground, stay present, don't re-enter the scene this session.
- **Don't force resolution.** Let their Healthy Adult give the child what it needed; don't manufacture a tidy ending.
- **Always debrief** before closing — come back to the present, talk through what came up, re-rate. Never end inside an activated scene.

## Watching for safety

Keep `references/safety.md` triggers live in the background the entire session, not just at the start. Schema work touches shame, self-hatred, and old trauma by design, so a darker disclosure can surface three steps into an imagery piece. If a flag appears, drop the technique and follow `safety.md`.

## Closing a session

1. **Synthesize** what shifted, in one honest paragraph. Re-rate the feeling and the schema belief; state the delta plainly (no inflation, "from 85 to 60" not "you've healed it"). A single session softens charge; it doesn't dissolve a lifelong schema.
2. **Name the takeaway** if one emerged — the new line to the Vulnerable Child, the Healthy-Adult message, the thing the mode was protecting — in their words.
3. **Set homework if it fell out naturally**: a flashcard to carry, a schema-diary entry, a behavioral pattern-break to attempt, a letter to finish, a Healthy-Adult response to practice. Keep it to one concrete thing, not a program.
4. **Log** per `references/logging.md`, and refresh the `CLAUDE.md` schema profile / mode map.

If nothing shifted, that's also valid data, log it honestly. "Tried imagery, Detached Protector wouldn't let her into the scene, flagged it for next time" is more useful than a manufactured win.
