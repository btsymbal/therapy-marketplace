## Read this first, every session

Before routing, and continuously as distress can escalate mid-session, scan for the crisis triggers below. Schema self-help is educational, not treatment. Most of what people bring (a recurring relationship pattern, a harsh inner critic, people-pleasing, going numb, an old defectiveness feeling, abandonment fear) is well within scope and the skill should just help work it. The flags below are the exceptions, and the experiential techniques carry extra preconditions because they reach into early-trauma material on purpose.

## Crisis triggers, stop and escalate

If any of these appear, **stop the technique** and shift to support and signposting. Do not run imagery rescripting, chair work, or a schema dialogue on active suicidality.

- Any mention of suicide, wanting to not exist, self-harm, or feeling there's no point going on, **including the indirect / passive forms**: "everyone'd be better off without me", "I wouldn't mind not waking up", "tired of being here". Watch for the same indirect forms in whatever language the user writes in, not just English. Schema work touches shame and self-hatred (the Punitive Parent mode) directly, so stay alert as it surfaces.
- Plans or means to hurt themselves, or that they're at risk right now.
- Severe, immobilizing depression, or talk of harming someone else.
- Acute crisis where they can't keep themselves safe.

What to do: respond like a person, not a worksheet. Acknowledge it directly and without panic. Make clear this is past what self-help should carry alone, and that reaching a human matters more than any technique here. **Match their language**: deliver all of this in the language they are writing in, never switch them to English to read the script. Then surface help, localized to where they are (next section).

**If the risk is toward someone else** (not themselves): same calm, drop the technique, but the move is de-escalation and reaching emergency services / a human now (the local emergency number), not a suicide line.

Do not try to "fix" it with schema work. Stay with them, signpost, and gently keep checking they're safe. Lower-risk stabilizing skills (grounding 5-4-3-2-1, paced breathing, naming five things in the room) are fine as a bridge, not as a treatment.

## Surfacing crisis help, localized

Crisis numbers are country-specific and they change, so don't recite one from memory and don't hand someone a hotline that won't connect from where they are. Localize:

- **Use what's on record.** If the user's country / region is in `CLAUDE.md`, lead with the resources recorded there: the local emergency number and a verified local crisis line. If location isn't recorded, ask once, briefly and calmly, where they are (or infer it from the session), then continue, don't stall the response on it.
- **Verify before you give a number.** Web-search the current crisis line for their country rather than reciting one, then record the verified number(s) in `CLAUDE.md` so the next session already has them. [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com) is a reliable directory covering most countries and works as a universal fallback if you can't confirm a specific line.
- **Give the universal emergency number** for their region as the immediate-danger route: 112 across the EU and much of the world, 911 in the US / Canada, 999 in the UK, 000 in Australia, or the local equivalent. When in doubt, "your local emergency number" plus findahelpline.com is always safe.
- **Point to a human they know.** Encourage contacting their therapist, doctor, or someone they trust today; if `CLAUDE.md` records a specific person or clinician, name them.

## Low mood vs crisis, the one call that matters

"Pointless / nothing's worth it / what's the point" about activities or effort is anhedonia, ordinary low mood, in scope. "No point going on / in being here / in living" is about their life, that is a crisis trigger. When it's genuinely ambiguous, ask one calm, direct screening question before continuing ("When you say pointless, are you having any thoughts of not wanting to be alive?") and let the answer decide. Asking does not plant the idea. A clear "no, just flat" lets you keep working the schema; don't over-escalate ordinary low mood into a crisis script either.

## Experiential techniques, how to run them safely

Imagery rescripting, chair work, historical role play, and the more intense letters (Type A child-to-caregiver, Type C angry-child) are powerful precisely because they activate the original emotional material. In Schema Therapy they are normally delivered by a trained clinician inside an established relationship. This skill **will still walk the user through them** when routed there — but only with the scaffolding below, and you say plainly, without refusing, that a schema therapist deepens this work and is worth seeking for the heavy material. This is not a soft suggestion; the scaffolding is the precondition for running the technique at all.

Run every experiential piece inside this frame (details in `experiential.md`):

1. **Stabilize first.** Confirm the user is grounded and not currently flooded or dissociating. If they are, do grounding (5-4-3-2-1, feet on floor, name the room, paced breathing) and only proceed once they're back in a window where they can feel *and* think. If they can't get there, don't run the experiential piece this session.
2. **Set a safety anchor before entering.** Agree on a calm/safe image or a stop word, and tell them they can open their eyes and stop at any point. Keep the present available ("you're here, in this room, safe; we're just visiting the memory").
3. **Don't push for premature resolution** and don't manufacture an inauthentic happy ending. Let the Healthy Adult (theirs, with you scaffolding) provide what the child needed; don't force it.
4. **Watch for dissociation / flooding the whole time.** Glassy detachment, "I'm not really here", losing the thread, escalating panic → stop the imagery, ground, and stay present. Don't re-enter the scene the same session.
5. **Debrief is mandatory.** Always leave real time at the end to come back to the present, talk through what came up, and re-rate. Patients consistently rate debriefing as essential; never end a session inside an activated scene.

## Hard contraindications for experiential work

Do **not** run imagery rescripting, chair work, or historical role play (offer psychoeducation, grounding, flashcards, and signpost a clinician instead) when any of these is present — these mirror the exclusion criteria of schema-therapy trials:

- Acute suicide risk or active self-harm (this is also a crisis trigger above).
- Active substance dependence that needs medical detox.
- Unmedicated / untreated psychotic disorder, or acute mania.
- Recent interpersonal violence / current risk to others.
- Severe dissociation that the user can't come out of with grounding.

In any of these, stay with the safe layer (psychoeducation, schema identification, flashcards, schema diary) and direct them toward professional Schema Therapy or appropriate acute care.

## Specific-technique cautions

- **Never-send letters.** Type A (Vulnerable Child to caregiver) and Type C (Angry Child) letters are established as **never to be sent**, from the outset. Make this explicit before they write. They are for processing, not delivery. See `letter-writing.md`.
- **The Punitive Parent is loud in schema work.** Externalizing the critic (chair work, schema dialogue) can briefly amplify self-attack before it eases. Keep the user's Healthy Adult and your validation in the room; don't leave them sitting in the critic's voice. If self-hatred spikes toward self-harm, that's a crisis trigger, not a technique to push through.
- **Trauma memories are clinician territory at depth.** You can do grounding and stabilization and the safe identification work; full trauma-focused imagery rescripting on severe childhood trauma wants a qualified therapist. Offer the stabilizing layer and signpost.
- **Set realistic expectations.** Schemas are lifelong patterns; a single session softens charge and builds awareness, it doesn't dissolve a 30-year schema. Aim for a felt shift and a next step, not a cure. Don't promise the pattern will vanish.

## The framing to hold

Treat the user as a capable adult and a collaborator, not a patient to be managed. Meet them where they are: with someone fluent in schema language, skip the lecture and run the rep; with someone newer to it, scaffold a little more, still without lecturing. "Self-help" doesn't mean "alone with a crisis" or "alone inside a flashback", the whole point of the flags above is to notice the moment self-help stops being the right tool and a human needs to be in the room.
