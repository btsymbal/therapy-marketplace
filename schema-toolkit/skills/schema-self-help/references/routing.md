## How to route

The user won't name a schema or a mode, they'll name a feeling, a situation, or a pattern they keep repeating ("I always pick people who can't love me back", "I feel like a fraud the second I'm praised", "I just go cold and shut down", "I can't say no to anyone"). Your job: from what they say plus the concrete moment, identify the **schema** that's firing and the **active mode** they flipped into, then reach for that pattern's **first-line technique** and open its file. Pick one technique to start, not a menu. Re-route freely if their answers point elsewhere.

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) and get a feeling + 0-100 intensity + a concrete moment first (`SKILL.md` flow steps 1-2). The full schema/mode vocabulary, and the canonical names to log, are in `core-model.md`.

If the pattern isn't clear yet, the on-ramp is `assessment.md`: identify the schema and map the active mode first, then route to a change technique.

## Schema / mode → first-line technique → file

The subset to reach for **first**. Each technique lives in the listed file; the model is in `core-model.md`.

| Pattern in the room | First-line technique(s) | Files |
|---|---|---|
| Pattern not yet named | Schema identification + mode mapping | `assessment.md` + `core-model.md` |
| Active Punitive / Demanding Critic | Chair work to externalize the critic; "rewriting the rules"; schema dialogue | `experiential.md`, `cognitive.md` |
| Detached Protector / Self-Soother blocking feeling | Name + validate the coping mode, then access the Vulnerable Child | `healthy-adult.md`, `experiential.md` |
| Vulnerable Child (abandonment, shame, deprivation) | Imagery rescripting; self-reparenting; flashcards as a transitional anchor | `experiential.md`, `healthy-adult.md`, `cognitive.md` |
| Compliant Surrenderer (people-pleasing, subjugation) | Behavioral pattern-breaking / assertiveness; chair work | `behavioral.md`, `experiential.md` |
| Self-Aggrandizer / Overcompensator | Internal empathic confrontation; imagery for the child beneath | `healthy-adult.md`, `experiential.md` |
| Between-session distress, craving, abandonment spikes | Schema flashcards; schema diary | `cognitive.md` |
| A schema belief driving a behavior, ready to test it | Behavioral pattern-breaking | `behavioral.md` |
| Anger at a caregiver / processing old harm | Letter writing (Type A / C, never sent) | `letter-writing.md` |
| Building the well part up | Healthy-Adult strengthening | `healthy-adult.md` |

Critical sequencing (from the model, `core-model.md`): cognitive and behavioral change usually lands *after* coping modes are softened and the emotional charge is worked experientially — don't lead with restructuring on a hot schema while a Detached Protector is fully up. And the Healthy Adult is the through-line of every technique, not a separate stage.

## Multi-pattern and re-routing

- Most sessions have one schema in the foreground; some are layered (abandonment *and* defectiveness; subjugation *and* self-sacrifice; a Vulnerable Child guarded by a Detached Protector). Work the most active one first, usually the coping mode that's blocking contact, then the Child underneath. Name the others and offer to come back.
- If a walk-through reveals the real pattern is different from where you started (you opened on "failure", it's actually a Punitive Parent), say so plainly and switch files. Don't finish the wrong technique for tidiness.
- **When distress is too high to think or to do imagery**, route to stabilization first (`safety.md` grounding), then back to the work.
- **"I just feel nothing"** is usually a coping mode (Detached Protector / Self-Soother), not an absence of material — name and validate the mode's protective job first (`healthy-adult.md`), don't try to force feeling or cognition through it.
- **A recurring relationship cycle** ("same dynamic every time") points at a schema-driven interpersonal template: map the schema + the mode sequence (`assessment.md`), then usually imagery for the root and behavioral pattern-breaking for the present-day move.
- **Hard or high-risk presentations** (BPD-level mode switching, complex trauma, eating disorders, forensic) → consult `conditions.md` for the profile and the honest "a clinician/program is the real treatment here" framing; deliver the safe layer.
