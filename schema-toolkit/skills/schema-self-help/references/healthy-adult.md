The Healthy Adult is the through-line of the whole skill, not a separate stage: every technique is, in the end, building the part that nurtures the Vulnerable Child, sets limits on the Impulsive Child, and stands up to the Punitive/Demanding Parent. Its seven dimensions (`core-model.md`): meta-awareness, reality orientation, agency, emotional connection, reciprocity, coherent identity, compassion. Early on you lend Healthy-Adult functioning by modeling it; the marker of progress is the user accessing their own. The two self-applicable adaptations of clinician techniques — self-reparenting (from limited reparenting) and internal empathic confrontation (from empathic confrontation) — live here. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: one step per turn, end on a question, wait.

---

## 1 · Healthy-Adult strengthening

**Reach for it when:** any time, but especially in consolidation (Phase 3) — after experiential work has softened a mode, to build the part that responds differently next time. Also the right move when the user can already see the pattern and needs to grow the well part rather than excavate more.

**Walk it through:**
1. **Find the Healthy Adult that's already there.** Ask: "When are you most like the wise, fair, capable version of yourself — even briefly? What's it like in those moments?" (Build from real instances, however small; don't install it from scratch.)
2. **Model it, then hand it over.** Where it's missing, voice the Healthy-Adult response yourself once, then ask them to take it: "Here's what a fully-on-your-side adult might say to that… now you say it, in your words."
3. **Rehearse in imagery.** Ask: "Picture the Healthy-Adult you handling [the hard situation] — what do they do, how do they hold themselves?"
4. **Use it in chair work** to answer critical/demanding modes from the Healthy-Adult position (`experiential.md` 2).
5. **Catch it in real time.** Whenever a Healthy-Adult move appears in what they say, name and validate it: "That right there — that was the Healthy Adult."
6. **Test it behaviorally** (`behavioral.md`) between sessions.
7. **Internalize.** Gradually reduce your prompting as they generate their own Healthy-Adult responses — that shift *is* the progress marker; log it.

**If it stalls:** a Punitive/Demanding Parent is too loud for the Healthy Adult to be heard → externalize and challenge the critic in chair work (`experiential.md` 2) first. Can't access it in imagery → write a Healthy-Adult letter (`letter-writing.md` 4) to externalize and practice the voice. "I don't have a wise part" → borrow one: a real or imagined figure who fully had their back, then transfer it.

## 2 · Self-reparenting (the Healthy Adult to the Vulnerable Child)

**Reach for it when:** a Vulnerable Child mode is activated — abandonment, deprivation, shame — and needs comfort and care. This is the self-help form of limited reparenting: in therapy the therapist provides the missing nurturance; here the user's Healthy Adult learns to give the younger self what the caregivers didn't, with you scaffolding and modeling it. (Note plainly: a real therapeutic relationship provides reparenting in a way self-work can't fully replace — this builds the internal capacity, and a clinician is worth seeking when the deprivation is deep.)

**Walk it through:**
1. **Identify the Child and its need.** Ask: "How old does the hurt feel, and what does that younger part most need right now — comfort, reassurance, protection, permission?"
2. **Speak to the Child by name, with warmth.** Model it first: address the child directly with calming, nurturing language ("you didn't deserve that; you weren't too much"). Then ask them to say it.
3. **Give what was missing.** Ask: "As the adult you are now, what do you want to give that little one that they didn't get? Say it to them."
4. **Make it concrete and ongoing.** Ask: "How could you keep showing up for that part this week — a phrase, a small caring act, a reminder?" (A flashcard or transitional object can carry it, `cognitive.md`.)
5. **Let the Child respond.** Ask: "What happens in the younger part when it hears that?"
6. Re-rate the feeling; note what landed for the log and the mode map.

**If it stalls:** the Healthy Adult can't feel warmth toward the Child (often a Punitive Parent intercepting) → confront the critic first (3, and `experiential.md` 2). It feels fake / performed → slow down, anchor to one real caring impulse rather than a script; or borrow a compassionate figure and let them do it first. Flooding → ground and debrief (`safety.md`).

## 3 · Internal empathic confrontation (meeting a coping or critic mode)

**Reach for it when:** a coping mode (Detached Protector, Compliant Surrenderer, Self-Aggrandizer/Overcompensator) is running and blocking the work, or a self-defeating pattern needs both validation *and* honest challenge. This is the self-applied form of the therapist's empathic confrontation: first deeply validate why the mode exists, then constructively question whether it still serves them — the two halves are inseparable, validation without challenge colludes, challenge without validation replicates the original harm.

**Walk it through:**
1. **Part 1 — Empathic validation.** Name the mode and honor its origin. Ask/offer: "This part learned to [shut down / please / puff up] because, back then, it kept you safe — that makes complete sense. When did it first have to do that job?"
2. **Name the present suffering** it now causes, without judgment. Ask: "And what does it cost you now, in the situations you care about?"
3. **Part 2 — Constructive challenge.** Invite the honest question. Ask: "Is the thing it's protecting against still true in your current life — or is it guarding against a danger that's mostly past?"
4. **Match the tone to the mode.** Gentle for a Vulnerable Child; warm but firmer with a Detached Protector that won't budge; most direct (still kind) with a Self-Aggrandizer/Overcompensator, which needs honest feedback about its impact on others.
5. **Invite the choice.** Ask: "Knowing it once protected you — would you be willing to let the Healthy Adult take this situation, just here, and see what happens?" Bridge to self-reparenting (2) or a behavioral pattern-break (`behavioral.md`).

**If it stalls:** the mode digs in under challenge → you went to challenge before enough validation; go back to Part 1 and stay there longer. A Detached Protector blocks all contact → validate the Protector directly and patiently before anything else; you may need imagery (`experiential.md`) to reach the Child behind it. The challenge tips into self-criticism → that's a Punitive Parent hijacking the confrontation; stop, name it, and re-anchor in genuine self-compassion.
