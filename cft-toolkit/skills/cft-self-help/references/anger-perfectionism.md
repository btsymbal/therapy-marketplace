## How to use this file

The anger and perfectionism layer. CFT's distinctive contribution to both is the same
reframe: each is a **protective strategy of an over-active threat or drive system**, not a
character defect — which is exactly the de-shaming the model provides. Assumes `model.md` and
`core-cmt.md`. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`. **Safety:** if anger involves risk of
harm to someone else, drop the practice — de-escalation and emergency help come first
(`safety.md`).

## Anger

CFT understands chronic anger as a **threat-system response without a soothing-system
counterbalance**, and it works the **shame-anger link**: anger is often a threat response *to
shame* (shame drives aggression as self-protection). The key clinical distinction is shame vs
guilt — too much shame fuels more anger and, in forensic contexts, recidivism, while
appropriate guilt motivates taking responsibility and making amends.

Above the shared baseline:
1. **Three circles, anger-framed** (`model.md` 1) — "anger is your threat system protecting
   you, not proof you're a bad person." De-shaming the anger lowers it.
2. **SRB and soothing-system skill-building** (`core-cmt.md` 1-2) — growing the
   counterbalance the threat system lacks.
3. **Shame-vs-guilt psychoeducation** — separate "I am bad" (shame, the target) from "I did
   something I regret" (guilt, which can guide repair). If shame underneath the anger is the
   engine, work `shame-self-criticism.md`.

## Perfectionism

CFT reads perfectionism as a **drive-system strategy adopted in response to a threat of
inadequacy or rejection** — the inner critic using relentless standards as protection. So the
work starts not by challenging the standards but by understanding the critic's protective
intent:

1. **Functional analysis of the perfectionist critic** — ask what it fears would happen if
   you eased off, what it's guarding against (usually shame, inadequacy, rejection). This is
   the same critic-as-protector move as in `shame-self-criticism.md`.
2. **Compassionate Self** (`core-cmt.md` 5) — building the wise, strong, warm alternative
   that can hold standards *with* kindness rather than under threat.
3. Chairwork with the perfectionist critic is the classic next step but is **clinician-only**
   (`advanced-clinician.md`); name it for therapy and use the compassionate self in self-help.

## If it stalls

- Anger spikes in-session → SRB and grounding first; don't do compassion work on a flooded
  threat system; if there's any risk to others, `safety.md`.
- The perfectionist critic won't soften / wants direct dialogue → that's chairwork, clinician-
  guided; consolidate the compassionate self and signpost (`advanced-clinician.md`).
- Shame is clearly the driver under either → route to `shame-self-criticism.md`.
- Compassion reads as "letting myself off / going soft" → re-frame with the model: compassion
  is sensitivity *plus* the courage and commitment to act, not indulgence (`model.md` 3).
