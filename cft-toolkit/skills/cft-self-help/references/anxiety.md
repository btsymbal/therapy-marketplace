## How to use this file

The anxiety layer. CFT's frame on anxiety is a chronically over-active threat system
without a soothing-system counterbalance — plus a second layer of shame and self-criticism
*about* being anxious ("what's wrong with me that I can't stop?"). Note there is **no
manualized CFT protocol for generalized anxiety as a named diagnosis**; CFT for anxiety is
delivered transdiagnostically. Where the anxiety is a discrete fear maintained by avoidance
(a phobia, panic), graded exposure is a CBT strength — the `cbt-toolkit` skill is the better
tool, and CFT is the adjunct when shame and self-attack ride alongside. Assumes `model.md`
and `core-cmt.md`. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`.

## Transdiagnostic anxiety

Above the shared baseline (psychoeducation on the evolved threat system + SRB):

1. **Safe / Soothing Place** (`core-cmt.md` 2) — direct soothing-system access to
   counterbalance the always-on threat.
2. **Compassionate Self** (`core-cmt.md` 5) — to relate to anxious thoughts with warmth and
   courage rather than avoidance or self-attack.
3. **Compassionate behavioral engagement** — gently replacing avoidance with values-
   consistent action taken *with* the compassionate self alongside (the courage in
   compassion), rather than white-knuckling it. If the core problem is avoidance of a
   specific feared situation, that's graded exposure — a CBT job (`routing.md`).

## Social anxiety

The mechanism is **external shame** — fear of negative evaluation, of being judged inadequate
or humiliated — and an inability to access social safeness. The signature CFT move here:

1. **Compassionate Other / Ideal Compassionate Image** (`core-cmt.md` 4). For social anxiety
   specifically this generates "a felt sense of being listened to without words" — social
   safeness that doesn't require any social *performance* to earn it. Particularly powerful;
   make it the centerpiece.
2. **Compassionate Self** (`core-cmt.md` 5) — to meet the post-event replaying and the fear
   of exposure with warmth.

Standard psychoeducation emphasises the social-threat system and external shame.

## OCD (treatment-resistant) — clinician-flagged

CFT for OCD targets the hyperactivated threat system around guilt and harm fears, and two
layers of self-criticism: **primary** (aimed at the intrusive thoughts — "I'm disgusting/
dangerous for having these") and **secondary** (aimed at the compulsions — "I'm weak/pathetic
for doing the ritual again"). The first-line CMT subset is Compassionate Self (wisdom,
strength, commitment) + Compassionate Letter Writing. **But:** the feared-compassion (FBR)
work this population usually needs is **clinician-only** (`advanced-clinician.md`), and
exposure-and-response-prevention for the compulsions themselves is clinician-guided CBT/ERP.
In self-help, offer the de-shaming of both layers of self-criticism and the compassionate-
self practice, and signpost specialist treatment. Read `safety.md`.

## If it stalls

- Imagery floods an already-anxious system → drop to SRB and the **Compassionate Kitbag**
  (`core-cmt.md` 10) as a body anchor before retrying.
- The Compassionate Other triggers backdraft (social anxiety with attachment wounds) → start
  from abstract warmth/light, build the figure across sessions (`core-cmt.md` 4, `model.md` 4).
- It's really avoidance of a specific feared thing → graded exposure, a CBT job; consider the
  `cbt-toolkit` skill (`routing.md`).
- The anxiety is OCD with strong feared-compassion blocks → clinician territory; consolidate
  the safe layer and signpost (`advanced-clinician.md`, `safety.md`).
