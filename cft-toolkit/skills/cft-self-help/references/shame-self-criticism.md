## How to use this file

The shame layer — the difficulty CFT was *built* for, and the most common reason to reach
for this skill: the felt sense of being fundamentally flawed, inferior, or unlovable, and
the inner voice that attacks the self. Gilbert developed CFT precisely because many people
understand cognitive reappraisal intellectually but can't generate a *felt* sense of a kind
inner voice — the "cognitive-emotional mismatch". This file assumes the model (`model.md`)
and the core ladder (`core-cmt.md`) and adds the shame-specific sequencing and framing.
Deliver every step per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: one cue per turn, warm, wait. **Safety:**
"worthless / a burden" sits close to the crisis line — screen if it's ambiguous
(`safety.md`).

## Naming the shame: external, internal, shame vs guilt

Getting the distinction across is itself de-shaming. Hold three:
- **External shame** — believing *others* see you as inferior, shameful, contemptible
  (fires the social-threat system).
- **Internal shame** — your own verdict that you are fundamentally flawed.
- **Shame vs guilt** — shame attacks the *self* ("I am bad"); guilt names an *act* ("I did a
  bad thing"). CFT keeps appropriate guilt (it motivates repair) and targets shame. If the
  user is collapsing a specific regret into a global self-condemnation, separating the two
  loosens it.

## The sequence

First-line for shame work, above the shared baseline (psychoeducation + SRB):

1. **De-shame with the model** (`model.md` 1-2). The three circles and "this is not your
   fault" land especially hard here, because shame's second layer is shame about being
   ashamed. Spend real time on this; it's not preamble, it's intervention.
2. **Soothing Rhythm Breathing** (`core-cmt.md` 1) — settle the threat system the
   self-attack keeps firing.
3. **Compassionate Self** (`core-cmt.md` 5) — the central move: build the wise, strong, warm,
   committed inner identity from the user's own remembered moments, then have *it* relate to
   the struggling self. If the user can't generate it from the inside, route through the
   **Compassionate Other** (`core-cmt.md` 4) first — being cared for by an image before
   caring from within.
4. **Compassionate Letter Writing** (`core-cmt.md` 8) as consolidation between sessions — the
   compassionate self addressing the part that's struggling, on paper.

**Chairwork** (the three-chair self-criticism dialogue) is the classic CFT shame technique
but is **clinician-only** (`advanced-clinician.md` 1). Name it as a goal for therapy; in
self-help, the Compassionate Self and the letter reach much of the same ground safely.

## The self-critic as protector

The self-critic is understood as *originally protective* — a threat-based strategy that
developed to prevent inferiority, rejection, or failure. Before doing anything with it, do a
gentle functional analysis: ask what the critic is *afraid would happen* if it went quiet,
and what it's trying to protect against. This reframes the critic from enemy to a frightened
guard, which is what makes a compassionate response possible rather than a war between two
parts of the self. (When the critic is an internalized *abusive* voice rather than rank-based
self-criticism, it's differentiated and handled with trauma-informed care — clinician
territory, `advanced-clinician.md`, `trauma-ptsd.md`.)

## If it stalls

- The compassionate self feels fake / "I'm not like that" → they're accessing qualities all
  humans have, not pretending; ask for any tiny real moment of kindness (`core-cmt.md` 5).
- Warmth toward the self triggers threat / "I don't deserve it" → that's a fear/block/
  resistance; name it, normalize it, step down to the Compassionate Other or to giving
  compassion to others first (`model.md` 4, `core-cmt.md` 4, 6).
- Self-attack won't loosen at all and the work wants the critic addressed directly → that's
  chairwork — clinician-guided; consolidate the compassionate self and signpost
  (`advanced-clinician.md`).
- It's really a discrete distorted belief that would yield to evidence-testing → that may be
  a CBT job; consider the `cbt-toolkit` skill (`routing.md`, "Is this actually a CBT job?").
