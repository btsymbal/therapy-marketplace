## Read this first, every session

Before routing, and continuously as distress can escalate mid-session, scan for the
crisis triggers below. CFT self-help is educational, not treatment. Much distress (shame,
self-criticism, low self-worth, ordinary anxiety and low mood, perfectionism) is within
scope and the skill should just help. The flags below are the exceptions, and CFT has a
larger clinician-only zone than most modalities, so read this carefully.

## Crisis triggers, stop and escalate

If any of these appear, **stop the practice** and shift to support and signposting. Do not
run an imagery exercise on active suicidality.

- Any mention of suicide, wanting to not exist, self-harm, or feeling there's no point
  going on, **including the indirect / passive forms**: "everyone'd be better off without
  me", "I wouldn't mind not waking up", "tired of being here". Shame populations are at
  elevated risk and may voice this obliquely ("I'm a burden", "the world would be cleaner
  without me"). Watch for the same indirect forms in whatever language the user writes in.
- Plans or means to hurt themselves, or that they're at risk right now.
- Severe, immobilizing depression, or talk of harming someone else.
- Acute crisis where they can't keep themselves safe.

What to do: respond like a person, not a script. Acknowledge it directly and without
panic — and with exactly the warmth this skill trains, which matters most here. Make clear
this is past what self-help should carry alone, and that reaching a human matters more
than any technique. **Match their language**: deliver all of this in the language they are
writing in. Then surface help, localized to where they are (next section).

**If the risk is toward someone else** (not themselves): same calm, drop the practice, but
the move is de-escalation and reaching emergency services / a human now (the local
emergency number), not a suicide line.

Do not try to "fix" it with compassion exercises. Stay with them, signpost, and gently
keep checking they're safe. Lower-risk stabilizing skills (SRB, grounding 5-4-3-2-1, paced
breathing) are fine as a bridge, not as a treatment.

## Surfacing crisis help, localized

Crisis numbers are country-specific and they change, so don't recite one from memory and
don't hand someone a hotline that won't connect from where they are. Localize:

- **Use what's on record.** If the user's country / region is in `CLAUDE.md`, lead with
  the resources recorded there: the local emergency number and a verified local crisis
  line. If location isn't recorded, ask once, briefly and calmly, where they are (or infer
  it), then continue — don't stall the response on it.
- **Verify before you give a number.** Web-search the current crisis line for their
  country rather than reciting one, then record the verified number(s) in `CLAUDE.md` so
  the next session already has them. [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com) is a
  reliable directory covering most countries and works as a universal fallback.
- **Give the universal emergency number** for their region as the immediate-danger route:
  112 across the EU and much of the world, 911 in the US / Canada, 999 in the UK, 000 in
  Australia, or the local equivalent. The CFT catalog lists Samaritans 116 123 (UK) and
  988 (US Suicide and Crisis Lifeline) — verify before quoting.
- **Point to a human they know.** Encourage contacting their therapist, doctor, or someone
  they trust today; if `CLAUDE.md` records a specific person or clinician, name them.

## Shame and low mood vs crisis, the call that matters

CFT works with people who feel worthless, and "worthless" sits close to the crisis line.
Distinguish carefully. "I'm not good enough / I'm a failure / I hate myself / I'm a
burden" *as a felt self-evaluation* is shame and self-criticism — the core CFT target,
in scope, route to `shame-self-criticism.md`. "There's no point in me being here / I don't
want to be alive / the world is better without me" is about their life — that is a crisis
trigger. When it's genuinely ambiguous (and with shame it often is), ask one calm, direct
screening question before routing ("When you say you're a burden, are you having any
thoughts of not wanting to be alive?") and let the answer decide. Asking does not plant
the idea. A clear "no, I just feel awful about myself" lets you treat it as shame; don't
over-escalate ordinary shame into a crisis script either.

## Clinician-only protocols, deliver only psychoeducation

These are designed to be run by a CFT-trained clinician and can transiently *raise*
distress, sometimes sharply. **Do not walk the user through them as self-help.** When the
routed problem points here, give the psychoeducation and the framing, say plainly that the
full protocol wants a trained clinician, and offer the lower-risk adjacent CMT skills
instead (`advanced-clinician.md` carries the detail).

- **Chairwork** (multiple-selves / three-chair self-criticism dialogue): can produce
  overwhelming distress; clients may dissociate or fragment. Requires skilled in-person
  management and stabilization. Self-help offers the Compassionate Self and Compassionate
  Other instead.
- **Feared-compassion (FBR) work:** deliberately introduces compassion as a feared
  stimulus and engages the origins of the threat response — exposure-shaped, needs a
  therapist to manage the backdraft. Self-help: name the fear/block/resistance, normalize
  it, slow the pace, and work the least-threatening flow (compassion to others).
- **Shame-memory imagery rescripting** (and compassionate dialogue with the child self):
  deliberate engagement with traumatic material; the compassionate self must be robustly
  established first. Clinician-only. Self-help: SRB and safe-place stabilization only.
- **All trauma / PTSD processing** (`trauma-ptsd.md`) and **all psychosis work / CFTp**
  (`psychosis-personality.md`): clinician-led. Offer grounding, SRB, and psychoeducation;
  do not run personified imagery or trauma processing.
- **Eating disorders** (`eating-disorders.md`): out of scope here; needs specialist CFT-E
  and medical monitoring. Signpost to professional assessment; do not run the practices as
  a weight/eating intervention.

## Backdraft and feared compassion, expected, not an emergency

Distinct from a crisis: when warmth enters a defended system, stored grief, fear, anger,
or numbness can surface — Gilbert's "backdraft", and the fears/blocks/resistances to
compassion (FBRs). This is **expected and is not a reason to escalate or stop the skill**;
it is the work showing up. The move is to slow down, return to SRB, validate and de-shame
the reaction ("it makes sense that warmth feels unsafe given what you've described"), and
step down the ladder — not to push through. Only escalate per the crisis triggers above if
genuine risk appears. The deeper FBR *intervention* is clinician-guided; what you do in
self-help is notice, normalize, and pace gently (`walkthrough-protocol.md`,
`advanced-clinician.md`).

## What is self-help-safe

Generally safe for self-directed practice, with the cautions above held live: Soothing
Rhythm Breathing, safe/soothing-place imagery, compassionate colors, compassionate letter
writing, compassionate mindfulness, the compassionate kitbag, and — gently, watching for
backdraft — the compassionate other, compassionate self, and the three flows. If any
exercise produces strong distress: stop, return to natural breathing, ground physically
(feet on floor, an object in the hand), and step down the ladder.

## The framing to hold

Treat the user as a capable adult and a collaborator, not a patient to be managed — and
meet them with the warmth the model is built on. But "self-help" doesn't mean "alone with
a crisis", and CFT's deepest techniques genuinely need a trained therapist and a
therapeutic relationship that is itself part of the mechanism. The flags above are how you
notice the moment self-help stops being the right tool and a human needs to be in the room.
