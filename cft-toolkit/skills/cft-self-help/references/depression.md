## How to use this file

The depression layer — specifically the **shame- and self-criticism-driven** low mood CFT
addresses: heaviness maintained by a threat system kept hot by self-attack and a soothing
system that's gone quiet. This is not a replacement for behavioral activation (that's a CBT
strength — see the `cbt-toolkit` skill); CFT's contribution is for when the low mood is
wrapped in shame and self-criticism, and warmth toward the self is what's missing. Assumes
`model.md` and `core-cmt.md`. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`. **Safety:** severe
depression or any suicidal thoughts need a clinician and crisis help, not this skill — drop
the practice and follow `safety.md`.

## Why "not your fault" comes first

Depression is typically compounded by *shame about being depressed* ("I'm pathetic for not
coping", "everyone else manages"). So the first intervention is the de-shaming model
(`model.md` 1-2): the low mood is a threat-/soothing-system imbalance, not a moral failure.
Lifting the second layer — the shame about the depression — is often where the felt shift
begins.

## The sequence

Above the shared baseline (psychoeducation + SRB):

1. **Safe / Soothing Place** (`core-cmt.md` 2) — re-open some soothing-system access in a
   system that's gone flat. A gentle, low-demand rung that asks little of a depleted person.
2. **Compassionate Self** (`core-cmt.md` 5) — build the warm, wise, committed identity and
   let it meet the depressive self-talk with understanding rather than argument. The
   commitment quality matters here: compassion includes the courage to act, not just
   softness.
3. **Compassionate Letter Writing** (`core-cmt.md` 8) — the compassionate self writes to the
   depressed self: validating how hard it is, why it makes sense, and gently encouraging one
   values-consistent step. Re-readable in the low moments.

**Chairwork** with the self-critic is a second-line CFT depression move but is
**clinician-only** (`advanced-clinician.md`); name it for therapy if psychoeducation and
imagery aren't enough.

## If it stalls

- Too depleted to hold imagery → stay with SRB and the compassionate-self *posture and
  expression* (body-first), or use the **Compassionate Kitbag** (`core-cmt.md` 10) as a
  physical anchor; keep it tiny.
- "Nothing's worth it / I can't be bothered" dominates (anhedonia, withdrawal) → that's
  behavioral activation territory, a CBT strength; consider the `cbt-toolkit` skill alongside
  this (`routing.md`).
- The letter turns self-critical → re-establish the Compassionate Self (`core-cmt.md` 5)
  before resuming; notice the tone aloud.
- Warmth toward the self triggers threat / numbness (backdraft) → normalize, step down to
  SRB, work compassion-to-others first (`core-cmt.md` 6, `model.md` 4).
