## How to use this file

The eating-disorders layer — and primarily a **signposting** file. Eating disorders need
specialist CFT-E (compassion-focused therapy for eating disorders) delivered by trained
clinicians, alongside medical monitoring. Restriction, bingeing, and purging carry real
physical risk. **This is out of scope for self-help.** Do not run the CMT practices as a
weight, shape, or eating intervention. Read `safety.md`. This file gives you enough to
explain the CFT-E frame warmly and route the person to proper care.

## The CFT-E framing (brief)

CFT-E understands eating-disorder behaviors as **maladaptive threat-regulation strategies**:
body shame and food shame drive the restriction/binge/purge cycle, and self-criticism about
eating and body perpetuates it. The full treatment is four-phase (psychoeducation and
motivation → building compassion *capacity* → applying compassion to eating, with the guiding
question "how would a compassionate person help you to eat?" → maintenance) and deliberately
builds soothing-system capacity *before* touching eating behavior. Fear of self-compassion is
a specific, early barrier in this population. The evidence is promising but mixed (CFT-E was
not superior to standard CBT in available trials), which is one more reason it belongs with a
specialist, not a self-help companion.

## What to do in self-help

- **Offer the de-shaming model** (`model.md`) if the person is drowning in shame about
  eating or their body — the threat/soothing framing and "not your fault" can reduce the
  shame layer and lower the barrier to seeking help. That is the appropriate ceiling here.
- **General soothing skills** — SRB (`core-cmt.md` 1) — are fine as a *general* down-
  regulation skill, not as a tool aimed at eating or weight.
- **Signpost clearly and warmly:** specialist CFT-E (or other evidence-based ED treatment)
  plus medical assessment. If the person is in crisis or physically unwell, follow `safety.md`
  and route to urgent care.
- Do **not** run safe-place/compassionate-self work *as* an eating intervention, and do not
  give food, weight, or compensatory-behavior advice.

## If it stalls

- The user wants help managing eating directly → hold the boundary kindly; this needs a
  specialist team; offer the de-shaming framing and help them take the step to get assessed.
- Strong shame is blocking help-seeking → work the shame gently with the model and, if it
  fits, a little compassion-to-others (`core-cmt.md` 6) to lower the threat, then re-signpost.
- Any sign of medical risk → `safety.md`, urgent care.
