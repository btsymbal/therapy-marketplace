## How to use this file

Brief adaptation notes for populations where the *delivery* of CMT changes even though the
core practices and model are the same. Use it as an overlay on the relevant condition file
(shame, anxiety, depression) and `core-cmt.md`, not as a standalone protocol. Several of
these populations (especially adolescents and perinatal) are usually run as clinician-led
*groups*; in self-help you adapt tone and framing and signpost where appropriate. Read
`safety.md`.

## Adolescents

Key adaptations: **simplify** the exercises; develop emotional vocabulary *before* compassion
training; lead with **warmth over rational explanation** (the felt experience matters more
than the model for younger clients); prefer concrete and visual content; and use
**role-reversal** rather than chairwork for younger clients (chairwork remains clinician-only
regardless, `advanced-clinician.md`). Where parents are involved, they act as "proto-
therapists" supporting practice between sessions. Note one documented caution: internet/group
CFT for young people has shown an occasional paradoxical group-level mood dip — pace gently,
keep it warm and concrete, and watch the felt response.

## Perinatal

Perinatal CFT (P-CFT) is delivered as a facilitated group adapted to the perinatal context;
the standard CMT content (psychoeducation, SRB, safe place, compassionate self) is the
backbone, with extra attention to the shame and threat that cluster around new parenthood
("I'm not a good enough parent"). In self-help, the shame and anxiety layers
(`shame-self-criticism.md`, `anxiety.md`) plus SRB and safe place are the safe core; signpost
to perinatal mental-health services where available, and apply `safety.md` vigilantly (the
perinatal period carries elevated risk).

## Older adults

CFT is feasible and well-received with older adults; the main adaptation is **pacing for
cognitive capacity** — shorter steps, more repetition, concrete language — and otherwise the
standard ladder applies. The de-shaming model and SRB tend to land well.

## If it stalls

- The model is too abstract (adolescents, cognitive limits) → drop it; build warmth directly
  through SRB, safe place, and concrete kind moments, and let the felt experience carry it.
- A group/clinician-led protocol is clearly indicated (perinatal, complex adolescent cases) →
  offer the safe core and signpost to the appropriate service.
- Any elevated-risk signal (perinatal especially) → `safety.md`.
