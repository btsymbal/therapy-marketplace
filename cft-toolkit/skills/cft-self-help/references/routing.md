## The baseline every route shares

Unlike a symptom-specific toolkit, **every CFT route starts from the same two moves**
regardless of the problem: the de-shaming psychoeducation (three circles, tricky brain,
"not your fault" — `model.md`) and Soothing Rhythm Breathing (`core-cmt.md`, 1). The
condition tables below show what is added *above that baseline*, not a replacement for it.
SRB must be reliably accessible before you introduce any imagery (`model.md`, the ladder).

## How to route

The user won't name a diagnosis — they'll name a feeling or a self-judgment ("I'm a
fraud", "I can't stop hating myself", "I freeze when people look at me", "I know it's not
true but I still feel disgusting"). Your job: from what they say plus the concrete moment,
identify the **problem area**, then reach for that area's **first-line CMT subset** and
open its file. Pick one practice to start, not a menu. Re-route freely if their answers
point elsewhere.

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) and get a feeling + 0-100 intensity + a concrete
moment first, and clock whether shame / self-criticism is the engine (`SKILL.md` flow
steps 1-2).

| Long-standing shame-based identity, very high threat reactivity, can't access imagery without flooding | Personality / complex presentation | `psychosis-personality.md` (clinician-guided) |
| Anger building, "I'm about to blow", anger that's really a response to feeling shamed | Anger | `anger-perfectionism.md` |
| Perfectionism, driving themselves, "I have to be flawless or I'm nothing" | Perfectionism | `anger-perfectionism.md` |
| Pain or illness pulling mood down, body as threat, shame about being unwell | Chronic pain / illness | `pain-illness.md` |
| Adolescent, perinatal, or older-adult context needing adaptation | Special population | `special-populations.md` |
| "Logic isn't changing the feeling", knows it rationally but still feels worthless / unsafe | The core CFT indication | `shame-self-criticism.md` (or the matching condition file) |

If two rows fit, ask one clarifying question to pick, or start with the one tied to the
most acute feeling. Shame underlies most CFT presentations — when in doubt and shame is
present, `shame-self-criticism.md` is the safe default.

## Problem area → first-line technique → file

The subset to reach for **first** in each area, above the shared baseline (psychoeducation
+ SRB). The core CMT practices live in `core-cmt.md`; condition files add sequencing and
emphasis.

| Problem area | First-line CMT subset (above baseline) | Files |
|---|---|---|
| Shame / self-criticism | Compassionate Self → (chairwork is clinician-only) + Compassionate Letter Writing as consolidation | `shame-self-criticism.md` + `core-cmt.md` |
| Shame-driven depression | Safe/Soothing Place + Compassionate Self + Compassionate Letter Writing | `depression.md` + `core-cmt.md` |
| Transdiagnostic anxiety | Safe Place + Compassionate Self + compassionate behavioral engagement replacing avoidance | `anxiety.md` + `core-cmt.md` |
| Social anxiety | Compassionate Other / Ideal Compassionate Image ("felt safety without words") + Compassionate Self | `anxiety.md` + `core-cmt.md` |
| OCD (treatment-resistant) | Compassionate Self (wisdom, strength, commitment) + Compassionate Letter Writing; FBR work is clinician-only | `anxiety.md` + `core-cmt.md` (see `safety.md`) |
| Trauma shame / PTSD | SRB + Safe Place stabilization + psychoeducation only; rescripting & chairwork are clinician-only | `trauma-ptsd.md` (see `safety.md`) |
| Anger / perfectionism | Three circles (anger / perfectionism as protective threat-/drive-strategies) + SRB + Compassionate Self | `anger-perfectionism.md` + `core-cmt.md` |
| Chronic pain / illness | SRB + Safe Place + three circles (illness as threat activator) + Compassionate Self | `pain-illness.md` + `core-cmt.md` |

## Is this actually a CBT job?

CFT is the right tool when the maintenance factor is **shame, self-criticism, or fear of
self-compassion** — when felt warmth and safeness are what's missing, and cognitive
reappraisal has already failed to move the feeling. If the distress is really a discrete
distorted thought that would yield to evidence-testing, a phobia maintained by avoidance, a
solvable practical problem, or panic needing interoceptive work, that is a CBT job — say so
and point to the `cbt-toolkit` skill rather than running compassion practices on it. The
two plugins are siblings; don't force one to do the other's work.

## Multi-problem and re-routing

- Most sessions centre on one problem; many are layered (shame *and* social anxiety; low
  mood *and* self-attack). Work one at a time, the most acute first. Name the others and
  offer to come back.
- If a walk-through reveals the real driver is different from where you started (you opened
  on anxiety, it's actually shame), say so plainly and switch files. Don't finish the wrong
  practice for tidiness.
- **When warmth activates threat** (backdraft / an FBR surfaces), don't re-route to a
  "stronger" technique — step *down* the ladder to SRB or safe place and pace gently
  (`safety.md`, `advanced-clinician.md`).
- **When distress is too high to imagine** (flooded, can't hold an image), don't start
  imagery: SRB and grounding first, then the kitbag (`core-cmt.md` 10) as a body anchor,
  and only then climb back to imagery.
- **When a flow is blocked** (the user can give compassion to others but not receive it, or
  can't direct any toward themselves), follow the three-flows order: out to others first,
  then receiving, then self (`core-cmt.md` 6-8). Don't push self-compassion on someone who
  can't yet access the easier flows.
