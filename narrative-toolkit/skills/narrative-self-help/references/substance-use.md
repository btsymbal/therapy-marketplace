## How to use this file

The substance use and addiction layer. NT's conceptualization: the **addiction story** has recruited the person into an identity — "I'm an addict" — and recovery requires **re-authoring** that identity, not just changing a behavior ("the recovering addict must become another person"). The work separates the person from the addiction story and builds an alternative account of who they are and are becoming. Assumes the six maps in `core-maps.md`. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one question per turn, decentered, wait.** **Safety:** functional analysis and structured treatment want a clinician; withdrawal from some substances is medically dangerous — signpost professional/medical support and don't position this as the treatment (`safety.md`). The self-help-safe NT layer is externalizing, re-authoring, consulting-your-consultants, and letters.

## 1 · Externalizing the addiction story

**Reach for it when:** the person is fused with "addict" as an identity. Externalize the substance/story as separate (`core-maps.md` Map 1), with full personification (it makes promises, it lies, it has tactics) — but **preserving accountability** for actions taken (`safety.md`).
**Walk it through:**
1. Name it — "the Drink", "the Craving", "the Habit", or their own term. Ask: *"That pull — if it were a separate operator working on you, what would you call it?"*
2. Expose its tactics (personification questions): *"What does it promise? Has it delivered? How does it convince you that it *is* you? Whose interests does it serve?"*
3. Map effects and counter-influence — every time they said no, or delayed, or walked away.
4. Position and justify (Stages 3-4): *"Is the person the Drink is turning you into the one you want to be?"*

**Accountability note:** externalizing the craving must never become "the Drink made me do it" as an excuse for harm to others — keep the person as the agent of their choices (`safety.md`).

## 2 · Re-authoring an alternative identity

**Reach for it when:** building the "another person" recovery requires (`core-maps.md` Map 2). Find moments — past or present — that belong to the person they're becoming, and thicken them into a history with a theme and a title.
**Walk it through:** unique outcomes (times they resisted, or lived by a different value) → both landscapes → link across time → *"If the person who keeps choosing differently had a name or a story, what is it?"* Project forward with unique-possibility questions (`cross-cutting-tools.md`).

---

## 3 · Consulting your consultants; letters

**Reach for it when:** the person has hard-won knowledge worth elevating. **Consulting-your-consultants** questions (`cross-cutting-tools.md` type 7) move them from patient to expert: *"From everything the Drink has taught you about how it operates, what would you tell someone it's just starting to recruit?"* This builds authority over the addiction story. **Letters** — to the substance, from the future recovered self — and **counter-documents** (a declaration of independence) anchor it (`documents.md`). Lived-experience archives and peer knowledge (the **Archive of Resistance** model, `collective-practices.md`) fit naturally here.

## Honest evidence and safety

Very limited. Shakeri et al. 2019 is the only controlled NT study for substance use (N=26, a hybrid NT+REBT protocol, Iran, single-centre) — consider NT for substance use **exploratory**. It is an adjunct to, not a replacement for, proper treatment, mutual-aid/peer support, and medical care for withdrawal. Keep `safety.md` live.
