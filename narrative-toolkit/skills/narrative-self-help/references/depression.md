## How to use this file

The depression layer: low mood, heaviness, withdrawal, "what's the point" about effort, the problem-saturated story "I'm just a depressed/worthless person". NT conceptualizes depression not as a biological thing intrinsic to the person but as a set of ideas and practices that have **dominated the life story** and recruited the person into a narrow account of themselves. Assumes the six maps in `core-maps.md`; this file adds the depression-specific flavor. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one question per turn, decentered, wait.** **Safety:** severe depression or any suicidal thought needs a clinician and crisis help, not this skill — drop the work and follow `safety.md`. Distinguish "pointless effort" (anhedonia, in scope) from "no point living" (crisis) — see `safety.md`.

## 1 · Externalizing "the Depression"

**Reach for it when:** the start of almost any depression work — the person is fused with the low mood ("this is just who I am now"). Separate it first (`core-maps.md` Map 1).
**Walk it through:**
1. Negotiate a name in their words — "the Depression", "the Weight", "the Fog", "the Flatness". Ask: *"If this heaviness were a thing sitting beside you rather than inside you, what would you call it?"*
2. Map its effects (relative influence): *"What does the Fog get you to stop doing? What does it tell you about yourself? When did it first move in?"*
3. Map counter-influence — this is where unique outcomes hide: *"When has the Fog wanted you to stay in bed and you got up anyway, even once?"*
4. Take a position and justify it: *"Is the way it's shrinking your life okay with you?"* → *"What does that 'no' say about what you actually want your days to hold?"*

**If it stalls:** name feels forced → try metaphor or imagery, or go smaller and more concrete; nothing the Fog hasn't taken → widen to before it arrived ("who were you before it moved in?"), or go to **Absent-But-Implicit** (`cross-cutting-tools.md`).

## 2 · Reconceptualization (past self vs present self)

**Reach for it when:** the person can contrast a former self with now, or notices any change — this is NT's identified mechanism for depression. The one retrieved NT-vs-CBT comparison (Fernández-Navarro et al. 2018) found that "reconceptualization innovative moments" — moments where the client contrasts past and present self and *explains the change* — predicted symptom improvement. So actively invite and elaborate them.
**Walk it through:**
1. Catch a contrast: *"You said you'd have handled that differently a year ago — what's different about how you did it this time?"*
2. Have them explain the change, in their words (don't explain it for them): *"How did you get from that old way to this one? What did you have to come to know or decide?"*
3. Move it into identity (`core-maps.md` Map 2, Landscape of Identity): *"What does being able to make that shift say about you?"*
4. Historicize and project forward (`cross-cutting-tools.md` question types 4 & 7).

**If it stalls:** no contrast available → run externalizing (1) and unique outcomes (3) to generate one, then come back.

## 3 · Unique outcomes against the Depression

**Reach for it when:** any moment, however small, the Depression didn't fully win — the entry point to re-authoring (`core-maps.md` Maps 5 → 2). In depression these are easy to discount ("that doesn't count, anyone could get out of bed"), so name and slow down on them.
**Walk it through:**
1. Find one: *"Was there a moment this week the Fog told you not to bother, and you did the thing anyway?"* (action, intention, feeling, or relational — all count).
2. Resist the discount: *"The Fog wants to call that nothing — but you did it while carrying all this weight. How did you manage that?"*
3. Both landscapes, then link to other such moments to build a history (`core-maps.md` Map 2).
4. Let them title the emerging story (e.g. "the days I take back").

**If it stalls:** everything gets discounted → use experience-of-experience questions (*"who wouldn't be surprised you did that?"*, `cross-cutting-tools.md`); thinking is too heavy → don't force it, acknowledge the weight and keep the externalizing stance; consider a therapeutic letter to hold the one moment that did surface (`documents.md`).

## Honest evidence

Limited. One controlled NT-vs-CBT comparison (Fernández-Navarro et al. 2018 — both effective; reconceptualization the identified NT mechanism); one quasi-experimental study of a structured 8-session program (NTEA, n=50). No NT-specific meta-analysis; no guideline specifically recommends White/Epston NT for depression. Offer this as a meaningful way to relate to the depression's story, not a guaranteed treatment, and keep `safety.md` live.
