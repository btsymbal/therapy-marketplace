## Read the safety boundary first

**Trauma work is clinician-guided.** This file gives the framing and the safe, stabilizing layer only — do **not** run trauma processing as self-help. Trauma-focused work can transiently raise distress before it helps. If trauma is the presenting issue, offer grounding/stabilization and the NT framing, and route to a trained clinician. See `safety.md`. **Note the distinction:** this is White/Epston NT for trauma, which does *not* revisit the traumatic event. **Narrative Exposure Therapy (NET)** is an entirely different, manualized, exposure-based protocol — clinician-only — covered in `net.md`; never conflate them or run NET here.

## How NT approaches trauma (and how it differs from exposure)

NT's approach to trauma is structurally different from trauma-focused CBT or NET: **it does not directly revisit or process the traumatic event.** Instead it works on the trauma's *effects* on identity and relationships — externalizing those effects, finding the values implied in the distress, reconnecting the person with supportive figures, and getting the preferred identity socially witnessed. The aim is to loosen the trauma's grip on the person's account of who they are, not to re-expose them to the memory.

---

## 1 · Externalizing the trauma's effects (not the event)

**Reach for it when:** the trauma's effects have colonized identity ("I'm damaged", "it's who I am now"). Externalize the *effects*, carefully (`core-maps.md` Map 1). **Never** externalize in a way that implies the survivor caused or "let in" the harm, and never dissolve a perpetrator's accountability (`safety.md`).
**Walk it through (safe layer):**
1. Name an *effect* as separate — "the Hypervigilance", "the Shame it left", "the Numbness". Ask: *"What would you call the shadow it left over how you see yourself?"*
2. Map what that effect does now, gently and without going into the event itself.
3. Map counter-influence: *"When has the Numbness expected you to disconnect and you reached for someone anyway?"*

**If it stalls or distress rises:** stop, ground (5-4-3-2-1, paced breathing), and route to a clinician. Stabilization over depth, always.

---

## 2 · Absent-But-Implicit

**Reach for it when:** the survivor can name the pain but no exception/strength will surface. The distress implies values the trauma couldn't erase (`cross-cutting-tools.md`). This is often the most workable safe move.
**Walk it through:** double-listen for what the pain implies they hold dear (safety, dignity, protecting others); reflect it back tentatively; trace its history; connect to figures who shared it. Keep it at the level of *values and connection*, not event detail.

---

## 3 · Re-membering and witnessing

**Reach for it when:** the survivor's "club of life" is dominated by negative/abusive figures, or they feel utterly alone in it.
**Walk it through:** **Re-Membering** (`core-maps.md` Map 3) to *downgrade* the membership weight of harmful figures and elevate even one figure who saw good in them. **Witnessing** (Map 4) gives the preferred, post-trauma identity social recognition — in self-help, via an imagined witness or a therapist-as-witness letter (`documents.md`), with real definitional ceremonies reserved for clinician/group settings. **The Tree of Life** (`collective-practices.md`) is designed precisely so trauma survivors approach identity through roots/skills/dreams *before* the wound — a safer entry than direct trauma talk.

## Honest evidence

Preliminary. Erbes et al. 2014 is the only retrieved pilot (n=14, pre-post, no control group). Clinician-guided only. The Ellis (2022) case recommends NT **integrated with DBT** (DBT for safety and emotion regulation) for clients with suicidality — NT alone is not a crisis intervention (`safety.md`). NT for trauma should not be undertaken as self-help; this file is framing and stabilization, with a clear handoff to a professional.
