## Read the safety boundary first

**Eating disorders require specialist involvement and medical monitoring.** NT for eating disorders is **not** a standalone self-help treatment, and its evidence base is rated "insufficient for conclusions" (Conti et al. 2022). Do not run it as treatment. What this file offers: the externalizing *framing* (which can reduce shame and self-blame) and a clear signpost to specialist assessment (CBT-E and medical care). If restriction, bingeing, purging, or medical risk is present, lead with the signpost. See `safety.md`.

This is also the domain with the **most developed NT technique cluster** (33 studies reviewed) — personified externalization, letters, the Anti-Anorexia/Anti-Bulimia Leagues, the Archive of Resistance, and Outsider Witnessing — so the framing below is rich, but it sits *alongside* specialist care, never instead of it.

## 1 · Personified externalizing ("Ana" / "Mia")

**Reach for it when:** the eating disorder is fused with identity and speaks in the first person ("I need to"). Personify and separate it (`core-maps.md` Map 1) — this is the foundational move here.
**Walk it through:**
1. Name it as a separate voice — many use "Ana" (anorexia) or "Mia" (bulimia), or their own name for it. Ask: *"That voice that sets the rules — if it were a separate character, what would you call it?"*
2. Expose its tactics (personification questions): *"What does Ana promise you? Has it kept those promises? How does it trick you into seeing yourself? Whose interests does it really serve?"*
3. Map its effects and the person's counter-influence — moments they defied a rule.
4. Position and justify (Stages 3-4): *"Is the life Ana is shrinking you toward the one you want?"*

**Caution:** keep this firmly paired with medical care; externalizing must never substitute for refeeding/medical safety.

## 2 · Letters to and from the eating disorder

**Reach for it when:** the person can put the relationship into writing (documented in 12 of 33 studies). Letters thicken the resistance between sessions (`documents.md`).
**Walk it through:** invite a letter *to* Ana (naming what it has cost them, declaring independence) and/or a letter *from* their preferred self answering it. Use their own words; acknowledge the pull honestly rather than pretending it's gone. A **counter-document** — a "Declaration of Independence from Ana" — can anchor the stand (`documents.md`).

## 3 · Archive of Resistance and consulting your consultants

**Reach for it when:** the person is far enough along to draw on, or contribute to, others' recovery knowledge. The **Anti-Anorexia/Anti-Bulimia League** (Epston; Madigan/Vancouver School) gathers letters, poems, and recordings from people resisting the disorder into a collective **"Archive of Resistance"**, circulating "solution knowledges" and making members "expert consultants" (`collective-practices.md`). In self-help, use **consulting-your-consultants** questions (`cross-cutting-tools.md` type 7): *"From everything you've learned fighting this, what would you tell someone Ana is just starting to recruit?"* — this elevates them from patient to authority and is itself anti-anorexic.

## Honest evidence

Largest NT study count (33, Conti et al. 2022) but explicitly **"insufficient for conclusions"**: only 3 of 33 used standardized measures; no RCTs; the largest quantitative study combined NT with motivational interviewing (not attributable to NT alone). Treat the framing as a shame-reducing adjunct to specialist, medically-monitored care — never a standalone treatment. Keep `safety.md` live.
