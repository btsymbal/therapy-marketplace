## How to use this file

Collective narrative practices are NT done with groups and communities — where identity claims are witnessed, peer solidarity is built, and "solution knowledges" circulate between people who share an experience. Most of these are inherently **group practices**: in a self-help setting you adapt the *individual* portions (e.g. drawing one's own Tree of Life, writing to an archive) and **signpost the genuine group form** where it adds something solo can't. Assumes the six maps in `core-maps.md` (especially Map 4, Definitional Ceremonies / Outsider-Witness). Deliver any individual portion per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one question per turn, decentered, wait.**

## 1 · The Tree of Life

**Reach for it when:** trauma, grief, refugee/displacement, chronic illness, or any time approaching identity head-on would be too direct or retraumatizing — the tree begins in *safe* territory (roots, skills, dreams) **before** the wound. Developed by Ncazelo Ncube (2006) with David Denborough for children affected by HIV/AIDS in South Africa. Works as a solo reflective exercise or in a group.

**The tree's parts and meanings:**

| Tree part | Stands for |
|-----------|-----------|
| Roots | Background, family history, influential past figures, cultural origins |
| Ground | Current context — where they live, daily activities |
| Trunk | Skills, abilities, values, principles |
| Branches | Hopes, dreams, wishes |
| Leaves | Important people (living or deceased) |
| Fruits | Gifts received from life or others (care, love, kindness) |
| Flowers / Seeds | Gifts given to others; contributions to community |

**The four-part group methodology:**
1. **Individual Tree Drawing** — each person draws their own tree using the structure above. *(This part works solo — walk the user through their tree, one part at a time, drawing out concrete people and moments.)*
2. **Forest of Life** — all trees displayed together; positive affirmations exchanged. Builds collective identity and peer solidarity. *(Group.)*
3. **Storms of Life** — collective reflection on difficulties faced, tools used, and how people cope — approached only after the identity foundation is laid. *(Group; solo-adaptable as gentle reflection once the tree is rich.)*
4. **Certificates and Songs (ceremony)** — a definitional ceremony honoring skills, values, bonds, dreams, and gifts, with witnesses (`documents.md` for certificates).

**If it stalls:** the tree feels thin → go more concrete on the trunk (specific skills, specific values with a moment attached); a part is empty (e.g. few leaves) → that itself is workable material, gently, not a failure. Other collective metaphor structures can substitute (Team of Life, Kite of Life, Kitchen of Life, Story Map).

## 2 · Anti-Anorexia / Anti-Bulimia Leagues and the Archive of Resistance

**Reach for it when:** eating-disorder recovery, where lived-experience knowledge is the authority (`eating-disorders.md`). Developed by David Epston; expanded by Stephen Madigan (Vancouver School). The League gathers letters, emails, recordings, songs, and poems from people resisting anorexia/bulimia into a collective **"Archive of Resistance"** that circulates "solution knowledges" and makes members **"expert consultants"** whose authority comes from lived experience. Two functions: (1) **community deconstruction** — reading others' stories, including the societal influences that recruited them; (2) **alternative-story thickening** — contributing creative writing that builds a *history* of anti-AN/BN achievements. In self-help, the individual move is **consulting-your-consultants** questions (`cross-cutting-tools.md` type 7) and contributing/​reading within an existing peer community.

## 3 · Definitional ceremonies in a group

The full group form of Map 4 (`core-maps.md`): roles (focal person, interviewer, two-to-three witnesses) rotate across sessions; witnesses respond only through the four categories (expression, image, resonance, transport) — never advice or applause. Witnesses may be practitioners, former clients experienced in the practice, family, friends, or community members. The structure deliberately promotes "transparency, accountability, and a reduced hierarchy". This is the practice to **signpost** when a person's preferred story genuinely needs a real audience; the solo adaptations are in `core-maps.md` Map 4.

## 4 · Other collective narrative practices

Beyond the Tree of Life, David Denborough and the Dulwich Centre developed: **Team of Life** (sports metaphors for teamwork and resilience), **Timeline**, **Story Map**, **Kite of Life**, **Kitchen of Life**, **Recipe of Life**, narrative songwriting, and **River of Memory** (developed for Rwandan genocide survivors). These are community-level approaches for groups sharing a collective experience, integrating NT with cultural and oral traditions. **Therapeutic letter-writing campaigns** (Madigan) — where members of a person's community, not just a therapist, write letters supporting their alternative story — are the most community-oriented document practice (`documents.md`).

## A note on the self-help boundary

**Evidence is limited and practice enthusiasm outstrips it.** Tree of Life studies have sample sizes of 1, 6, 8, and 29; no completed RCT exists; one controlled study (Randle-Phillips et al. 2016) found *no change* on quantitative wellbeing/self-esteem measures while qualitative data reported peer support and connectedness. Offer these as meaningful, connection-building practices — honestly framed — and be clear when a real group or facilitator is needed rather than a solo adaptation. For trauma, grief, or eating disorders, the group forms should be facilitator-led (`safety.md`).
