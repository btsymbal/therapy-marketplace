## How to use this file

The chronic illness layer (including cancer, MS, chronic pain, long-term conditions). NT adapts by **externalizing the illness and its effects from identity** — separating "I have MS / cancer / chronic pain" from "I *am* a sick person" — so the condition stops authoring the whole self. The aim isn't to deny the illness (it's real, often progressive) but to reclaim the parts of identity it has eclipsed. Assumes the six maps in `core-maps.md`. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one question per turn, decentered, wait.** Hold realistic expectations — NT helps the *relationship to* the illness and the surrounding identity, not the disease itself; don't promise symptom change. Coordinate with, never replace, medical care (`safety.md`).

## 1 · Externalizing the illness

**Reach for it when:** the illness has colonized identity and daily choices. Externalize it as separate (`core-maps.md` Map 1) — naming the illness's *effects* as the target, not pretending the diagnosis away.
**Walk it through:**
1. Name the effect-layer — "the Fatigue", "the Fog", "the way MS tries to run the schedule". Ask: *"What would you call the part of this that decides what you can and can't do?"*
2. Map its effects on identity, relationships, and what it's taken: *"What has it convinced you that you can no longer be?"*
3. Map counter-influence: *"When has it expected you to give up an activity and you found a way to keep some of it?"*
4. Position and justify: *"Is the size it's claiming in your life the size you want to give it?"*

**If it stalls:** the losses are real and large → don't minimize; externalize the *story* the illness tells about who they now are, while honoring genuine grief for what's changed (`grief.md` for the loss layer).

## 2 · "Taking the keys back" — unique outcomes

**Reach for it when:** looking for where the person still has agency despite the illness. The signature NT question here: *"When have you taken the keys back from the MS — a moment you, not it, decided how the day went?"* These unique outcomes (`core-maps.md` Map 5) are the entry to re-authoring an identity that *includes* the illness without being defined by it.
**Walk it through:** find a keys-back moment → both landscapes → link several into a history → title the story ("the life I still drive").

## 3 · Re-authoring and online witnessing

**Reach for it when:** building and confirming the preferred identity. **Re-Authoring** (`core-maps.md` Map 2) thickens the keys-back moments. For social confirmation, **online communities** have been proposed as a vehicle for outsider witnessing in chronic illness (Johnson et al. 2020) — peers who live the same condition can witness the preferred identity in a way few others can. In self-help, point toward an existing condition community, or use a therapist-as-witness letter / imagined witness (`core-maps.md` Map 4, `documents.md`). A **counter-document** recording capacities the illness hasn't taken can offset deficit-focused medical records (`documents.md`).

## Honest evidence

Limited. For cancer, Jones et al. 2026 reviewed 9 RCTs/quasi-experimental studies, all at high risk of bias — "efficacy could not be established", though NT's cultural responsiveness was noted as a potential benefit over manualized protocols. Offer the work as a meaningful way to reclaim identity alongside medical treatment, with no promise of symptom or disease change. Keep `safety.md` live.
