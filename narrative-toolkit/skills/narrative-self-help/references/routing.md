## How to route

The user won't name a diagnosis or a map, they'll tell a story about themselves or a situation ("I'm just a failure", "I'm broken since the breakup", "I can't stop being the anxious one", "I miss my dad and don't know how to carry him"). Your job: from what they say plus the concrete moment, identify the **problem domain**, then reach for that domain's **first-line map cluster** and open the right file. Pick one place to start, not a menu. Re-route freely if their answers point elsewhere.

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) and get the problem-saturated story + a concrete moment first (`SKILL.md` flow steps 1-2). The six maps themselves live in `core-maps.md`; the cross-cutting question tools (seven question types, Absent-But-Implicit) in `cross-cutting-tools.md`.

## Externalizing is almost always the first move

Across nearly every domain the foundational move is Externalizing (Map 1) — separate the person from the problem, negotiate an experience-near name, map its effects, and invite the person to take a position on it. It is what makes the rest possible (you can't find a unique outcome to a problem the person believes they *are*). Open there unless the work is specifically grief (often lead with Re-Membering) or a request for a document/witness. Externalizing is sustained as a *stance* throughout, not a one-time step.

## Multi-problem and re-routing

- Most sessions are one story; some are layered (depression *and* self-criticism; trauma's effects *and* grief). Work one at a time, the most acute first. Name the others and offer to come back.
- If a conversation reveals the real problem is different from where you started, say so plainly and switch files. Don't finish the wrong map for tidiness.
- If no unique outcomes surface no matter how you look, that's the cue for the **Absent-But-Implicit** technique (`cross-cutting-tools.md`) — the counter-story is implied in the distress itself.
- If the person is stuck at an abstract level and can't get to meaning, drop to a lower **Scaffolding** level (`core-maps.md` Map 6) — go concrete before going abstract.
- When distress is too high for any re-authoring, stabilize first (`safety.md`), then return.
