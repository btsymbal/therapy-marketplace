## How to use this file

The identity layer: cultural and racial identity, and LGBTQ+ / gender identity — anywhere a person is up against **dominant cultural discourses** that have pathologized, erased, or prescribed who they're "supposed" to be. NT is unusually well-suited here: its social-constructionist premises locate the problem in *culture and power*, not in the person, and its **anti-pathologizing stance** — refusing to treat a sexual, gender, racial, or cultural identity as a "condition" — is directly aligned with the experience of marginalization. NT work with Aboriginal Australian, Māori, and other Indigenous communities (Dulwich Centre) is foundational to the tradition. Assumes the six maps in `core-maps.md`. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one question per turn, decentered, wait** — and here especially, do not impose; the person is the authority on their own identity.

## 1 · Deconstructing the dominant discourse

**Reach for it when:** a person has absorbed a dominant narrative (heteronormativity, the gender binary, a racialized or colonial story, a "model minority" or assimilation script) as if it were truth about them. Deconstruction is the move: make the discourse *visible as a discourse* — something authored by power, not a fact — so it can be questioned.
**Walk it through:**
1. Externalize the *discourse*, not the person (`core-maps.md` Map 1): name it — "the Should-Be-Normal story", "the Binary", "the message that I'm too much / not enough". Ask: *"Where did that rule about who you're allowed to be come from — is it yours, or was it handed to you?"*
2. Map its effects and tactics: *"What does this story demand of you? Whose interests does it serve? What does it cost you to obey it?"*
3. Trace its history and cultural source — making clear the problem lives in the discourse, not in them.
4. Position and justify (Stages 3-4): *"Is this story one you choose to live by, or one you'd refuse if you could?"*

## 2 · Re-authoring the preferred identity

**Reach for it when:** the person is ready to author who they *prefer* to be, on their own terms. Re-Authoring (`core-maps.md` Map 2) builds the preferred gender / sexuality / cultural account from lived moments that already express it.
**Walk it through:** find unique outcomes — moments they lived as themselves, refused the script, or felt at home in their identity → both landscapes → link into a history → let them title it. Use unique-possibility questions (`cross-cutting-tools.md`) to open the future the preferred identity makes possible.

---

## 3 · Absent-But-Implicit beneath internalized oppression

**Reach for it when:** the person voices internalized shame or self-rejection and no positive identity claim will surface. **Absent-But-Implicit** (`cross-cutting-tools.md`) is a natural fit: the pain of internalized oppression implies a value, a dignity, a preferred way of being that the dominant story has buried. Double-listen for it, reflect it back, trace its history, connect them to others who hold it.

---

## 4 · Witnessing and community

**Reach for it when:** the preferred identity needs recognition by people who share it. **Outsider Witnessing** (`core-maps.md` Map 4) using **community members** is especially powerful here — being witnessed by others of the same culture, race, or queer/trans community confirms an identity in a way an outsider can't. **Intersectionality** matters: for QTPOC (queer and trans people of color) the discourses stack, and the work must hold more than one axis at once (Chan, Thacker Darrow & Duran 2025). In self-help, point toward affirming community and use imagined-witness or letter forms (`collective-practices.md`, `documents.md`).

## Honest evidence

Practitioner-consensus only. There is **strong theoretical alignment** and practitioner endorsement, but **no White/Epston NT outcome study** was found specifically for LGBTQ+/gender-diverse clients, nor controlled outcome trials for cultural/racial identity work. Offer this as a values-aligned, anti-pathologizing way to author identity, honestly noting the evidence is alignment and practice wisdom, not trials. Keep `safety.md` live (minority-stress distress can carry real suicidality risk).
