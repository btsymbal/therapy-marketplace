## How to use this file

The substance-use layer. The MBCT-derived program here is **Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention
(MBRP)** — manualized *separately* from standard MBCT as an 8-week post-treatment program, and
distinct from it. It assumes the core decentering stance from `model.md` and `core-practices.md`.
The self-help-safe layer is **urge surfing** and the **SOBER space**; the structured treatment
(functional analysis, formal relapse-prevention therapy) wants a clinician. Read `safety.md`:
active substance *dependence* is a standard exclusion from general MBCT and needs professional
treatment. Deliver per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one cue per turn, end on an invitation, wait.**

---

## The boundary

Say it plainly: MBRP is normally a post-treatment program run after, or alongside, structured
addiction treatment — it's not a standalone fix for active dependence. What this skill can safely
offer is two in-the-moment skills for riding out a craving (urge surfing, SOBER), framed as
decentering from the craving. For dependence, structured treatment and a clinician come first;
signpost accordingly.

## 1 · Urge surfing

**Reach for it when:** a craving is present — an urge to drink, smoke, pick, pull, scroll, use —
and the user wants to ride it out without acting on it.

**The frame:** a craving is a **transient mental and physical event** — it builds, peaks, and
subsides like a wave — not an emergency or a command that must be obeyed. Decentering from the
craving is the whole move.

**Walk it through (live, one cue at a time):**
1. "Notice the craving *as* a craving — a set of body sensations and thoughts — rather than as
   something you have to act on right now. Just name it: 'craving's here.'" Wait.
2. "Picture it as a wave in the ocean: it's building now, it'll reach a peak, and then it
   *always* subsides. You don't have to make it go — just watch it move." Wait.
3. "Bring attention to where you feel it in the body. Where is it? What's it like — tight, hot,
   restless? Does it shift as you watch it?" Wait.
4. "Use the breath as your surfboard — ride each in-breath and out-breath as the wave rises." 
5. "Watch it crest and start to fall. Notice: you can have this craving *and* not act on it.
   Both are true at once."

**If it stalls:** the urge keeps spiking → return to the breath anchor and stay with "it will
crest and fall"; you're outlasting it, not defeating it. Acting feels inevitable → use the SOBER
space (2) to insert a deliberate pause and choice point.

## 2 · The SOBER space

**Reach for it when:** a high-risk moment or a craving where the user needs a structured pause to
move from automatic reaction to deliberate choice. This is MBCT's 3-Minute Breathing Space adapted
for the addictions context.

**Walk it through (one letter at a time):**
1. **S — Stop.** "Whatever's happening, just pause. Stop the automatic momentum for a moment."
2. **O — Observe.** "What are you feeling, in the body and the mind? What's happening right now?
   Just notice it, without judging." Wait.
3. **B — Breathe.** "Bring attention to the breath as an anchor — a few breaths, riding the
   sensations." Wait.
4. **E — Expand.** "Widen awareness to the whole body and the situation around you — the bigger
   picture, not just the urge." Wait.
5. **R — Respond.** "From this wider, steadier place — what's a skillful response here? Not the
   automatic one; a chosen one." 

**If it stalls:** they can't get past Observe because the feeling's too strong → drop to feet-on-
floor sensory grounding first, then continue. The high-risk situation itself is escalating →
prioritize leaving it / reaching support over completing the practice.

## 3 · Relapse-prevention awareness

**Reach for it when:** consolidating, between cravings — building awareness of triggers and a
plan, ideally alongside professional treatment.

**Walk it through:** adapt the relapse-signature approach (`core-practices.md`, 1.15) to substance
use — identify personal high-risk situations, triggers, and the early thoughts/sensations that
precede use, and plan mindful responses (urge surfing, SOBER, reaching support) for each stage.
Record the accumulating picture in `CLAUDE.md` (`logging.md`).

**If it stalls:** there's active dependence or escalating use → this is past self-help; warmly
and concretely signpost structured addiction treatment and a clinician (`safety.md`).
