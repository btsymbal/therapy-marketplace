## How to use this file

The eating-disorders layer. **There is no validated MBCT protocol for eating disorders**, and
there's a specific reason for caution (the observing-facet paradox, below). Read `safety.md`:
eating disorders need specialist CBT-E / ED care and medical monitoring; this is largely out of
scope for self-help. This file exists so you handle an ED disclosure safely — caution, the right
emphasis if any mindfulness is used, and firm signposting. Deliver anything per
`walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one cue per turn, end on an invitation, wait.**

---

## The boundary

Say it plainly and kindly: eating disorders are serious conditions that need specialist
assessment and medical monitoring; they're not something to work through with a self-help
mindfulness skill. There's no validated MBCT-for-ED protocol, and some mindfulness facets can
even feed ED pathology (next section). The most useful thing this skill can do is encourage
specialist help and, *only* alongside it, emphasize the safer facets of mindfulness if the user
is already doing mindfulness work.

---

## 1 · The observing-facet paradox (why caution)

**Reach for it when:** explaining why the usual MBCT practices aren't simply recommended here.

**Convey, briefly:** in eating disorders, the **observing/monitoring** facet of mindfulness —
bare self-observation and body-monitoring — is associated with *worse* eating pathology, while
the **acceptance and non-judgment** facets are protective. So the standard "observe your body
sensations" instruction (e.g. an unmodified body scan) can backfire, heightening body
preoccupation. This is why a generic body-scan-first protocol is the wrong move for ED.

---

## 2 · What to emphasize if mindfulness is used

**Reach for it when:** the user is already in specialist care and wants a safer mindfulness
emphasis as an adjunct (not instead of treatment).

**Walk it through (lean toward acceptance and compassion, away from bare observation):**
- **Emphasize acceptance and non-judgment**, and **self-compassion** (loving-kindness, the kind
  curious stance of inquiry) — these are the protective, therapeutic facets for ED presentations.
- **Be cautious with the body scan** (`core-practices.md`, 1.2): monitor whether it heightens
  body-related preoccupation for this individual, and drop it if so. Favor breath, sounds, and
  self-compassion practices over body-monitoring.
- Frame practice as building a kinder relationship to oneself, not as another arena for
  monitoring or control.

**If it stalls:** body-focused practice increases preoccupation or distress → stop it; route to
self-compassion and to their specialist team.

---

## 3 · Signposting

**Reach for it when:** always, with any ED presentation.

Encourage specialist eating-disorder assessment and medical monitoring (CBT-E is the evidence-based
treatment). If there are signs of physical health risk, that needs prompt medical attention — see
`safety.md`. Stay warm and non-judgmental; the goal is to get them connected to the right care, not
to deliver a substitute for it.
