## How to use this file

The bipolar layer (MBCT-BD). **This is a clinician-guided protocol** — adjunct to pharmacotherapy,
delivered as a 12-session clinician-led program — not something to run as self-help. This file is
for understanding and signposting, plus the narrow self-help-safe layer. Read `safety.md`: an
**active manic/hypomanic episode is a contraindication**, and the all-day intensive retreat is
omitted for bipolar because stimulation can precipitate hypomania. Evidence is preliminary; the
most consistent benefit is anxiety reduction, and depression benefits are mixed. Deliver any
practice per `walkthrough-protocol.md`: **one cue per turn, end on an invitation, wait.**

---

## The clinician-only boundary

Say it plainly to the user: MBCT for bipolar disorder is an extended, clinician-led program
designed to run alongside medication and medical monitoring, with daily mood tracking and
personalized emergency plans. The full protocol is not safe or appropriate to self-administer.
What this skill can offer is **understanding of the approach** and a couple of low-intensity,
self-help-safe practices — and a strong steer toward a clinician and their existing care team.

If a manic or hypomanic episode is active or emerging, drop practice entirely and follow
`safety.md` (medication and clinical management come first).

## 1 · What MBCT-BD is (psychoeducation)

**Reach for it when:** the user wants to understand how mindfulness fits a bipolar condition.

**Convey, briefly:** MBCT-BD adapts the standard program for bipolar:
- Extended to **12 sessions**, adjunct to pharmacotherapy.
- **Daily mood monitoring from session 1**, to build baseline and early-warning awareness.
- **Emergency plans built early** (sessions 2–3), before the full skill set exists.
- **Shortened, alternated meditations early on** — body scans and sittings kept short and
  alternated with movement, to avoid prolonged inward attention when mood may be unstable.
- **Prodromal-mania recognition** added (see 3), plus structured problem-solving.
- **The all-day retreat omitted or modified** (stimulation risk).
- Later sessions add self-compassion, loving-kindness, and self-soothing for both depressive and
  hypomanic states.

## 2 · The self-help-safe layer

**Reach for it when:** the user, in a stable phase and ideally alongside their clinician, wants a
low-intensity practice between appointments.

**Walk it through (kept deliberately light):**
- The **regular 3-Minute Breathing Space** (`core-practices.md`, 1.8) as a brief, contained,
  three-times-daily practice — short by design, not prolonged inward sitting.
- Brief grounding / anchoring when distressed.
- Keep meditations **short and alternated** with gentle movement; avoid long body scans or
  extended sits, especially if mood is shifting.

**If it stalls:** mood is destabilizing, energy or sleep-need is changing → stop and route to 3
and `safety.md`; this is when a clinician needs to be involved, not when to push practice.

---

## 3 · Prodromal-mania awareness

**Reach for it when:** building early-warning awareness (best done with a clinician), in a stable
phase.

**Walk it through:** help the user identify their **personal prodromal signs of mania/hypomania**
— reduced need for sleep, increased energy, pressured speech, inflated self-esteem, racing
thoughts — and the mindful response: **reducing stimulation and activity** when these signs
appear, rather than riding the elevation. This mirrors the relapse-signature plan (1.15) but for
the *upswing* as well as the downswing, and belongs in the user's emergency plan with their care
team.

**If it stalls:** signs of an active episode → this is no longer prodromal awareness; follow
`safety.md` and steer to medical care immediately.
