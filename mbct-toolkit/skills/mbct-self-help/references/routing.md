## How to route

The user won't name a diagnosis — they'll name a feeling or a situation ("I can't stop
replaying it", "my mind won't switch off", "I feel like I'm sleepwalking through my days",
"the worry just spirals"). Your job: from what they say plus the concrete moment, identify the
**problem area**, then reach for that area's **first-line practice** and open its file. Pick one
practice to start, not a menu. Re-route freely if their answers point elsewhere.

Always do the safety scan (`safety.md`) and get a feeling + 0-100 intensity + a concrete moment
first (`SKILL.md` flow steps 1–2). And clock the MBCT-specific read: is the mind stuck in
**doing mode** (fixing, analyzing, ruminating) when **being mode** is what's needed? If acute
distress is live, **anchor first** — a breath/feet anchor or the responsive 3-Minute Breathing
Space (`core-practices.md`, 1.9) — before any turning-toward work.

## Multi-problem and re-routing

- Most sessions are one problem; some are layered (rumination *and* low mood; worry *and*
  self-criticism). Work one at a time, the most acute first. Name the others and offer to come
  back.
- If a walk-through reveals the real problem is different from where you started, say so plainly
  and switch files. Don't finish the wrong practice for tidiness.
- **Anchor before turning-toward, always.** If the user is flooded, don't open with allowing
  (1.13) or sounds-and-thoughts (1.5) — establish a steady anchor or run the responsive 3MBS
  (1.9) first, then climb (`model.md`, sequencing).
- **Rumination vs problem-solving.** If the churning is about a *real, solvable* problem (a true
  deadline, an actual undone task), mindfulness is the wrong tool — sitting with it won't solve
  it and may feel like avoidance. Name it and signpost problem-solving. MBCT is for the
  *unsolvable-by-thinking* churn (mood, regret, "what if", self-judgment).
- **"Thoughts are not facts" is the spine.** Across nearly every area, the underlying move is
  decentering (1.5, `model.md`). When in doubt about which practice, an anchor plus watching
  the thought as a mental event is rarely wrong.
- **Acute night vs chronic insomnia:** at 2am don't set up a multi-week practice schedule;
  decenter the "wrecked tomorrow" catastrophizing and allow wakefulness rather than fighting it
  in the moment (`insomnia.md`); reserve the routine-building for a planning session.
