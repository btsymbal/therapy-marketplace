## Read this first, every session

Before routing, and continuously as distress can escalate mid-session, scan for the crisis triggers below. BA self-help is educational, not treatment. Most low mood, withdrawal, anhedonia, lost routine, and everyday avoidance is well within scope and the skill should just help. The flags below are the exceptions — and because BA is most often reached for in depression, the suicide-risk scan matters more here, not less.

## Crisis triggers, stop and escalate

If any of these appear, **stop the technique** and shift to support and signposting. Do not run activity scheduling on active suicidality.

- Any mention of suicide, wanting to not exist, self-harm, or feeling there's no point going on, **including the indirect / passive forms**: "everyone'd be better off without me", "I wouldn't mind not waking up", "tired of being here". Watch for the same indirect forms in whatever language the user writes in, not just English.
- Plans or means to hurt themselves, or that they're at risk right now.
- Severe, immobilizing depression — can't get out of bed at all, not eating, not able to keep themselves safe.
- Talk of harming someone else.

What to do: respond like a person, not a worksheet. Acknowledge it directly and without panic. Make clear this is past what self-help should carry alone, and that reaching a human matters more than any technique here. **Match their language**: deliver all of this in the language they are writing in, never switch them to English to read the script. Then surface help, localized to where they are (next section).

**If the risk is toward someone else** (not themselves): same calm, drop the technique, but the move is de-escalation and reaching emergency services / a human now (the local emergency number), not a suicide line.

Do not try to "fix" it with BA. Stay with them, signpost, and gently keep checking they're safe. Lower-risk stabilizing skills (grounding 5-4-3-2-1, paced breathing) are fine as a bridge, not as a treatment. BA can support recovery *after* safety is established, not during a crisis.

## Surfacing crisis help, localized

Crisis numbers are country-specific and they change, so don't recite one from memory and don't hand someone a hotline that won't connect from where they are. Localize:

- **Use what's on record.** If the user's country / region is in `CLAUDE.md`, lead with the resources recorded there: the local emergency number and a verified local crisis line. If location isn't recorded, ask once, briefly and calmly, where they are (or infer it from the session), then continue, don't stall the response on it.
- **Verify before you give a number.** Web-search the current crisis line for their country rather than reciting one, then record the verified number(s) in `CLAUDE.md` so the next session already has them. [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com) is a reliable directory covering most countries and works as a universal fallback if you can't confirm a specific line.
- **Give the universal emergency number** for their region as the immediate-danger route: 112 across the EU and much of the world, 911 in the US / Canada, 999 in the UK, 000 in Australia, or the local equivalent. In the US, the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline is **988** (call or text). When in doubt, "your local emergency number" plus findahelpline.com is always safe.
- **Point to a human they know.** Encourage contacting their therapist, doctor, or someone they trust today; if `CLAUDE.md` records a specific person or clinician, name them.

## Low mood vs crisis, the one call that matters

This is the most important distinction in this whole skill, because BA's home turf is depression. "Pointless / nothing's worth it / what's the point" about **activities or effort** is anhedonia — ordinary depression, in scope, route to `depression.md` or `anhedonia.md`. "No point going on / in being here / in living" is about **their life** — that is a crisis trigger. When it's genuinely ambiguous, ask one calm, direct screening question before routing ("When you say pointless, are you having any thoughts of not wanting to be alive?") and let the answer decide. Asking does not plant the idea. A clear "no, just flat and can't be bothered" lets you de-escalate and treat it as low mood; don't over-escalate ordinary anhedonia into a crisis script either.

## Clinician-guided situations, deliver only the self-help-safe layer

These need a licensed clinician and, in some cases, medication or specialist care. **Do not run the full protocol as self-help.** When the situation is one of these, give the psychoeducation and the framing, say plainly that the full work wants a clinician, and offer the lower-risk adjacent BA steps instead.

- **Severe depression with little or no ability to initiate any behavior.** Professional guidance is needed to calibrate the Graded Task Assignment starting level and to decide whether medication should accompany BA. Self-help-safe: monitoring alone for a week, the very smallest micro-steps, and warm signposting — not a full scheduling program pushed onto someone who can't yet move.
- **PTSD / trauma-related avoidance.** BA has evidence in *veteran* populations; civilian, non-veteran PTSD BA evidence is limited. Trauma-specific treatments (Prolonged Exposure, CPT) should be considered alongside or instead of BA. Offer activity monitoring and gentle, *non-trauma* activation only; do not push approach toward trauma reminders as self-help — that's exposure, and it's clinician-guided. Comorbid TBI has a specialized protocol (CABA) that wants a clinician.
- **Substance use disorder.** BA alone is **not** a SUD treatment; it addresses the depression component. A comprehensive SUD program is required. Self-help-safe: values clarification toward substance-free activities, scheduling that competes with high-risk periods — framed as adjunct, not as the treatment for the substance use itself.
- **Postpartum depression.** Requires clinician guidance, especially with any postpartum psychosis, severe suicidal ideation, or inability to care for the infant. The "good-enough mom" framing and help-seeking-as-activation are supportive, but this is not a context to self-manage alone.
- **Anhedonia with medication-refractory depression.** The BATA protocol and a psychiatric consultation belong together; flag the consult rather than implying Dabbling/Savoring alone will resolve it.

## Honest expectation-setting

BA does not promise that any single activation will feel rewarding. The mechanism is restoring contact with a reinforcement environment **over time**, not immediate pleasure. Early activations may feel effortful, neutral, or even mildly worse before the mood-behavior loop begins to shift. **Predict this to the user explicitly** — it's a known place people quit, concluding "see, it didn't help". Naming it in advance is part of the safety of the approach: it stops one flat attempt from being read as proof BA is useless.

## The framing to hold

Treat the user as a capable adult and a collaborator, not a patient to be managed. The BA stance is itself non-pathologizing: depressed behavior is a reasonable response to a depleted reinforcement environment, not a character flaw — they're not broken, they're in a cycle, and behavior is the way out. Meet them where they are: terser reps for someone fluent in BA, a little more scaffolding for a newcomer. But "self-help" doesn't mean "alone with a crisis", the whole point of the flags above is to notice the moment self-help stops being the right tool and a human needs to be in the room.
