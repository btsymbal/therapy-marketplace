## How to use this file

The delivery-and-population layer — adaptations of how BA is delivered for specific groups and formats. It does **not** change the core techniques (run those from `core-techniques.md`, one step per turn per `walkthrough-protocol.md`); it changes session length, who's involved, the materials, and the channel. Use it to tune the standard work to the user in front of you.

## Older adults

BA is well-evidenced in older adults. Adaptations:
- **Standard Martell-style protocol** (functional analysis, activity registration, scheduling, relapse prevention).
- **Shorter sessions** — ~30 minutes can fit better than 50.
- **Telephone delivery** is effective and often preferred when mobility or transport is limited.
- **Account for functional limitations:** scheduling must fit physical limits; **mastery-oriented** activities (4) matter especially when physical decline limits pleasure options.
- **Early-stage dementia:** guided low-intensity BA with workbooks, caregiver involvement, initial face-to-face before transitioning to self-management, and culturally/linguistically adapted materials.

## Adolescents

- **A-BAP (Adolescent BA Program):** ~14 sessions, tailored to developmental stage.
- **Parent involvement:** dedicated sessions where parents learn to **reinforce activation** and avoid reinforcing avoidance (a contingency-management move, 15).
- **Extended life domains:** beyond the standard 10, add **peer relationships** and **school engagement** as their own domains, given developmental centrality.
- **Developmentally adapted language:** avoid clinical framing; use activity-tracking formats (app-based, informal logs) that feel natural.
- **School and peer targets:** scheduling explicitly targets attendance, peer activities, extracurriculars.
- **Autistic adolescents:** longer rapport-building before scheduling; emotional-literacy work (labeling internal states) before functional analysis; parent involvement; flexible/online delivery. (Evidence base here is very limited — proceed gently.)

## Primary care & low-intensity delivery

- **Stepped care:** a brief 2–4 session BA check-in, stepping up to the full protocol if response is insufficient.
- **Simplified materials:** low-literacy activity-monitoring forms; pictorial TRAP diagrams.
- **Integrate with physical-health appointments:** build scheduling around existing medical visits.
- This is the level at which BA is delivered by briefly-trained practitioners — its scalability is a feature, and it's why BA suits self-help.

## Group BA

As effective as individual BA. Format-specific mechanisms:
- **The group is itself a source of reinforcement** — peer validation, shared problem-solving, social contact. A genuine additive ingredient.
- **Shared functional analysis:** members spot each other's TRAP patterns, which builds insight and cuts shame.
- **Each member keeps their own hierarchy;** the group provides accountability.

## Internet, app, and telephone BA

- **Guided and minimally-guided internet BA (iBA)** is effective versus inactive controls; **unguided** iBA did not reach significance. Browser-based delivery slightly outperformed smartphone apps.
- **Telephone BA** is effective, notably in older-adult primary care, and widens access.
- The practical implication for this self-help skill: **guidance matters** — the interactive, step-by-step walk-through (the whole point of this skill) is closer to the guided forms that work than to unguided self-administration.

## Cultural adaptations

- **Values assessment must be culturally calibrated** (3): life areas and their relative weight differ across contexts — collectivist values may rank higher, individualistic achievement domains lower.
- **Activities must be culturally available and acceptable** — schedule from what's actually on offer in their world.
- **Adapt psychoeducation language and metaphors** to context.
- **Low-literacy populations:** pictorial materials, simplified forms, verbal rather than written homework.
- **Collective cultures:** social-network engagement is both a therapeutic target and a delivery mechanism.

## The honest nuance to say out loud

Internet-delivered BA showed **no significant effect at 6-month follow-up** in the available meta-analysis — a real durability gap. Unguided, ongoing self-administration may not maintain gains. Where BA is delivered at a distance or through an app, it should come with ongoing check-in or support built in. For this skill, that means: encourage continuity (the `ba-log/` + `CLAUDE.md` memory exists partly for this), and don't imply a one-off session is a course of treatment.
