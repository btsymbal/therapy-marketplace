## How to use this file

Depression is BA's primary target — most of this skill's sessions land here. This file adds the depression-specific *shape* on top of the universal toolkit; it does **not** restate the techniques. Run them from `core-techniques.md` by number, delivered one step per turn per `walkthrough-protocol.md`. **Safety:** severe depression with no initiative, or any suicidal thought, wants a clinician and crisis help, not this skill — drop the technique and follow `safety.md`.

## The BA read on depression

Hold and transmit this framing, because it changes what the user does: depression is a **learned behavioral pattern maintained by current behavior-environment interactions**, not a flaw or a willpower deficit. The recovery mechanism is **behavior change**, not insight or motivation. The non-pathologizing line lands: "you're not broken — you're in a cycle, and behaviour is the lever." (Full model in `foundations.md`.) Recovery runs outside-in: act, then mood follows.

## The session arc (where a course of BA goes)

Not a script for one session — the map of how a course of BA unfolds, so you know what comes next and don't over-schedule too early.

- **Early (assessment & psychoeducation):** give the BA rationale (outside-in, the avoidance cycle). Start **Activity Monitoring** (1). Then establish a **routine** (10) and introduce **Mastery & Pleasure** (4); a first, light **functional analysis** (2).
- **Middle (active scheduling & avoidance work):** **Activity Scheduling** (5) built from the monitoring data; **TRAP/TRAC** (9) and graded **anti-avoidance** homework (11). Then increase complexity, use **Behavioral Experiments** (7) to test behavior-mood links, **Problem-Solving** (8) for real barriers, and teach the **ACTION** acronym (16).
- **Later (values, maintenance, relapse prevention):** **Values** work (3) linking scheduling to valued directions; mop up remaining avoidance; **Social Skills** (14) if indicated; build the **Relapse Prevention Plan** (19) and start spacing sessions out.

In a single self-help session you'll usually touch just one or two of these — meet the user where the course actually is (check `CLAUDE.md` for where you left off).

## Severity adaptations

Match the entry point to severity — pushing a full scheduling program onto someone who can't yet move backfires.

| Severity | First adaptation | Lead techniques |
|---|---|---|
| Mild–moderate | Standard arc | Monitoring (1) → Scheduling (5) → TRAP/TRAC (9) → Values (3) |
| Moderate–severe | **Monitoring only** for a week or two, then add micro-step GTA | (1) → (6) → (5) |
| Severe / minimal initiative | Do the first scheduled activity **in-session**; lean on contingency management | (6) + (15) — and read `safety.md`, this is clinician-territory |
| Anergic / hypersomnic | **Wake-time consistency first**; GTA with the smallest possible steps | (10) → (6) |

## Handling low motivation and amotivation

Low motivation is a **core symptom** of depression, not a personal failing and not a reason to wait. BA's whole answer is the outside-in principle: don't wait for motivation, act and let it follow. The concrete tools:

- **Micro-step Graded Task Assignment** (6): decompose until each step is incontestably doable. "Sit on the edge of the bed for 2 minutes" is a legitimate start.
- **Activity menus by energy level:** build, *with* the user, a short list sorted by energy — very-low-energy days (make tea, sit outside 5 minutes) vs moderate (text a friend, cook one meal). Store it in `CLAUDE.md` (`logging.md`).
- **Environmental prompts:** put the cue in the way — shoes by the door, the hobby materials out on the table — to cut the activation energy to start.
- **Dabbling** (`anhedonia.md`) when even familiar activities feel inaccessible — brief novel activities with no performance expectation.
- **Social Support Contracting** (15): a support person reinforcing activation can stand in for internal motivation early on.
- **ACTION** (16): distinguishes deliberate rest from avoidance, and chooses by values rather than current mood.

## The honest nuance to say out loud

BA does **not** promise that every activation will feel rewarding. The mechanism is restoring contact with a reinforcement environment **over time** — not immediate pleasure. Early activations may feel effortful, neutral, or even mildly low before the mood-behavior loop turns. **Predict this explicitly**, because it's exactly where people quit, reading one flat attempt as proof it doesn't work. Naming it in advance is part of making BA stick.
